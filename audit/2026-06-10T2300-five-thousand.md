## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: gold > $5,000 is not a gold story but
a money story — a no-confidence vote in fiat cast by the central banks themselves (now holding more gold than
US Treasuries for the first time since 1996), triggered by the 2022 weaponization of Russia's reserves. The
two-edged kicker: the signal is true, but the price sits at the first real-terms record since 1980, a peak
followed by ~85% real drawdown over 20 years. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `ca7b5b4` → published; title "Five Thousand" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Gold broke **$5,000** first time **Jan 26, 2026** (~$5,110–5,115 intraday); ATH ~**$5,589** **Jan 28, 2026**;
  still >$5,000 mid-March 2026. **First real (inflation-adjusted) record since 1980** — genuine new discovery.
- Central-bank buying **>1,000 tonnes/yr in 2022, 2023, 2024** (vs pre-2022 ~400–500t norm); **244t in Q1
  2026** (WGC, strong continuation); **43%** of CBs plan to add in 2026 (up from 29% two years prior).
- **Gold > US Treasuries** as a share of CB reserves — **first time since 1996** (Morgan Stanley).
- Catalyst: 2022 Western freeze of ~**$300B** of Russia's FX reserves → dollar reserves weaponizable; gold
  is no one's liability.
- Forecasts: JPM/BofA see **$6,000** by end-2026 / spring 2026.
- **1980 precedent:** peak ~**$850** (Jan 21, 1980); buy-at-peak → **−68.9% nominal** by 2000, ~**−85%
  purchasing power**; bottom ~**$264** (2000); real record stood **46 years**.

Corrections made vs first-pass research:
- Dropped an unreliable "Q1 2026 buying collapsed to 16 tonnes" figure; WGC shows **244t** (strong). Used 244t.
- 2025 full-year CB tonnage conflicts across sources (~863t WGC vs 1,100–1,237t elsewhere); article avoids the
  contested single number and frames buying via the robust 2022–2024 ">1,000t/yr" record instead.
- Fixed typo "a bleak in the hull" → "a breach in the hull."
