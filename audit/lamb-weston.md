# Audit — Lamb Weston (LW): "Lamb Weston sells 7% more fries for 63% less profit as the glut it built comes due"

## Method

Forensic thesis: Lamb Weston's fiscal Q3 2026 headline (net sales +3% to $1.56B, volume up, guidance midpoint raised) masks a deteriorating business. The company is defending share in an oversupplied frozen-potato market by surrendering price — volume +7% fully offset by price/mix -7% at constant currency, leaving constant-currency sales flat — while GAAP profit collapsed 63%. The forensic moves: (1) denominator illusion — volume narrative hides falling revenue-per-unit; (2) cyclical-priced-as-secular — a capacity glut and soft restaurant traffic, not a durable share-gain engine; (3) a physical $32.5M raw-potato write-off as the glut made tangible; (4) adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings, where the decline widens the further down the income statement you read; (5) governance as verdict — activist Jana Partners (with Continental Grain) won board control and the chairmanship; (6) the cost-cut/capex-retreat reframe; (7) the long-dated GLP-1 demand drift, correctly sized as small near-term; (8) two-sided customer concentration. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes genuine volume wins, real cost program, cheap valuation, high dividend, and the small near-term GLP-1 impact.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Q3 FY2026, quarter ended Feb 22, 2026; reported April 1, 2026; SEC Form 8-K / businesswire / Baking Business / gurufocus):
- Net sales: $1,564.8 million, up ~3% YoY (increase of $44.3M; FX favorable $47.4M; constant-currency essentially flat). CONFIRMED.
- Volume +7% offset by price/mix -7% at constant currency. CONFIRMED (authoritative 8-K language). NOTE: some secondary coverage and call commentary cited "+12% volume" using a different framing; the article uses the 8-K constant-currency figures (7%/7%). Correction applied during fact-check — earlier draft used 12%.
- GAAP net income: $54.0M, down 63% from $146.0M. CONFIRMED.
- Diluted EPS: $0.39 vs $1.03 prior year (EPS missed ~$0.58 consensus). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $271.7M, down 27% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted income from operations down 38%. CONFIRMED.
- $32.5M pre-tax charge for write-off of excess raw potatoes in International segment (lower-than-expected EMEA volumes). CONFIRMED.
- North America net sales $1,035.0M (+5%); International $529.8M (-1%). CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 outlook updated: net sales $6.45–$6.55B (prior $6.35–$6.55B); adjusted EBITDA $1.08–$1.14B; capex cut to ~$400M from ~$500M. CONFIRMED.
- Cost-savings program: target at least $250M by end of FY2028; now expected to be exceeded. CONFIRMED.
- Activist: Jana Partners (with Continental Grain) won board battle July 2025; board expanded to 13 from 11; four Jana nominees joined; former Nestlé USA CEO Bradley Alford is chairman. CONFIRMED. (Article attributes activism to Jana, not Starboard; some 2026 coverage also notes broader activist interest.)
- Dividend: $0.38/share quarterly declared (payable June 5, 2026); yield ~3.6%. CONFIRMED.
- Stock near six-year lows; 52-week low $37.62; ~22% one-year total-return decline. CONFIRMED (as of mid-2026).
- GLP-1: ING estimate ~0.25% reduction in total food demand in 2026; surveys show GLP-1 users spend less at fast food and on grocery impulse buys. CONFIRMED as directional, near-term-small.

All load-bearing figures are adjudicated company-reported facts (8-K / press release), not allegations. The activist's "added too much capacity / mis-accounted for demand" characterization is an activist allegation, labeled as such in the text.
