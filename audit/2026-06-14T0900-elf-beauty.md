# Audit — e.l.f. Beauty (2026-06-14T0900-elf-beauty.md)

Title: "e.l.f. Beauty's Sales Jumped 35%, but Strip Out the Acquisition and Growth Was 1%." Batch 11, article 2 of
20 (target 2,900+; actual ~2,901). Source: consumer. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-14T0900-elf-beauty/`.

## Method
Forensic thesis: ELF net sales +35% to $449.3M in Q4 FY2026, but ~34pp came from the Rhode (Hailey Bieber)
acquisition ($113M); organic (ex-Rhode) grew only ~1% — a near-stall vs its multi-year hypergrowth past. Frames:
bought-growth-masks-organic-stall (M&A carries the headline while the core sputters); low-cost model vs the tariff
wall (~55% avg China tariff in FY26 threatens the price leadership that IS the brand); below-guide FY27;
law-of-large-numbers maturation; celebrity-brand concentration + integration risk; growth-multiple re-rating
asymmetry. Honest-nuance: genuinely disruptive proven model, beloved brand, real international runway, Rhode
outperformed its earnout ($57.6M fair-value adj), gross margin rose +140bps to 73%, the 1% may be partly a brutal
comp + US mass-beauty softness rather than a permanent stall.

## Fact-check (research + verification → all confirmed)
CONFIRMED (ELF Q4 FY2026 8-K / 10-K / CNBC / Globe and Mail transcript, reported May 20 2026; FY ended Mar 31 2026):
- **Net sales +35% to $449.3M** ✓; **Rhode contributed $113M / ~34pp** ✓ → **organic ~1%** ✓
- **Avg tariff rate ~55% in FY26** (>2x prior); FY27 guidance assumes 35%; expecting ~$55M tariff refund ✓
- **Gross margin +140bps to ~73%** (pricing offset tariffs) ✓
- $57.6M fair-value adjustment on Rhode earnout (outperformance) ✓
- FY2027 guidance: sales $1.84–1.87B; adj EPS $3.27–3.32 — **below analyst expectations** (CNBC/research search) ✓

### Editorial care
- Organic ~1% is the honest skeptic figure (company-disclosed: 35% total − ~34pp Rhode). Conceded repeatedly that
  the model/brand/runway are excellent and that 1% may be a hard comp, not necessarily permanent. Tariff threat
  framed structurally (low-cost model vs China exposure), with the $55M refund noted as a one-time cushion. Rhode
  framed as a genuinely good asset AND a different risk profile (celebrity/integration) — both. No exact P/E
  asserted (premium qualitative). No price target; no buy/sell. Not Apple-related.

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
