# Instacart's first $1B quarter ran on ads, not delivery — and the engine is cooling

## Method

Forensic thesis: Instacart (Maplebear Inc., CART) is structurally an advertising company wearing a grocery-delivery costume. Its core delivery business clears a flat ~7.1% transaction take rate on the goods it moves — thin, contested, and not rising despite efficiency gains. The GAAP profit that excites the market is carried overwhelmingly by a high-margin advertising business ("advertising and other" revenue) selling shelf placement back to the CPG brands whose products it delivers. The bear frames deployed: (1) ads-mask-thin-delivery-economics; (2) flat take rate = price-taker, not moat; (3) management's own Q2 guidance shows deceleration across every metric that justifies the multiple (GTV, ads, EBITDA, margin expansion); (4) denominator illusion — GTV growth (13%) outpacing order growth (10%) on basket size + grocery price, not trips; (5) commodity-price-taker exposure to grocery inflation; (6) moat-vs-loophole — same captive-shopper ad inventory is contested by DoorDash, Uber, Amazon; (7) adjusted-vs-GAAP wedge ($300M EBITDA vs $144M net income) and buyback-as-dilution-mop; (8) priced-for-perfection asymmetry — multiple, not just earnings, must reset. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes real GAAP profit, real FCF, structurally superior ad asset, capital-return-from-strength, and genuine low online-grocery penetration.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Q1 2026, reported May 6, 2026; press release / 8-K; corroborated by Motley Fool transcript, Investing.com, Progressive Grocer, StockTitan):
- GTV: $10.29 billion (stated $10,288M), up 13% YoY — CONFIRMED
- Total revenue: $1.02 billion ($1,019M), up 14%; first time over $1B — CONFIRMED
- Transaction revenue: $733 million, up 13%, "maintained" 7.1% of GTV — CONFIRMED
- Advertising and other revenue: $286 million, up 16%, fastest since Q3 2023, 2.8% of GTV — CONFIRMED
- GAAP net income: $144 million, up 36% YoY (1.4% of GTV, 14% of revenue) — CONFIRMED
- Adjusted EBITDA: $300 million, up 23% YoY — CONFIRMED
- Orders: 91.2 million, up 10% YoY — CONFIRMED
- AOV: $113, up 3% YoY — CONFIRMED
- Operating cash flow $268M; free cash flow $253M — CONFIRMED
- Buyback: authorization raised by $1B to $3.5B total — CONFIRMED (StockTitan "$3.5B buyback"; release notes $1B increase)
- Q2 2026 guidance: GTV $10.1–$10.25B (+11–13%); advertising +11–14%; adj. EBITDA $290–$300M (+11–15%); GTV to outpace orders — CONFIRMED
- Stock fell ~8% post-print — CONFIRMED (TIKR: "Instacart Stock Falls 8% After Q1 Earnings")

Corrections made: none required; all load-bearing figures verified against primary (SEC 8-K / IR) and secondary sources. The ~$156M adjusted-vs-GAAP wedge is arithmetic from confirmed figures ($300M EBITDA − $144M net income). Take-rate percentages (7.1%, 2.8%) are company-stated. Thesis premise (ads are the profit engine; growth decelerating) held true on the latest quarter.
