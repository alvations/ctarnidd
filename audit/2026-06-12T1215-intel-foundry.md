# Audit — Intel foundry (2026-06-12T1215-intel-foundry.md)

Title: "Washington Bet Billions on Intel's Comeback. Its Customers Haven't." Method + fact-check; kept out of the
article. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T1215-intel-foundry/`. Batch 7, article 9 of 10 (target

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: INTC tripled on extraordinary STRATEGIC
validation (US govt ~10% stake ~$36B; Nvidia $5B; MSFT/AMZN/AAPL foundry agreements), but the COMMERCIAL
validation is near-zero — Intel Foundry external revenue ~$174M/qtr (largely Altera, a deconsolidated former
subsidiary), operating loss ~$2.4B (~−50% margin), and the 10-Q concedes Intel has been "unsuccessful to date"
at attracting significant external customers. The distinction: strategic backers each have a non-commercial
motive (national security / supply hedge / signaling) — Nvidia's $5B explicitly excluded foundry — which is not
the same as winning chips on merit. Yield gap (18A ~55-60% vs TSMC N2 ~65-70%) and the structural difficulty of
the foundry business (trust + Intel competes with its would-be customers; AMD escaped the integrated model)
compound it. Honest-nuance: CHIPS/govt de-risk the existential capex threat, yields improving 7-8%/mo, DCAI +22%,
$15B+ lifetime commitments, real strategic logic.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → enhanced → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Intel 10-Q/SEC / TechPowerUp / Manufacturing Dive / CNBC / eeNews / Tom's Hardware / Bloomberg):
- Intel Foundry Q1 2026 operating loss **$2.4B**; **external revenue $174M** (primarily **Altera** post-Q3-2025
  deconsolidation); 10-Q: "**unsuccessful to date**" on external customers; op margin ~−50%.
- US govt **10% / $8.9B** stake (biggest shareholder; +5% warrant if foundry <51%); value ~**$36B** post-rally.
- **Nvidia $5B** (214.7M sh @ $23.28, ~4%) — deal **excludes foundry** making chips for Nvidia.
- DCAI +22%, foundry rev +16%; 18A yields ~55-60% vs **TSMC N2 ~65-70%** (yield gap holds; 18A perf/transistor
  slightly ahead — noted for fairness); $15B+ lifetime commitments; MSFT/AMZN/Apple agreements (Apple
  preliminary, WSJ); CHIPS $19.5B.

### Enhancements (strengthen, not corrections)
1. Added the **Altera** nuance to the $174M external figure (genuine third-party revenue is even smaller).
2. Used the exact 10-Q **"unsuccessful to date"** wording (stronger than the original paraphrase).
3. Added that **Nvidia's $5B explicitly excluded foundry** manufacturing.

### Editorial care
- Apple deal labeled "reported/preliminary" (WSJ). The "Intel may de-emphasize 18A for 14A" point labeled as
  reports. Bull case (govt de-risking, yield progress, DCAI growth, commitments, strategic logic, 18A perf
  parity) given a full honest-nuance section. Thesis is "commercial conversion unproven / priced as certain,"
  not "Intel fails."

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
