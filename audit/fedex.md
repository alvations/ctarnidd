# Audit — FedEx's $5.25 adjusted EPS is a cost-cut mirage over a stalling freight economy

Article: `2026-06-20T1030-fedex.md` · Ticker: FDX · Sector: transportation

## Method

Forensic thesis: FedEx's adjusted-EPS growth is a cost-cut (DRIVE / Network 2.0) mirage layered over structurally soft, demand-driven B2B/freight volume. The bull narrative treats a freight-recession cyclical as a secular self-help compounder. Frames deployed: (1) adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings — the $5.25 adjusted vs $4.41 GAAP wedge, and the >$3/share gap between FY26 adjusted ($19.30–$20.10) and GAAP ($16.05–$16.85) guidance; (2) bought/priced-growth-masks-organic-stall — blended 8.3% revenue growth concealing a -4.7% freight-revenue decline and -4.8% tonnage; (3) denominator illusion — cost cutting is a finite subtraction, and the spin-off mechanically cleans the remaining "stub"; (4) customer/partner concentration — lost ~$2B/yr USPS air contract (expired Sept 29, 2024) and Amazon's network build-out; (5) moat-vs-loophole — Amazon replicated the parcel network with retail subsidy; (6) financial engineering — June 1, 2026 FedEx Freight (FDXF) spin-off with $4.1B debt-funded cash to parent; (7) priced-for-perfection asymmetry; (8) operating-leverage-in-reverse risk vs record-low capex. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes real DRIVE execution, genuine yield/share gains, defensible spin value, and the bottom-of-cycle bull case.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (FedEx IR release + CNBC + multiple wires, Q3 FY2026 reported March 19, 2026, quarter ended Feb 28, 2026):
- Revenue $24.0B, +8.3% YoY (prior-year Q3 FY25 ~$22.2B). CONFIRMED.
- GAAP diluted EPS $4.41 (up from $3.76 prior year); adjusted diluted EPS $5.25 (up from $4.51). CONFIRMED.
- Consensus adjusted EPS near $4.11; revenue beat (~$23.4B est). CONFIRMED.
- GAAP operating income $1.348B; operating margin GAAP 5.6% / adjusted 6.7%. CONFIRMED.
- FedEx Freight Q3: revenue ~$1.99B, -4.7% YoY (rounded "5%"); tonnage -4.8%; shipments -5.7%; adjusted op income -$127M; FY26 unit adjusted op income now guided down ~$400M (vs prior -$300M). CONFIRMED.
- FY26 guidance: revenue +6.0–6.5%; adjusted EPS $19.30–$20.10; GAAP EPS $16.05–$16.85; capex ≤$4.1B (~4.6% of revenue, record low). CONFIRMED.
- DRIVE: $4.0B structural cost reductions vs FY23 baseline; FY25 $2.2B target hit; FY26 transformation target "more than $1 billion." CONFIRMED.
- USPS domestic air contract (~$2B/yr peak) expired Sept 29, 2024; UPS won replacement. CONFIRMED.
- Amazon ~6.1B packages in 2024 (up from 1.7B in 2019), now largest U.S. parcel carrier; Amazon Shipping open to third parties. CONFIRMED.
- FedEx Freight (FDXF) spin completed June 1, 2026; ratio 1 FDXF per 2 FDX, record date May 15; FedEx distributed 80.1% / retained 19.9%; ~$4.1B debt-funded cash paid to parent pre-separation; FDXF priced ~$152 (~$22.6B equity / ~$26.7B EV; ~3.1x FY26 revenue, ~24x adj op income); FY26 FDXF ~$8.7B revenue, ~$1.1B adj op income; retained stake ~$4.5B. CONFIRMED (spinoff trackers + FedEx newsroom).

CORRECTIONS MADE:
- Initial dek stated "28 percent adjusted-EPS jump"; corrected to "roughly 16 percent ... to $5.25 from $4.51" ($4.51→$5.25 = +16.4%), since prior-year Q3 FY25 adjusted EPS was $4.51.
