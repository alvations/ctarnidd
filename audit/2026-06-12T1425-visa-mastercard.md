## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: V/MA are a near-perfect business
(>$20T/yr volume, 50-67% op margins, capital-light, 60-yr two-sided moat). Every prior "disruptor" rode ON the
rails; stablecoins (+ the 2025 GENIUS Act) are the first credible BYPASS that goes AROUND them, letting merchants
— who've fought interchange for decades — settle peer-to-peer with no interchange. The TELL: Mastercard's up-to-
$1.8B BVNK acquisition defends by owning the bypass, conceding the threat and migrating volume to lower margins.
Real-time rails (UPI/Pix/FedNow/SEPA) already proved the moat can be routed around. The threat is EROSION
(cross-border/B2B/large-merchant — the highest-margin flows) not extinction. Honest-nuance section: consumer-side
stickiness (rewards/fraud/credit), replication difficulty, adaptation from strength, multiple already compressed.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Mastercard 8-K / Fortune / American Banker / Banking Dive / fxcintel / MLQ):

- **GENIUS Act** (mid-2025) framework; merchants/Walmart/Amazon exploring; V/MA −19-23% on stablecoin fears;
  real-time rails UPI/Pix/FedNow/SEPA.

### Editorial care
- Thesis explicitly EROSION not extinction; the disruption is "real and different in kind" but the moat
  "genuinely formidable." Full honest-nuance section. Walmart/Amazon stablecoin claims hedged ("exploring," "in
  principle," "could"). Durbin/interchange-war history stated as documented context. The BVNK acquisition framed
  as smart defense that nonetheless concedes the threat. No claim the duopoly is "about to fall" (explicitly the
  opposite).
