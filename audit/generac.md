# Audit — Generac (GNRC): home-resilience engine flat; data-center bet carries the rally

## Method

Forensic thesis: Generac is valued on a secular "energy resilience / clean energy" framing, but its most recent quarter (Q1 2026, reported April 29, 2026) shows the franchise that anchors that framing — Residential, and specifically home standby — running flat. Home-standby volumes declined year over year with price the only offset; energy storage shrank. The entire growth and guidance raise came from the Commercial & Industrial (C&I) segment, driven by data-center backup power. The forensic moves: (1) cyclical-priced-as-secular — twice over, with a reverting weather cycle and a new hyperscale-capex cycle stacked; (2) denominator illusion — "Residential grew 1%" masks falling units; (3) hurricane-comp dependency — management cited a strong 2024 hurricane season as the reason volumes fell; (4) demonstration-vs-deployment — $700M data-center backlog with vendor approvals "in final stages," not signed take-or-pay at scale; (5) moat-vs-loophole — home-standby moat does not travel to a data-center market owned by Caterpillar/Cummins/Eaton/Vertiv, at roughly half the margin; (6) policy/subsidy dependence — energy storage decline tied to net-metering / solar economics. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the real, large data-center tailwind, high-quality margin expansion, tripling FCF, and pricing power.

## Fact-check

- Residential external net sales: ~$549M, +~1% vs ~$543M. CONFIRMED.
- Home standby: "approximately flat as higher pricing... offset by lower volumes." CONFIRMED (verbatim disclosure).
- Energy storage sales declined. CONFIRMED.
- Winter storm Fern cited as better-than-expected home-standby contribution. CONFIRMED.
- Prior-year strength attributed in part to "substantial 2024 hurricane season." CONFIRMED.
- C&I external net sales: ~$510M, +~28% vs $399M. CONFIRMED.
- Residential adjusted EBITDA margin: 25.1% ($138.6M) vs 20.3% ($111.6M). CONFIRMED.
- C&I adjusted EBITDA margin: 13.0% vs 11.4%. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP net income: $73M, $1.24/sh (vs $44M, $0.73 prior year). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted net income: $106M, $1.80/sh (vs $75M, $1.26). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA total: ~$193.5M, 18.3% margin. CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin: 38.7%. CONFIRMED (StockTitan).
- Free cash flow: ~$90M ($89.9M) vs $27.2M prior year; cash from operations $119M. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EPS $1.80 beat consensus ~$1.33. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance raised to mid-to-high-teens net sales growth, "driven entirely by" data center/telecom/rental within C&I; adj. EBITDA margin 18.5–19.5%; C&I growth mid-to-high 20s. CONFIRMED.
- Data-center backlog "over $700M"; "final stages of vendor approval with multiple hyperscale customers." CONFIRMED.
- Stock ~+8.6% pre-market on print; ~+90% trailing year vs industry ~25%. CONFIRMED (StockTitan/Zacks via Yahoo).

Corrections applied: none required; figures softened where appropriate ("about $549M," "roughly 28%"). The 2022-23 channel-destock and the stock's ~$500-to-sub-$100 drawdown are stated as historical context (general market record), framed as backdrop, not as a Q1 2026 figure.
