## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Arm is a near-perfect royalty business
but trades at one of the market's most extreme valuations (fwd P/E ~100-190x, ~38x revenue) on ~20% royalty
growth. The certain part (smartphones) is mature/saturating; the explosive part (data center/AI) the multiple
prices is contested/unproven. Strategic risk: Arm abandoned NEUTRALITY by launching its own AGI CPU → competes
with its own licensees ("Switzerland building tanks"). It sued Qualcomm/Nuvia and LOST decisively. RISC-V = the
royalty-free bypass being paved alongside the toll road. SoftBank ~90% control = thin float / volatility. Honest-
nuance: elite model, real AI inflection (DC royalty doubled), rising Armv9/CSS rates, $100B+ DC TAM — deserves a
premium, just maybe not 100-190x.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Arm 6-K/SEC / StockTitan / GuruFocus / TechCrunch / Tom's Hardware / Qualcomm IR):
- FY2026 revenue **$4.92B**; royalty **$2.61B (+21%)**; licensing **$2.31B (+25%)**; data-center royalty MORE
  THAN DOUBLED. Fwd P/E **~100-190x**. **SoftBank ~90%**.
- **Arm AGI CPU**: first in-house chip in ~36 yrs; 136-core DC CPU; **Meta lead partner**; ByteDance/Oracle
  customers — confirms "competes with its own customers."
- Armv9/CSS rising royalty rates (~5%→10%+ ASP); $100B DC TAM by 2030; RISC-V threat; smartphone maturity.

### Correction (stale framing — important)
- Draft framed the Qualcomm/Nuvia case as "locked in litigation... trial scheduled for late 2026." Verification:
  **Qualcomm won a DECISIVE victory** (Delaware judge ruled no breach of Arm's ALA, dismissed Arm's remaining
  claim, denied Arm a new trial; further matters lingering into 2026). REWROTE to: Arm sued a major customer and
  **lost** — accurate, and it strengthens the relationship-souring / lost-leverage thesis rather than weakening
  it.

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes Arm is one of the best businesses ever built; thesis is price + strategy, not quality.
  RISC-V framed as a long-term threat that "remains years behind" for demanding apps (honest-nuance). Multiple
  hedged ("well over a hundred... approaching or exceeding 180"). SoftBank overhang framed as structural, not a
  prediction. No claim Arm fails — explicitly "will likely remain one of the most important companies in tech."

No remaining errors.
