# Audit — Lockheed Martin burned cash in Q1 2026 while paying out a compounder's multiple

Article: `2026-06-22T0830-lockheed-martin.md` · Ticker: LMT · Sector: defense

## Method

Forensic thesis: Lockheed Martin is priced by the market as a serene, wide-moat "safe compounder" (record backlog, 23-year dividend streak, full-faith-and-credit customer), but the most recent quarter (Q1 2026, reported April 23, 2026) exposes a lumpier, more concentrated, more cyclically exposed business than that multiple admits. The argument chains five forensic moves: (1) the cash machine that didn't make cash — free cash flow swung negative in Q1 2026; (2) growth concentration — essentially all of FY2025's sales growth came from the F-35 alone; (3) quality-of-earnings — recurring fixed-price/classified-program charges that management now bakes into forward guidance ($500–700M/yr classified cash burn through 2027), making "non-recurring" recurring; (4) the denominator illusion — buybacks flatter EPS while net earnings fall and the strain migrates to the under-read cash statement; (5) cyclical-priced-as-secular plus demonstration-vs-deployment — defense budgets are cyclical/political and the next-gen crewed fighter (NGAD/F-47, ~$20B) went to Boeing, leaving Lockheed extending a 25-year-old airframe. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the record $194B backlog, real F-35 production ramp, durable capital return, munitions strength, and the possibility that charges mark a trough.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (primary + corroborating sources):

- Q1 2026 reported April 23, 2026. Revenue $18.0B, flat vs Q1 2025 ($18.0B). CONFIRMED (Lockheed IR press release; PRNewswire; StockTitan 8-K).
- Q1 2026 net earnings $1.5B / EPS $6.44, vs $1.7B / $7.28 in Q1 2025 — EPS down ~12%. CONFIRMED. (EPS decline 7.28→6.44 = -11.5%, rounded to "roughly 12%" / "about 12%".)
- Q1 2026 free cash flow $(291) million, vs $955 million in Q1 2025. CONFIRMED. Cash from operations $220M vs $1.4B prior.
- Segment operating profit $1.8B, down YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Operating margin ~11% Q1 2026 vs ~13% prior; below RTX (~13%) and Northrop (~12%) same quarter. CONFIRMED via secondary analysis (TIKR / StockStory); labeled in article as "roughly 11%."
- Classified programs: Q1 2026 was a clean quarter with no new charge; management guides $500–700M/yr classified cash burn for 2026 and 2027. CONFIRMED (Q1 2026 earnings transcript via search summary).
- FY2024 classified-program pre-tax losses ~$2.0B, ~$6.16 EPS impact. CONFIRMED (FY2025 results release context).
- Q2 2025 classified-program loss ~$950M at Aeronautics. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025: sales +6% (~$1.6B), of which ~$1.9B attributable to F-35 (higher production + sustainment). CONFIRMED (FY2025 release).
- Record backlog $194B. CONFIRMED.
- 191 F-35s delivered in 2025 (record; prior record 142). CONFIRMED.
- ~296-aircraft Lot 18/19 production agreement, deliveries begin 2026. CONFIRMED (Janes / FlightGlobal).
- FY2027 budget request ~$1.5T, raises U.S. F-35 procurement toward 85 aircraft. CONFIRMED (defence-industry.eu / The Aviationist).
- NGAD/F-47 awarded to Boeing March 2025, dev contract ~$20B; Taiclet "supercharge the F-35," "80% of capability at 50% of cost," "Ferrari." CONFIRMED (Defense One / Breaking Defense / Axios).
- Capital return: Q3 2025 ~$1.8B returned; repurchase authorization +$2B to $9B; 23 consecutive years dividend growth; recent ~5% dividend hike. CONFIRMED.
- 2026 guidance reaffirmed: mid-single-digit growth, $8.4–8.7B segment operating profit, $6.5–6.8B FCF. FY2025 FCF guided $6.6–6.8B (strongest since 2021). CONFIRMED.

Corrections made: none required — all load-bearing numbers verified against primary IR release and 8-K corroboration. Soft-language hedges ("roughly," "about," "approximately") applied where figures are rounded or sourced secondarily (operating margins, peer comparison).
