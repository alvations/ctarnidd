# MongoDB reaccelerates to 25% growth, but its $4.4M GAAP profit rests on a $493M stock subsidy

## Method

Forensic thesis (quality-of-earnings + cyclical-priced-as-secular). The suggested angle — "decelerated from hypergrowth, growth depends on variable Atlas consumption, heavy SBC dilutes adjusted profitability, premium multiple" — was partly stale: MongoDB actually REACCELERATED to 25% revenue growth in Q1 FY27 (reported May 28, 2026) and posted its first-ever GAAP net income. The thesis was pivoted to the accurate forensic frame:

1. The celebrated $4.4M GAAP net income masks a GAAP loss from operations of $24.8M — the net profit comes from non-operating (interest) income, not the database business.
2. The $112.3M non-GAAP net income is built by adding back stock-based compensation (~$493.9M FY26), a ~$148M GAAP-to-non-GAAP swing on operating income in the quarter.
3. Atlas (~75% of revenue) is consumption-priced and macro-sensitive — a single accelerating quarter is the wrong unit of analysis.
4. At ~$26.2B market cap / ~$2.6B TTM revenue (~10x P/S), the market pays a secular/subscription multiple for cyclical/consumption revenue. Guidance of 19-20% FY27 growth after a 25% quarter is management's own tell.

Honest-nuance section concedes: the reacceleration is genuine (not an accounting trick), product/NRR strength is real, the loss trajectory is improving fast, and bears have been wrong before.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 FY27, reported 2026-05-28; primary: MongoDB IR / PRNewswire 8-K release; corroborated StockTitan, Motley Fool transcript, Investing.com):
- Total revenue $687.6M, +25% YoY — CONFIRMED
- Subscription revenue $666.1M (+25%); services $21.5M (+22%) — CONFIRMED
- Atlas revenue $512.5M, +29.4% YoY, ~75% of total (up from ~72%); +$117M dollar growth — CONFIRMED
- EA/other revenue ~$153.7M — CONFIRMED
- GAAP loss from operations -$24.8M (vs -$53.6M prior year) — CONFIRMED
- GAAP net income $4.4M / $0.05 per share (first positive GAAP bottom line; vs prior-year loss) — CONFIRMED
- Non-GAAP operating income $123.2M (vs $87.4M) — CONFIRMED
- Non-GAAP net income $112.3M / $1.32 per share — CONFIRMED
- 81.6M diluted weighted-average shares (Q1 FY27) — CONFIRMED
- FY26 stock-based compensation ~$493.9M — CONFIRMED (AlphaQuery / FY26 reporting)
- Customers >67,700 (vs ~57,100); $100K+ ARR customers 2,895 (vs 2,506) — CONFIRMED
- Net ARR expansion rate ~119% — CONFIRMED
- FY27 guidance $2.92B-$2.96B (~19-20% growth); Q2 guide $729M-$734M — CONFIRMED
- Price ~$329.50, market cap ~$26.2B as of 2026-06-18; ~$2.6B TTM revenue, ~10x P/S — CONFIRMED (stockanalysis.com / Investing.com)

Corrections made vs. original angle: the premise of "decelerated from hypergrowth" was softened/reframed — the quarter ACCELERATED to 25% from ~22%; article states this explicitly and builds the thesis on composition-of-profit and consumption cyclicality rather than on a false deceleration claim. Forward P/E (~80x) labeled as an approximation (annualized single-quarter non-GAAP EPS), not asserted as a precise figure.
