# Audit — Pool Corp's $5.3B revenue is flat for a third year while new-pool builds sit 40% below their peak

Article: `2026-06-18T1400-pool-corp.md` · Ticker: POOL · Sector: distribution

## Method

Forensic thesis: **cyclical priced as secular / floor-is-also-the-ceiling.** Pool Corporation's maintenance business (chemicals, replacement parts, consumables across an ~11M-pool installed base) is genuinely durable and grew again in Q1 2026. But the company's pandemic-era growth and premium multiple were built on new-pool construction, which has now declined for four consecutive years — roughly 58,000-60,000 U.S. in-ground builds in 2025 versus ~98,000 at the 2022 peak (~40% below peak). Net sales have been pinned near $5.3B since 2023; 2025 EPS fell ~4% and operating income fell ~6%. 2026 guidance ($10.87-$11.17) implies only flat-to-low-single-digit EPS growth. At ~18x forward (vs. ~27x five-year average), the stock still embeds a construction recovery that management cannot point to with conviction. The asymmetry: the maintenance floor caps the downside; the absent construction recovery caps the upside — dead-money-at-a-premium risk, not collapse risk.

The premise as suggested ("new-build demand down") is directionally correct on construction units, but the article was PIVOTED to be accurate: total revenue is NOT declining (Q1 2026 net sales rose 6%; FY2025 flat). The accurate frame is the multi-year *plateau* plus the structurally reset construction line, not an outright revenue decline.
