# Audit — Toast Books $126 Million Profit on $1.63 Billion, but 80% Rides a 51-Basis-Point Take Rate

## Method

The suggested angle assumed Toast's GAAP profitability was still in doubt. Verification against the Q1 2026 (quarter ended March 31, 2026; reported May 7, 2026) results showed that premise is now FALSE — Toast posted clean GAAP net income of $126M. The thesis was therefore PIVOTED from "can it ever be profitable" to a quality-of-revenue / take-rate forensic case:

1. **Denominator illusion** — ARR ($2.2B) is the headline, but ~80% of total revenue ($1.63B) is payment processing, not subscription software.
2. **Take rate is the whole ballgame** — the payments take rate is ~51 bps (up 2 bps YoY), a thin spread Toast earns downstream of Visa/Mastercard interchange. Two basis points moves the largest revenue stream.
3. **Hardware subsidy** — hardware + professional services ran at NEGATIVE gross margin (~ -13% of recurring gross profit streams), a deliberate loss-leader to plant payment rails; exposed to tariff costs on imported devices.
4. **Cyclical priced as secular** — four-fifths of revenue tracks discretionary restaurant dining, one of the most recession-sensitive consumer categories, yet is valued near a SaaS multiple.
5. **Competition compresses the spread** — Square (Block), SpotOn, Shift4 are paid to undercut the exact 51 bps Toast lives on.
6. **Valuation asymmetry** — high-thirties trailing P/E and >30x EV/EBITDA price the optimistic answer to every open question.

Mandatory honest-nuance section ("What the bulls genuinely get right") concedes durable profitability, beloved product, real switching costs, working mix-shift toward higher-margin ancillary fintech (monetization >1.03%, SaaS gross margin >80%), and a lengthening international/enterprise runway.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against SEC Form 8-K (Exhibit 99.1, tost-20260331) and BusinessWire release (May 7, 2026):

- GAAP net income Q1 2026: **$126M** (vs $56M Q1 2025). CONFIRMED.
- Total revenue: **$1.63B**, +22% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP operating income: **$110M** (vs $43M); operating income margin **21%**. CONFIRMED.
- Diluted EPS **$0.20** (vs $0.09). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA **$179M**, 34% margin, +35% YoY (vs $133M). CONFIRMED.
- Free cash flow **$115M** (vs $69M). CONFIRMED.
- ARR **$2.2B**, +26% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Net new locations **~7,000**; total **~171,000**, +22% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- GPV **$51B** (one source $51.3B), +22% YoY; TTM GPV **$204B**. CONFIRMED (range used).
- Payments take rate **~51 bps**, +2 bps YoY; total monetization take rate **1.03%**. CONFIRMED.
- Hardware + professional services gross profit ~ **-13%** of recurring gross profit streams; drag attributed to customer acquisition + tariff costs. CONFIRMED.
- SaaS gross margin crossed **80%** (reported ~81%). CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance raised: recurring gross profit growth **~21%-23%**; adjusted EBITDA **$790M-$810M**. CONFIRMED.
- Market cap **~$14B** (June 2026 ~$14.29B); trailing P/E high-thirties (~37.9x-39x); EV/EBITDA **~31.75x**; forward P/E ~18.8x. CONFIRMED (described as "neighborhood"/ranges).
- Fintech ~80% of revenue: historical disclosure ~81%; described as "roughly four-fifths" / "historically." Softened, not asserted as a current precise figure.
- International markets: U.K., Ireland, Canada, Australia. CONFIRMED.
- Competitors: Block/Square, SpotOn, Shift4 (FOUR). CONFIRMED.

No invented figures. Contested/imprecise items (fintech revenue share, GPV exact, valuation multiples) softened to ranges or "roughly/neighborhood." Allegations vs facts: the entire bear thesis is framed as interpretation of disclosed facts, not as wrongdoing.
