# Audit — Upstart's revenue jumped 44% in Q1 while its GAAP loss widened and its AI model faces a fraud suit

Article: `2026-06-17T1000-upstart.md` · Ticker: UPST · Sector: fintech

## Method

Forensic thesis: Upstart is valued as a software company immune to the credit cycle on the strength of its "AI underwrites better than FICO" claim, but Q1 2026 (reported May 5, 2026) shows surging cyclical growth masking deteriorating profitability and a contested core claim. Frames used: cyclical-priced-as-secular (61% origination growth is a macro-window recovery, not a 61%-smarter model); adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings (revenue +44% yet GAAP loss widened, adjusted EBITDA fell in absolute dollars, SBC bridges loss to "profit"); denominator illusion (the $1.0B on-balance-sheet loan book framed as only 6.2% of $16.4B powered loans, but large against a sub-$3B market cap); moat-vs-loophole and demonstration-vs-deployment (funding-partner dependence is the most cyclical variable); priced-for-perfection asymmetry (50-60x+ P/E vs high-single-digit industry); allegation-labeled litigation risk (securities class action over Model 22).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported May 5, 2026; primary = company IR / 8-K, corroborated by StockTitan and Investing.com):
- Total revenue $308M, +44% YoY (beat ~$303M consensus). CONFIRMED.
- Fee revenue $277M, +49% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Originations ~$3.4B, +61% YoY; 425,356 loans, +77% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP net loss $6.6M (vs $2.4M loss Q1 2025); operating loss $7.5M (vs $4.5M); GAAP EPS ~-$0.07. CONFIRMED.
- Contribution profit $137M, +34%; contribution margin 50% (down from 55%). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA $40.5M, down from $42.6M; margin 13% (down from 20%). CONFIRMED.
- Stock-based compensation ~$29.8M (vs ~$34.8M Q1 2025). CONFIRMED.
- On-balance-sheet loans ~$1.0B = ~6.2% of ~$16.4B total Upstart-powered loans outstanding. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance reiterated: ~$1.4B revenue, ~$1.3B fee revenue, ~$294M adjusted EBITDA (~21% margin). CONFIRMED.
- Market cap ~$2.9B (June 2026); trailing P/E reported in low-50s to >60x by various providers vs high-single-digit industry; stock down ~44% YTD per some accounts. CONFIRMED as reported ranges; labeled as dispersed/approximate in article.
- Securities class action (Pomerantz, Levi & Korsinsky, Gross Law Firm) re "Model 22" AI underwriting, class period ~May 14–Nov 4, 2025; allegations that Model 22 overreacted to macro signals and accuracy/approval-lift was overstated. LABELED AS ALLEGATIONS, not adjudicated. CONFIRMED that suits were filed/publicized.
