# Audit — Carvana (2026-06-12T1010-carvana.md)

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Carvana's operational comeback from the
2022 near-bankruptcy is genuinely real, but the ~$87B valuation prices the risks that nearly killed it as solved
when they're only reduced — ~$5.1B junk-rated debt with a mid-2026 "interest cliff"; a profit engine leaning on
gain-on-sale of SUBPRIME auto loans into securitizations + Ally (a $4B commitment with an ~Oct 2026 renewal
cliff); record ~6.9% subprime delinquencies; persistent founding-family stock selling; and detailed bearish
allegations. All are fair-weather strengths that deteriorate together in a recession — the 2022 setup. Honest-
nuance section credits the genuine profitability, real turnaround, vast TAM, and proven execution.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (CNBC / StockTitan / InsiderFinance / MLQ / Yahoo):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$6.43B** (+52%); net income **$405M** (vs $373M y/y); retail units **187,393** (+40%); gross
  profit **$1.27B**; GPU **$6,783**; adj EBITDA **$672M**; cash $2.4B; total debt **$5.1B** (junk-rated); market
  cap **~$87B**.
- Insider pattern corroborated: **268 open-market insider trades in 6 months — 0 buys, 268 sales.**
- 2022 near-death (~$370 → <$4) = documented market history.

SOURCED (search-01; not reconfirmed in verify): ~6.9% subprime delinquencies; Ally $4B / ~Oct 2026 renewal
cliff; mid-2026 interest cliff; Garcia ~$3.6B (2020-21) + ~$1.4B sales; Hindenburg allegations.

### Editorial care
- Hindenburg claims (accounting manipulation, lax underwriting, ~$800M related-party loan sales) are explicitly
  **labeled as allegations**, disputed by the company, "not adjudicated findings"; the article cautions that
  short-sellers talk their book.
- Insider selling stated as **documented fact**, explicitly **not** as proof of wrongdoing ("insider selling is
  not, by itself, proof of anything").
- Ernest Garcia II's "complicated financial history" referenced only vaguely (his 1990 bank-fraud guilty plea is
  a matter of public record; the article does not assert specifics).

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
