## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Marvell is the #2 in the custom-AI-silicon
duopoly (Broadcom >70% / Marvell ~20% target) — a genuinely great, fast-growing business — yet trades at a HIGHER
forward P/E than the dominant leader Broadcom: a premium to own second place (the AMD-vs-Nvidia "priced for the
catch" error). Structural fragility: extreme customer concentration (a few hyperscaler programs; one delay craters
a quarter); the customers are also the competition (hyperscalers building in-house chip teams); TSMC chokepoint;
capex-cycle cyclicality. Nvidia's $2B is part of the circular AI-financing web, not pure endorsement. The steadier
optical franchise is the real hedge but isn't what's priced. Honest-nuance: huge TAM (+45%), strong #2 in a
booming duopoly, real design wins, real tech.

### Correction (stale/mislabeled)
- Draft cited "record data-center revenue of $816 million (+109%)." Verification: current Q1 FY27 data center =
  **~$1.83B (76% of total)**; **$816M was a year-ago/prior-basis figure**. Rewrote the absolute number to ~$1.83B
  / 76% / "more than doubled." The **+109% growth RATE retained** in analysis (data center more than doubled —
  accurate).

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes Marvell is a fine/excellent business; thesis = relative value + structure, not failure.
  Nvidia $2B framed as both endorsement AND circular-financing data point (balanced). Broadcom 70%/Marvell 20%

  risks, not predictions of imminent collapse.
