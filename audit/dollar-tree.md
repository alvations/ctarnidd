# Audit — Dollar Tree's 38% EPS jump rests on a tariff windfall and a price moat it already abandoned

Article: `2026-06-18T0900-dollar-tree.md` · Ticker: DLTR · Sector: discount-retail

## Method

Forensic thesis: the post-Family-Dollar standalone Dollar Tree was re-rated as a clean compounder on a flagship Q1 FY2026 beat (adjusted EPS +38%), but the beat is assembled from materials the company itself labels temporary or cyclical, and the brand's defining asset — the fixed single price — has been voluntarily dismantled into a multi-price ladder. Forensic moves used: (1) traffic vs. ticket decomposition — comp is price-driven (+4.5% ticket) on negative traffic (-1.0%); (2) moat-vs-loophole — the $1.25-to-$7 ladder ends single-price differentiation and drops DLTR into the DG/FIVE/WMT value brawl; (3) quality-of-earnings — freight, shrink, and a ~$110M IEEPA tariff refund are reversible/non-recurring; (4) adjusted-vs-GAAP divergence in the full-year guide (GAAP ~$6.50–6.90 vs adjusted $6.70–7.10); (5) denominator illusion — $595–600M buyback shrinks share count; (6) priced-for-perfection asymmetry at ~16.7x forward; (7) demonstration-vs-deployment on multi-price rollout; (8) commodity-price-taker / cyclical-priced-as-secular. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the divestiture was right, margin gains are real, comps are positive, and multi-price is a legitimate adaptation.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (primary: Dollar Tree 8-K/10-Q for quarter ended May 2, 2026, filed/reported late May 2026; corroborated by StockTitan, Yahoo Finance, Motley Fool transcript, SEC EDGAR):
- Q1 FY2026 quarter ended May 2, 2026; results released/filed ~May 28, 2026.
- Net sales $4,970.5M, +7.2% YoY (total revenue ~$4,975.8M including other revenue).
- Comparable store sales +3.5%; traffic -1.0%, average ticket +4.5%.
- Gross margin 36.8% (vs 35.6% PY), +120 bps; operating margin 9.5% (vs 8.3% PY), +120 bps.
- GAAP income from continuing operations $347.3M; GAAP diluted EPS from continuing ops $1.76 (vs $1.47 PY).
- Adjusted diluted EPS $1.74, +38% YoY; consensus ~$1.55.
- Share repurchases ~$595–600.4M (5,552,410 shares); shares outstanding ~193.4M at May 2, 2026.
- FY2026 guidance: net sales $20.5–20.7B, comps +3–4%, adjusted EPS from continuing ops $6.70–7.10 (raised); GAAP diluted EPS guide ~$6.50–6.90.
- Family Dollar sold July 5, 2025 to Brigade Capital Management + Macellum Capital Management; base price $1,007.5M cash; ~$800M net proceeds.
- Multi-price (Dollar Tree 3.0): ~5,300 stores at end of FY2025; expanded assortment in "majority" of stores by May 2, 2026; range $1.25–$7.
- Tariffs: early estimate ~$20M/month; ~$110M IEEPA-related refunds received post-quarter.
- Valuation: forward P/E ~16.7; stock ~$112.88 (June 15, 2026); ~9,100+ Dollar Tree stores.

Corrections made during drafting: none material. The article uses "net sales" $4,970.5M (not the slightly higher total-revenue figure) and frames GAAP-vs-adjusted guidance as a ~20-cent spread, consistent with sources. Tariff refund described as "approximately $110 million" and non-recurring.
