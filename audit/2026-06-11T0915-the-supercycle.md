## Method

News-driven: Micron crossing $1T on the AI memory boom; MU among the day's movers. Original market-research
piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis & series capstone: the market has declared "the death of
cyclicality" in tech's most cyclical business and paid a permanent ($1T) price for peak-cycle earnings; but
fat peak margins are the FUEL for the capex binge that ends every memory cycle; supplier discipline always
breaks; cyclicals look cheapest at the top (peak earnings); the bust was two years ago (negative margins); the
demand rides the AI capex wave; "this time is different" marks tops. Distinct from the chip pieces (TSMC/ASML/
Broadcom/Nvidia/SMCI) — this is memory + the cyclicality theme. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `a95572c` → published; title "The Supercycle" held)

Verified as drafted:
- MU crossed **$1T** market cap first time **May 26, 2026** (+**19%** session; UBS PT $535→$1,625).
- Q2 FY2026 revenue **$23.86B** (+**196%** YoY, +**75%** seq); gross margin **75%** (guide ~81%); operating
  income $16.5B; EPS beat.
- **HBM** 2026 capacity **sold out**; HBM market ~**$62B** 2026 (from ~$30B); **HBM4** shipping (Nvidia Vera
  Rubin). Three-supplier "discipline"; tight into 2027-28.
- History: DRAM peaked late 1995, **-51% (1996)**, **-65% (1997)**; boom→over-invest→glut→crash.
- **2022-23 bust**: FY2023 **net loss ~$5.8B**, gross margin ~**-9%** for the year (**<-30%** one quarter) —
  added at fact-check (confirms the negative-margin claim with hard figures).
- Harvard prof (cycles since 1980s) to **Fortune**: "looks like every other memory cycle, just bigger… this
  too will pass."

No corrections; one enhancement (FY2023 loss/margin figures). "Cheapest at the top," "supplier discipline,"
and the series-capstone close are analysis built on the verified facts.
