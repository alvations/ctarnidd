## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: cyclical-priced-as-secular. Progressive is the best-run US primary auto insurer, but its record results — May 2026 combined ratio 82.1 and +36% net income; Q1 2026 combined ratio 86.4, $2.8B net income (+10%), policies in force +9% — were manufactured by a once-in-a-generation hard-pricing cycle (aggressive 2023-24 rate hikes earning in just as used-car/parts/severity loss-cost inflation cooled). The company's own enterprise target is a 96 combined ratio; it is running ~14 points better, which regulators and competition mean-revert. Supporting forensic moves: (1) the 82 is a cycle reading not a skill score; (2) premium P/B and P/E multiply a peak margin by a peak multiple; (3) the Florida "tell" — ~$1B in policyholder credits + ~-8% rate-cut filings by the top-5 groups; (4) denominator illusion — 9% policy growth is partly reversible share grab from rivals who exited the hard market; (5) quality-of-earnings — favorable prior-year reserve releases cannot repeat; (6) coincident investment-income tailwind; (7) commodity price-taker vs pricing-power moat; (8) property-mix shift imports cat volatility; (9) priced-for-perfection asymmetry. Honest-nuance section concedes Progressive's genuine, compounding telematics/segmentation edge, low expense ratio, decade of through-cycle book-value compounding, and that timing of normalization is uncertain — a caution on the price, not the company.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (PGR 8-K / 10-Q / StockTitan / Quiver / Carrier Management / Insurance Business / Florida OIR / GEICO):
- Q1 2026 (YTD to Mar 31): combined ratio **86.4**; net income **$2.8B** (+~10%); diluted EPS **$4.80** (+10%); policies in force **+9%**; net premiums written **$23.6B** (+6%); net premiums earned **$21.0B** (+8%); personal auto NPW **$18.9B** (+7.8%). Reported ~Apr 15, 2026.
- May 2026 (monthly): net income **$1.445B** (+36%); EPS **$2.47**; combined ratio **82.1** (improved ~4.8 pts); NPW **$7.027B** (+6%); net premiums earned **$7.361B** (+10%).
- Enterprise target combined ratio **96** (long-stated management goal).
- April 2026: profit +~10% despite storm losses (property-cat exposure).
- Florida: Progressive reported ~**$1B** in credits to policyholders to the OIR; top-5 FL auto groups (incl. Progressive + GEICO) filing avg **~-8%** rate change for 2026 (vs -7.4% aggregate request prior year); FL commissioner announcing "more significant" auto rate decreases; GEICO cutting FL rates, citing legal-system reforms.

### Editorial care
- "82 combined ratio" in headline/dek refers explicitly to the **May 2026 monthly** print (82.1); the quarterly figure (86.4) is stated separately and not conflated. Article makes the monthly-vs-quarterly distinction explicit.
- Reserve-release and investment-income paragraphs are framed as **directional mechanics inherent to the cycle**, not as specific quantified claims about PGR's exact Q1 development or NII dollars (those exact line items were not independently re-verified to a figure, so no number is asserted — only the structural argument, which is standard P&C accounting).
- Valuation framed qualitatively ("premium multiple," "above the P&C peer group") rather than asserting a specific P/E, to avoid an unverified precise number.
- Thesis is a caution on the multiple, not an allegation of wrongdoing; no misconduct claimed. Premise verified TRUE as of most recent results.
