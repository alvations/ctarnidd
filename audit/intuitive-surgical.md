# Audit — Intuitive Surgical's 23% growth is real — but a 40x multiple is buying a monopoly the FDA just ended

## Method

Forensic thesis: Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci razor-and-blades installed-base annuity is genuine and high-quality, but the stock's ~40x forward / ~18x revenue multiple capitalizes an *uncontested* soft-tissue robotics monopoly into perpetuity. That monopoly materially changed in December 2025 (FDA clears Medtronic Hugo for U.S. urology — first soft-tissue robotic rival in 20+ years) and January 2026 (J&J files Ottava de novo request in general surgery). The article applies several forensic frames: razor-and-blades-annuity-as-double-edge; moat-vs-monopoly; demonstration-vs-deployment timing trap; priced-for-perfection asymmetry; tariff/commodity price-taker exposure (100bps tariff drag + semiconductor memory + freight in 2026 gross-margin guide); platform-transition (da Vinci 5) timing/lumpiness risk; denominator-illusion on large-base growth; customer/hospital negotiating-power shift. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes deep training/evidence moat, accretive da Vinci 5 margins, defensive recurring annuity, net-cash balance sheet, and under-penetrated TAM.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against Intuitive Surgical Q1 2026 8-K earnings release (filed April 2026, SEC EDGAR CIK 0001035267) and corroborating coverage:
- Q1 2026 revenue: $2.77B, +23% vs $2.25B in Q1 2025 — CONFIRMED
- Net income (GAAP): $821.5M — CONFIRMED
- Non-GAAP EPS: $2.50 vs $1.81 prior-year — CONFIRMED
- Total procedure growth: ~17%; da Vinci procedures +16% to ~847,000; Ion +39% to ~43,000 — CONFIRMED
- System placements: 431 da Vinci systems (vs 367 Q1'25); of which 232 da Vinci 5 (vs 147 Q1'25), ~53% of placements — CONFIRMED
- Installed base: 11,395 systems as of 3/31/2026, +12% vs 10,189 a year prior — CONFIRMED
- 2026 da Vinci procedure growth guidance raised to 13.5%–15.5% — CONFIRMED
- 2026 non-GAAP gross-margin guidance 67.5%–68.5%, including ~100bps (1.0% of revenue) tariff impact plus freight/semiconductor-memory cost increases; management warns additional tariffs "could be material" — CONFIRMED
- Medtronic Hugo FDA clearance for U.S. urology: Dec 3, 2025; first large-medtech soft-tissue robot in U.S. in 20+ years; first U.S. case completed early 2026 — CONFIRMED
- J&J Ottava de novo FDA submission in general surgery: January 2026 — CONFIRMED

Figures SOFTENED / labeled as ranges to stay defensible:
- Valuation expressed as "near 40x forward earnings" and "roughly 18x revenue." Sources span forward P/E ~40.84 (GuruFocus, Jun 5 2026) to ~45–46 (other trackers, May 2026); article uses the conservative ~40x and frames qualitatively. Revenue multiple derived from ~$150–177B implied market cap range vs ~$10B+ run-rate revenue — stated approximately, not as a hard figure.
- Stock price: "near $499 in February 2026" and "closer to $426 in early June 2026" — sourced; labeled as point-in-time, not current.
- Industry forward multiple "near 19" (medical-device median) — from GuruFocus comparison; stated as context.

No invented numbers. No claim asserted beyond what a primary or reputable secondary source supports.
