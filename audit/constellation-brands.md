# Constellation Brands' beer engine just stalled to +1% as Modelo and Corona shrink

## Method

Forensic thesis: Constellation Brands (STZ) is owned as a structural-growth beer franchise — a company that grows volume while U.S. alcohol consumption declines — and the fiscal 2026 results (year ended February 28, 2026; reported April 8, 2026) no longer support that premise. The beer engine, the entire reason to own the stock, has stalled: Q4 beer net sales grew only 1%, full-year beer net sales fell 3% to $8.32B, shipments fell 3.8%, and depletions fell 2.1%. Critically, the two flagship brands the bull case rests on are in decline (Modelo Especial depletions ~-3%, Corona Extra ~-7%), with growth backfilled by far smaller labels (Pacifico +15%, Victoria +16%). The wine-and-spirits segment was deliberately divested (-58% net sales, -72.9% shipments), removing diversification at the exact moment the beer engine flattened. Margins are compressing (Q4 operating margin -340bps to 33.2%; beer-margin guidance cut to 37-38% from 39-40%) on an overbuilt Veracruz brewery's fixed-cost absorption plus aluminum tariffs. The forward beer-revenue guidance is a flat band of -1% to +1%. Added forensic frames: policy/demographic exposure (Mexican-import supply chain + Hispanic-skewed consumer sensitive to immigration/tariff dynamics); adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings (Q4 adjusted EPS $1.90 vs GAAP ~$1.16, with the Canopy cannabis impairment history coloring trust in adjustments); leverage against a flat cash engine (~2.7x net leverage, $1.6B returned to holders); and de-rated-multiple-is-not-cheap.
