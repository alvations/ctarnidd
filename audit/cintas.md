# Audit — Cintas Trades at 36 Times Earnings on 8% Growth as It Bets $5.5 Billion on UniFirst

Article: `2026-06-18T1300-cintas.md`
Ticker: CTAS (Nasdaq) · Sector: business-services

## Method

Forensic thesis: Cintas is a genuinely superb uniform-rental and facility-services compounder, but it trades near 36x trailing earnings — a software-style multiple — on a business whose revenue is structurally indexed to employment and business formation. The piece applies several forensic frames to the gap between that premium price and the underlying reality:

- **Priced-for-perfection / cyclical-priced-as-secular** — a headcount-linked, partly cyclical services firm valued as if its growth floated free of the labor market.
- **Decelerating growth beneath a record headline** — organic growth of 8.2% in FQ3'26 is healthy but below the post-pandemic double-digit rates that earned the multiple.
- **Bought-growth-masks-organic-stall** — the ~$5.5B UniFirst acquisition supplements decelerating organic growth; acquired growth is lower-quality for a premium-multiple stock.
- **Quality of earnings** — adjusted EPS guidance excludes UniFirst transaction costs; operating-margin comparison is normalized against a prior-year one-time gain.
- **Denominator illusion** — EPS grew 9.7% while net income grew 8.4%; buybacks lift per-share figures faster than the profit pool.
- **Commodity price-taker at peak margin** — record 51.0% gross margin extrapolated as a baseline despite input-cost (labor, energy, fuel, cotton) exposure.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the route-density moat, recurring contracted revenue, 45-year dividend streak, capital discipline, and the historical fact that the "expensive" call on CTAS has usually been wrong.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (FQ3 fiscal 2026, reported March 25, 2026):
- Revenue $2.84B, up 8.9% YoY (from $2.61B). CONFIRMED.
- Organic revenue growth 8.2% (ex-acquisitions and FX). CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin $1.45B = 51.0% of revenue, all-time high (vs 50.6% prior year). CONFIRMED.
- Diluted EPS $1.24, up 9.7% (vs $1.13). CONFIRMED.
- Net income $502.5M, up 8.4% (vs $463.5M). CONFIRMED.
- Operating income 23.2% of revenue vs 23.4% prior year; ex one-time prior-year gain, +40bps. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: revenue $11.21B–$11.24B; adjusted diluted EPS $4.86–$4.90 (excludes UniFirst transaction costs). CONFIRMED.
- Returned ~$1.45B to shareholders via buybacks + dividends in the quarter; $1B buyback authorization (Oct 28, 2025). CONFIRMED.
- 45 consecutive years of dividend increases. CONFIRMED.
- Trailing P/E ~36x (36.52x per public.com as of June 13, 2026; 36.31x at $170.28 on TTM EPS $4.69 per fullratio). Article uses "roughly 36 times" — defensible range. CONFIRMED as range.
- Forward P/E ~30.6x (gurufocus, March 28, 2026). Noted for context; article centers on trailing ~36x.

CORRECTIONS made during fact-check:
- UniFirst deal value tightened from "roughly $5 billion" to "roughly $5.5 billion." The final transaction announced March 11, 2026 was ~$5.5B ($275/share cash); an earlier January 2025 proposal implied ~$5.3B. Headline and body updated to $5.5B and "multiple attempts before it finally closed."

Nothing in the article asserts a figure that could not be sourced. The cyclicality/employment-linkage claims are framed as structural characteristics of a headcount-billed rental model, not as adjudicated facts.
