# Audit — NextEra Energy (NEE)

Subject: NextEra Energy, Inc. (NYSE: NEE) — utilities sector

## Method

Forensic thesis: NEE trades at a growth premium to peer utilities (~22x earnings vs a sector nearer 20x) on the narrative that a regulated Florida monopoly (FPL) bolted to the largest U.S. clean-energy developer (NextEra Energy Resources) is a secular compounder. The piece argues the premium rests on three correlated, rate-sensitive dependencies: (1) continuous cheap access to a ~$40B/year capital program on a $221B balance sheet with ~$94B long-term debt; (2) federal tax-credit monetization (IRA transferability) underwriting the development business' returns; (3) the financial complexity of CEPF/tax-equity/equity-linked structures. The load-bearing evidence is the company's OWN action: its former yieldco NextEra Energy Partners (NEP) suspended distributions to zero in Jan 2025, rebranded as XPLR Infrastructure, and committed to buying back ~$3.7B of convertible equity portfolio financings (CEPFs) it could no longer roll — the "canary" demonstrating what rate stress does to the structure. Frames used: cyclical-priced-as-secular; bought-growth-vs-organic; adjusted-vs-GAAP; moat-vs-loophole (subsidy dependence); priced-for-perfection asymmetry; customer/partner & regulatory concentration.
