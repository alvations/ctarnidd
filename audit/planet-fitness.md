# Planet Fitness cut 2026 growth in half after its strongest sign-up season delivered 36% fewer new members

## Method

Forensic thesis: PLNT is a capital-light franchisor whose premium narrative and unit-expansion runway depend on rising members-per-club, healthy franchisee returns, and an equipment-financing flywheel. The Q1 2026 print (reported May 7, 2026) beat estimates on the trailing scorecard but exposed a denominator illusion — roughly 90% of the 3.5% same-club comp came from price (rate), not membership volume — while net new member adds fell ~36% year over year during the industry's strongest (Q1) joining season. Management slashed full-year guidance (same-club 4-5% → ~1%; revenue ~9% → ~7%; adj-EBITDA ~10% → ~6%; adj net income to -2%) and canceled planned price increases, and the stock fell ~30%. Frames used: denominator illusion, bought-vs-organic growth, cyclical-priced-as-secular, quality-of-earnings (adjusted vs GAAP/volume metrics), franchisee/partner-return dependence, equipment commodity-cyclicality, priced-for-perfection asymmetry.
