## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: SaaS runs on PER-SEAT pricing; Salesforce
built ~$40B on it. AI agents (Agentforce) do the work of the humans those seats represent → fewer seats. So
Salesforce sells the weapon aimed at its own model — the purest innovator's dilemma. Agentforce ARR $1.2B (+205%)
is real but tiny vs the ~$40B legacy base; the race is whether new AI revenue outruns seat EROSION (customers
already cutting ~10% of seats). Pricing CHAOS = the tell (consumption→tepid→AELA per-"digital-worker"; 3+
models). Sector-wide proof: Atlassian's FIRST-EVER seat decline; HubSpot −51%/Monday −44%; ~$285B "SaaSpocalypse."
CRM −~32% YTD. Value-trap risk (cheap-vs-history ≠ cheap-vs-a-dismantled-engine; consumption = lower multiple).
Honest-nuance: self-cannibalizing is the RIGHT move; agents-as-digital-labor could exceed seat revenue; Salesforce
owns the data/workflow; +205% real; fear largely priced.
