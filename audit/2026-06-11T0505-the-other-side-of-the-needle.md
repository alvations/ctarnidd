## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis & series capstone: Novo Nordisk is the
GLP-1 *loser* — the counterpart to batch-1's Eli Lilly ("The Appetite Trade") — and the cleanest pharmaceutical
expression of the series' core lesson: being right about the technology and right about the price are different
problems. A genuine miracle drug, a company that fell from ~$650B (Europe's most valuable) by more than
two-thirds, lapped by Lilly, facing a patent cliff, price war, failed next-gen (CagriSema), and a CEO ouster.
Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `ddfe67e` → published; title "The Other Side of the Needle" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Mkt cap peaked ~**$650B** mid-2024 (Europe's most valuable); shares past **1,000 DKK**; ADR now high-$30s,
  near 52-wk low (~$35).
- 2026 guidance: sales AND operating profit both declining **5–13%** (cFX), "unprecedented pricing pressure."
- **Eli Lilly** >**60%** US obesity market (≈**63%** branded scripts / 57% total GLP-1 by Q3 2025); tirzepatide
  overtook Wegovy/Ozempic sales in 2025.
- **CagriSema** disappointed (REDEFINE-1 missed 25% target, 22.7% weight loss, −26% Dec 2024; later
  tirzepatide-comparison concerns); "path back" framing.
- Pricing: up to **48%** India cuts; **$675** US list price effective **Jan 1, 2027** (~50% Wegovy / ~35%
  Ozempic); **Hims & Hers** patent suit (Feb 9, 2026); FDA compounding crackdown.
- CEO **Maziar Mike Doustdar** (first non-Danish); ex-CEO **Lars Rebien Sørensen** chair; execs out; "worse
  before better." Oral **Wegovy pill** 170,000+ in ~4 weeks. Lipitor/Pfizer patent-cliff precedent (historical).

CORRECTION at fact-check: decline magnitude DIVERGES across sources (Reuters ~55% from June-2024 peak; Motley
Fool "72% crash"; 24/7 "75%"; ADR math ~69%) → softened "75% / three-quarters" throughout to "**~70% / more
than two-thirds**" (robust). The "right on tech, wrong on price" capstone and patent-cliff-history sections are
analysis built on the verified facts.
