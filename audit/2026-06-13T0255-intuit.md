## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Intuit is priced as a premium AI compounder
(Q3 FY26 rev +10% to $8.6B, GAAP op income $4B, raised FY guide ~$21.3B / +13-14%), but its most profitable
franchise — TurboTax — monetizes the FRICTION of paid US tax filing, and the government's free Direct File was just
ELIMINATED for filing season 2026 (Trump admin; Treasury Sec/acting IRS commissioner Bessent: "the private sector
can do a better job"; 160 Democratic lawmakers pushing a bill to revive it). The near-term win exposes the
structure: TurboTax's moat is a POLITICAL TRUCE, not a structural wall. Frames: moat-vs-loophole / moat-is-a-
political-settlement (an election can re-litigate); monetizing-a-removable-friction (FTC sanctioned Intuit for
deceptive "free" marketing; decades of lobbying / the limited "Free File" stewardship); the American exception vs
return-free/pre-filled filing in peer democracies; TurboTax is the SLOWEST-growing major segment (~7%, vs GBS ~16%,
Credit Karma, TurboTax Live +38%); AI cuts both ways (commoditizes the simple DIY end while powering the premium
expert end); 17% workforce reduction = cost pressure/reallocation. Honest-nuance: QuickBooks is a genuine
election-proof moat; Intuit is diversified well beyond tax (much less TurboTax-dependent than before); Credit Karma
rebound; TurboTax Live +38%; the death of Direct File genuinely removes the near-term threat; real, working
AI-expert strategy; raised guidance; $4B op income.
