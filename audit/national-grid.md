# Audit — National Grid (NGG): record capex and 78p EPS, but a £44bn debt load funds the dividend

## Method

Forensic thesis: National Grid is marketed as a safe, CPIH-linked yield instrument, but it is structurally a leveraged option on regulatory generosity. The FY2026 (year ended 31 March 2026) results, reported 14 May 2026, show underlying operating profit +9% to £5.7bn and underlying EPS +8% to 78.0p — but the company invested a record £11.6bn, roughly double its operating profit, financed partly by £2.8bn of new net debt, lifting net debt to £44.2bn. The £70bn five-year plan extends this spend-twice-your-profit model indefinitely. Forensic frames used: bought-growth (asset growth is financed, not generated); moat-vs-loophole (the regulator, not the market, sets the allowed return — RIIO-T3's 6.12% real cost of equity); adjusted-vs-statutory quality of earnings (78.0p underlying vs 65.5p statutory, a ~16% wedge); priced-for-perfection asymmetry (8–10% EPS CAGR assumes flawless delivery); bond-proxy rate sensitivity; gearing engineered to climb toward high-60s; the 2024 £7bn rights issue and ~15% dividend rebase as a preview, not an aberration; dividend funded at the margin by borrowing, not free cash flow.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (SEC Form 6-K FY2026; National Grid 14 May 2026 statement; Investegate; StockTitan; Investing.com):
- Results reported 14 May 2026 for FY ended 31 March 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Underlying operating profit £5.7bn, +9%. CONFIRMED.
- Underlying EPS 78.0p, +8% at constant currency. CONFIRMED.
- Statutory EPS 65.5p, +9%. CONFIRMED.
- Total dividend 48.49p, +3.8%, CPIH-linked (final 32.14p). CONFIRMED.
- Record capital investment £11.6bn (2024/25: £9.8bn). CONFIRMED.
- Asset growth 10.9% (FY); 11.7% regulated asset growth cited in one source. CONFIRMED (article uses 10.9% as the headline FY figure and 11.7% as regulated asset growth — both sourced).
- Net debt £44.2bn at 31 March 2026, +£2.8bn YoY; increase reflects capex partly offset by £2.8bn divestment proceeds (National Grid Renewables, Grain LNG). CONFIRMED.
- Regulatory gearing 61%; expected to drift toward high-60s over RIIO-T3. CONFIRMED.
- £70bn five-year investment plan; 8–10% underlying EPS CAGR off 78.0p baseline; FY2027 EPS guided +13–15%. CONFIRMED.
- RIIO-T3 final determination: 6.12% real allowed cost of equity at 60% notional gearing, 2026–2031. CONFIRMED.
- May 2024 rights issue: £7bn fully underwritten, 7-for-24 at 645p, ~£6.8bn net, ~91% take-up, dividend rebased ~15%. CONFIRMED.
- NGG ADR price high-$80s (~$87.68); ADR yield mid-3% range; trailing P/E ~18–22 depending on source/measure. CONFIRMED as a defensible range; article presents these as ranges, not point assertions.

Corrections made during drafting: none material. Valuation multiples and ADR yield framed as ranges to reflect source dispersion. Asset-growth figure standardised to the 10.9% FY headline with 11.7% noted as regulated asset growth.
