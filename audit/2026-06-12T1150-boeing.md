## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Boeing's operational recovery is genuine
(revenue +14% to $22.2B, 143 deliveries, MAX ramping, FCF burn narrowing, S&P off CreditWatch, record $695B
backlog) — but the balance sheet says it is far from complete: ~$47-50B debt vs NEGATIVE shareholders' equity
(−$3.3B, owes more than it owns), FCF still −$1.45B (the $1-3B positive guide is back-half-loaded), Moody's Baa3
(one notch above junk) with negative outlook, $8B 2026 maturities, KC-46A defense losses showing the fixed-price
pathology persists, and the 777X ~7 years late. The 737 ramp is simultaneously the recovery and its biggest risk
(quality-vs-rate, post-MAX). Honest-nuance: the duopoly + $695B backlog mean the franchise survives almost any
balance-sheet stress; "survives and recovers eventually" ≠ "the worst is over now."

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Boeing 8-K/10-Q / StockTitan / Motley Fool / technotrenz):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$22.2B** (+14%); **FCF −$1.45-1.5B**; total debt carrying value **~$47B** (paid down $6.9B);
  cash+securities **$20.9B**; **143** deliveries; **record backlog $695B**; FY FCF guide **$1-3B** reaffirmed.
- 737 MAX **42/mo → 47 (summer) → 52**.

SOURCED (search-01; reputable aviation/finance, not reconfirmed in verify): **negative equity −$3.3B**; total
debt up to **$53.3B** (gross); **Moody's Baa3** / S&P CreditWatch-removal + negative outlook / junk-downgrade
risk; **777X ~7 yrs late, ~$15.9B charges (incl $4.9B Q3'25), ~30 jets parked at Paine Field, cert slipping past
2026**; defense +21% to $7.6B incl **~$0.6B KC-46A loss**.

### Notes / precision
- Article cites **no specific small quarterly net-loss dollar figure** (sources varied $4M vs $7M) — avoided the
  discrepancy deliberately; uses "burns cash" / "FCF −$1.45B" instead.
- "Roughly $50 billion of debt" sits between the $47B carrying value and the $53.3B gross figure — a defensible
  round-number characterization.
- Record **$695B backlog** added to the bull section after verification (strengthens honest-nuance).

No corrections to load-bearing facts. Bull case (duopoly, backlog, Ortberg credibility, earned S&P upgrade)
given a full honest-nuance section; thesis is "recovery incomplete / no margin of safety," not "Boeing fails."
