# Audit — Nucor's $3.23 quarter is built on a 50% tariff wall, not a wider moat

Article: `2026-06-21T1800-nucor.md` · Ticker: NUE · Sector: materials

## Method

The suggested angle (commodity price-taker riding tariff-protected prices into a rolling-over construction cycle; through-cycle-compounder narrative vs a mid-cycle multiple on falling ASPs) was tested against the most recent quarter. Premise required calibration, not a full pivot: Q1 2026 was a *strong* quarter (EPS beat, record shipments, *rising* ASPs and margins), not falling ASPs. The accurate forensic thesis is therefore a cyclical-priced-as-secular / moat-vs-loophole argument:

- The $3.23 print (+264% YoY) is flattered by a demolished year-ago comp ($0.77 / $156M net), a denominator illusion.
- The earnings engine was a 14% YoY rise in average sales price per ton and a 7M-ton record shipment quarter — both sitting on a 50% Section 232 tariff that cut import share from 22% to ~15%. That is a policy loophole, not a moat: every domestic peer sits behind the same wall.
- Record volume is partly import substitution (a one-time level shift), not recurring demand growth; Nucor itself guides only "slight" 2026 demand improvement.
- Management's own "harvest phase" framing is cyclical-peak vocabulary.
- The bull's $6.7B "through-cycle" EBITDA target (Nov 2022 Investor Day) is capitalized as if certain, while the single biggest variable — the tariff — is a political decision no operating excellence can hedge.
- Cyclical priced as secular; low spot P/E is a value-trap artifact of peak-flattered E.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes: best-run NA steelmaker (low-cost EAF model, cash-generative through troughs), fortress balance sheet ($2.48B cash + undrawn $2.25B revolver to 2030), genuine downstream diversification, and a real secular demand story (data centers, grid, infrastructure, reshoring).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (primary: Nucor IR / SEC 8-K & 10-Q FY2026; corroborated StockTitan, Investing.com, Recycling Today, ad-hoc-news):
- Q1 2026 net earnings attributable to stockholders: $743 million. CONFIRMED.
- Q1 2026 diluted EPS: $3.23. CONFIRMED.
- Q1 2026 net sales/revenue: $9.50 billion, +21% from $7.83B in Q1 2025. CONFIRMED.
- Year-ago Q1 2025: $156 million net / $0.77 EPS. CONFIRMED (implies +264% EPS, +376% net — both labeled in-article as comp-flattered).
- Gross profit ~$1.50B (+148% YoY); operating income ~$1.12B (+247% YoY); operating margin 12% vs 4%. CONFIRMED (third-party computation; figures consistent across sources). Labeled "roughly/about" in text.
- Record steel mill shipments: 7 million tons, highest quarterly volume in company history. CONFIRMED.
- Average sales price per ton: +14% YoY in steel mills segment. CONFIRMED.
- Guidance was $2.70–$2.80/sh; actual $3.23; consensus $2.82. CONFIRMED.
- Steel mills segment pretax ~$1.1B, more than double prior quarter. CONFIRMED.
- Import share fell from 22% (early 2025) to ~15% (Q1 2026). CONFIRMED (Nucor commentary via Argus/Steel Market Update).
- Section 232 tariff doubled from 25% to 50% in June 2025 for all partners except UK (25%). CONFIRMED.
- Q1 2026 capex $661M (~40% to West Virginia mill); FY capex guide ~$2.5B; ~$950M on Apple Grove (WV) mill in 2026; targeted completion late 2026. CONFIRMED (WV Gazette-Mail, earnings call).
- Capital returned to shareholders Q1 2026: $254M (dividends + buybacks). CONFIRMED.
- Cash & ST investments $2.48B; undrawn $2.25B revolver not expiring until March 2030. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025: EBITDA ~$4.2B; net earnings ~$1.7B reported / $1.8B adjusted; EPS $7.52 reported / $7.71 adjusted. CONFIRMED.
- FY2024 EPS $8.46 ($2.03B net); FY2023 EPS $18.00 ($4.53B net). CONFIRMED (8-K FY2025, Macrotrends).
- Q4 2025 EPS $1.73 missed $1.86 est; revenue $7.69B below $7.87B est. CONFIRMED.
- Nov 2022 Investor Day through-cycle EBITDA target $6.7B; ~$500M EBITDA uplift from completed projects. CONFIRMED.

Corrections made during drafting: none required after verification; all margin/operating-income figures hedged with "roughly/about" since they are derived rather than headline-reported.

No invented numbers. No allegation of wrongdoing — the thesis is an economic/valuation argument, not a fraud claim.
