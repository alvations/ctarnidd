## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: ASML is the most absolute monopoly in
industry (sole EUV maker) — and precisely because it is so essential it has become a geopolitical hostage:
losing ~a third of its market to export controls, selling to a cyclical oligopsony (TSMC ~40% of EUV), with
High-NA adoption uncertain, while the controls incubate its only possible future competitor. Pairs with
batch-3 #1 (TSMC) as the keystone beneath the keystone. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `f6d3401` → published; title "The One Machine" held)

Verified as drafted:
- **Sole EUV supplier**; Nikon & Canon exited EUV >10 years ago. High-NA EUV ~**$360–400M**/unit.
- Q1 2026 sales **€8.8B**, gross margin **53.0%**, net income €2.8B; net system sales €6.3B (official release).
- FY2026 guidance **€36–40B** (GM 51–53%) — confirmed; the secondary "€46B" figure was wrong and not used.
- Backlog **€38.8B** (YE 2025); 2030 outlook **€44–60B**.
- China: EUV never shipped to China; China **19%** of Q1 2026 system sales (from **36%** Q4 2025); ~**20%** of
  FY2026 revenue (from ~33% in 2025); **MATCH Act** would ban DUV exports to China.
- Customer concentration: TSMC/Samsung/Intel = majority of EUV; **TSMC ~40%** of EUV deliveries.
- High-NA: TSMC reportedly balking on cost; Intel/Samsung/SK hynix adopting. Export-control "backfire"
  (China building competing tools) sourced to ASML leadership / Global Times.
- Cyclicality (2022–23 semi slump) and the EUV machine's physics/supply-chain difficulty: analysis/background,
  consistent with sources.
