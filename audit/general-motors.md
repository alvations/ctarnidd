# Audit — General Motors' record EPS is engineered by a shrinking share count, not a growing business

Article: `2026-06-20T1800-general-motors.md` · Ticker: GM · Sector: autos

## Method

Forensic thesis (denominator illusion + quality-of-earnings + cyclical-priced-as-secular): GM's Q1 2026 "blowout" — a 40%+ adjusted-EPS beat and a raised full-year outlook — is driven primarily by financial engineering and non-recurring items rather than organic growth. Three legs support the headline: (1) a share count GM has shrunk by 40%+ since 2015 (buyback inflates per-share figures while net income to stockholders is guided down), (2) a one-time ~1.5-point margin benefit from a Supreme Court IEEPA tariff accounting adjustment (~$500M favorable, non-recurring), and (3) a favorable base effect against prior-period Cruise/China write-downs. The cash engine is high-margin but deeply cyclical full-size ICE trucks/SUVs into a softening US consumer; EV unit still booking restructuring charges; China profitable but a fraction of its former scale. The asymmetry: the levers flattering EPS (buyback, truck mix, tariff true-up) reverse together if the cycle turns.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported April 28, 2026):
- Revenue $43.6B, down from $44.0B in Q1 2025 — CONFIRMED (TradingView, CNBC).
- Net income attributable to stockholders $2.6B — CONFIRMED.
- EBIT-adjusted $4.3B — CONFIRMED.
- Diluted GAAP EPS $2.82; adjusted EPS $3.70 (up 33% YoY from $2.78; vs ~$2.64 consensus, >40% beat) — CONFIRMED (Quartr, TIKR, CNBC).
- North America EBIT-adjusted ~$3.66–3.7B, up double digits YoY, 10.1% margin — CONFIRMED.
- Raised FY2026 EBIT-adjusted guidance to $13.5–$15.5B; adjusted EPS $11.50–$13.50 — CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 net-income-to-stockholders forecast LOWERED to $9.9–$11.4B due to special charges — CONFIRMED.
- ~1.5-point margin benefit from Supreme Court IEEPA tariff accounting adjustment (~$500M favorable) — CONFIRMED (CNBC, StockTitan).
- Gross tariff costs guided $2.5–$3.5B for FY — CONFIRMED.
- $1.1B additional EV restructuring charges in Q1 2026 (supplier contract cancellations) — CONFIRMED.
- EV losses narrowed several hundred million YoY — CONFIRMED.
- China equity income ~$100M ex plant-sale gain, sixth consecutive profitable quarter — CONFIRMED.
- Q1 2026 buyback $800M at ~$75/share; ~$19B cash; $5.5B remaining authorization; new $6B 2026 program — CONFIRMED.
- Shares outstanding ~904M end-2025 vs ~995M end-2024 vs ~1.2B end-2023; ~1.54B (2015) → ~0.90B (2025), 40%+ reduction — CONFIRMED (gmauthority, stockdividendscreener).
- Prior-period charges: ~$4B China JV restructuring/impairment + ~$500M Cruise shutdown (Q4 2024 area), >$10B invested in Cruise since 2016, ~$1B/yr expected Cruise savings — CONFIRMED (WardsAuto, SmartCitiesDive, RoadToAutonomy).

Corrections made: none required; all figures softened to ranges/approximations where sources varied (e.g., "roughly $3.66 billion," "on the order of $500 million"). Thesis premise held up against the most recent quarter.
