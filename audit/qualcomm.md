# Audit — Qualcomm's $238B Valuation Leans on a Royalty Toll Apple Is Walking Away From

## Method

Forensic thesis: Qualcomm's market value rests on a high-margin licensing (QTL) toll and a premium-Android chip (QCT) franchise whose single largest customer — Apple, one of three customers each ≥10% of FY2025 revenue — has publicly committed to designing Qualcomm out. Apple's own modem (C1) is set to reach ~20% of iPhones in 2026 and 0% by 2027, with the standard-essential 5G licensing agreement expiring in 2027. Independent estimates size the combined modem + RF revenue cliff at ~$7.3B–$7.8B annually beginning in 2028. The standalone fiscal Q2 2026 quarter already shows the strain (revenue down ~2-3% YoY, QCT handset -13% YoY, operating margin compressing ~34%→31%), while the diversification engines (auto, IoT) are real but too small to backfill the cliff. The stock trades near a 52-week high at ~23x earnings, pricing a cyclical China recovery and pricing the known Apple cliff at roughly zero — an asymmetric setup. Frames used: customer concentration, moat-vs-loophole, denominator illusion (flat smartphone TAM), cyclical-priced-as-secular, demonstration-vs-deployment (auto design-win backlog), quality-of-earnings (margin mix-down), priced-for-perfection asymmetry.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Qualcomm fiscal Q2 2026, reported April 29, 2026; primary release on company IR / q4cdn, corroborated by multiple outlets):
- Total revenue: ~$10.6 billion, down ~2-3% YoY (sources cite 2.2%–3%; article softened to "roughly 2% to 3%"). CONFIRMED.
- Non-GAAP diluted EPS: ~$2.65, down ~7% YoY, high end of guidance. CONFIRMED.
- QCT revenue: ~$9.1 billion, down ~4.2% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- QCT handset sub-line: down ~13% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- QTL revenue: ~$1.4 billion, up ~5% YoY, EBT margin ~72%. CONFIRMED.
- QCT automotive: ~$1.3 billion, up ~38% YoY; crossed ~$5B annualized run rate. CONFIRMED.
- QCT IoT: ~$1.7 billion, up ~9% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Non-GAAP operating margin: ~31.0% vs ~34% prior-year quarter. CONFIRMED.
- China QCT Android shipments "meaningfully below" end demand; management expects China handset bottom in fiscal Q3 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Q3 FY2026 guidance: revenue $9.2–$10.0 billion; non-GAAP EPS $2.10–$2.30; QCT $7.9–8.5B; QTL $1.2–1.4B. CONFIRMED.
- Apple modem: Qualcomm expects ~20% of iPhones in 2026, 0% by 2027; licensing agreement to 2027. CONFIRMED.
- Apple revenue cliff: combined modem (~$5.7–5.9B) + RF/subsystems (~$1.6–1.9B) = ~$7.3–7.8B annual shortfall beginning 2028 (analyst estimate, labeled as estimate). CONFIRMED as estimate.
- Apple paid >$2.5B in 2024 for Qualcomm patent licenses (analyst estimate, labeled). CONFIRMED as estimate.
- Apple, Samsung, Xiaomi each ≥10% of FY2025 consolidated revenue (per 10-K). CONFIRMED.
- QTL royalty structure: ~3.25% of device net selling price, subject to per-unit caps. CONFIRMED (described as "historically structured around").
- Global smartphone shipments Q1 2026: -2.9% YoY to ~293.8 million units; full-year ~1% growth forecast. CONFIRMED.
- Stock ~$226.50, market cap ~$238B, P/E ~23.17, forward P/E ~22.78, dividend yield ~1.67%, 52-week range ~$121.99–$259.92 (as of June 19, 2026). CONFIRMED.

Corrections made: revenue YoY decline softened from "roughly 2%" to "roughly 2% to 3%" in body to span the range reported across sources. All analyst-derived figures (Apple revenue cliff, $2.5B licensing) explicitly labeled as estimates in the article. No invented numbers.
