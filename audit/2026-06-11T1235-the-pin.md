## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: consumer AI hardware dies of one iron
law — it does a subset of what the smartphone already does, on a device you buy/charge/carry/learn separately.
Humane and Rabbit are the two cautionary post-mortems; OpenAI/Jony Ive's $6.5B io bet and reported 40–50M-unit
Foxconn commitment is the most credible version of a bet with, so far, a 0% success rate — made harder because
Apple/Google have already absorbed the assistant into the phone for free. Includes the "demonstration vs
deployment" frame, the venture-lottery incentive that funds the graveyard, and an honest-nuance section on what
OpenAI would need (new category, not phone replacement; a job the phone does badly; frontier models +
distribution).

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title "The Pin" held)

CONFIRMED (primary/strong):
- Humane: raised **$230M**; sold to **HP for $116M** (Feb 2025), Pin discontinued; **<10,000** shipped; reviews
  panned (slow/heat/battery); **returns exceeded sales** by 2024; $699 + $24/mo. (Fortune, TechCrunch, Tech
  Startups.)
- Rabbit R1: ~**100,000** units; **mass returns**; struggling to make payroll; OS = **modified Android**
  ("Rabbit Launcher" installable on Android phones). (TechRadar + others.)
- OpenAI acquired **io Products** (Jony Ive co-founded) all-stock ~**$6.5B** (May 2025); first device **H2
  2026**, screenless/AI-native. (Axios + multiple.)

HEDGED (secondary/reported — language kept soft in article):
- Rabbit "**~5,000 active users / ~95% abandonment**": secondary write-ups, not a primary disclosure → article
  says "**reportedly** had about 5,000 active users."
- **Foxconn 40–50M units**: secondary (MindStudio/Introl) → article says "**said to be** tooling up to build
  forty to fifty million units."
- Sora "~$1M/day" referenced only qualitatively in the "wider overreach" section (not asserted as a hard figure
  in the body; the body says "bled cash at extraordinary rates").

No corrections to load-bearing facts; the two reported figures carry explicit hedges. The law-of-the-rectangle,
assistant-absorption, and venture-lottery sections are analysis.
