# Audit — Twilio rallies 30% on an AI story, but four points of its 20% growth are pass-through and acquisitions

## Method

Forensic thesis: Twilio's Q1 2026 (reported April 30, 2026) was a genuinely good quarter — fastest growth in three years, first GAAP profit milestone consolidated, SBC below 10% of revenue for the first time since IPO. The assigned angle ("growth decelerated hard / commoditized / heavy SBC") is partly stale and was PIVOTED. The accurate forensic frame:

1. **Denominator illusion / quality-of-growth:** Reported revenue grew 20% but organic only 16%; the wedge is acquisitions + incremental A2P carrier pass-through fees (revenue Twilio collects and remits to carriers, keeping ~nothing). The same fees are a ~70bp margin headwind — padding the top line while dragging margin.
2. **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry:** Stock +~30% on the print, +~53% TTM, ~$21B mkt cap, forward P/E ~33. Yet full-year organic guidance is just 9.5–10.5% — the company forecasts the organic engine to roughly halve from the 16% it just printed.
3. **Cyclical-priced-as-secular:** Consumption (per-message/per-minute) revenue surge in an AI-spend up-cycle is being valued as a secular platform.
4. **Disappeared metric:** Twilio discontinued Active Customer Accounts disclosure beginning Q1 2026 — the exact quarter of the re-rating.
5. **Adjusted-vs-GAAP:** Non-GAAP op income $279M vs GAAP $108M; ~$171M wedge dominated by SBC (~$137M implied, 9.7% of rev).
6. **Buyback runs to stand still** vs SBC; commodity price-taker floor under the AI layer.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes: real 16% organic inflection (from 12% prior year), authentic GAAP profit, exceptional SBC discipline, DBNRR recovery to 114%, real AI product pull, picks-and-shovels positioning.

- Revenue: $1.407B (~$1.41B), +20% reported, +16% organic YoY — CONFIRMED. Highest growth in 3+ years.
- Reported-vs-organic wedge = acquisitions + incremental A2P fees — CONFIRMED (company statement).
- GAAP income from operations: $108M — CONFIRMED.
- Non-GAAP income from operations: $279M, record 19.8% margin — CONFIRMED.
- GAAP diluted EPS $0.57; GAAP net income attributable to common ~$90M — CONFIRMED ($0.57 corroborated by TradingView; $90M from IR/search).
- Non-GAAP diluted EPS $1.50 vs $1.27 consensus — CONFIRMED.
- SBC 9.7% of revenue, below 10% first time since IPO, ahead of 2027 target, down 220bp YoY — CONFIRMED. ~$137M dollar figure is IMPLIED (9.7% × $1.41B) and labeled as such in the article.
- ~70bp non-GAAP margin headwind from incremental U.S. carrier fees — CONFIRMED.
- DBNRR 114% (vs 107% Q1 2025) — CONFIRMED. Peak-era 125–130%+ is historical context (2021), labeled as such.
- FCF $132.3M (~$132M) Q1; operating cash flow $153.2M; FY26 FCF guide $1.08–1.10B — CONFIRMED.
- Active Customer Accounts disclosure discontinued beginning Q1 2026; ~43,000 net adds noted — CONFIRMED.
- Buybacks: $253.4M in Q1; ~$1.1B done of $2B authorization (Jan 2025, expires Dec 31 2027); $892.0M remaining as of Mar 31, 2026 — CONFIRMED.
- Q2 2026 guide $1.420–1.430B (+15.5–16.5%); FY26 reported 14–15%, organic raised to 9.5–10.5% from 8–9% — CONFIRMED.
- Stock ~+30% after print (after-hours ~$183.34 vs $140.91 prior); ~+53% TTM; ~$21B mkt cap; forward P/E ~33 (33.53 per GuruFocus as of May 31, 2026) — CONFIRMED, with "~"/"roughly" hedging used throughout.

Corrections made vs original angle: PIVOTED from "decelerated hard / GAAP loss" — Twilio is GAAP profitable and accelerating.
