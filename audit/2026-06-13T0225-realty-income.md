## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Realty Income's monthly dividend is
genuinely safe (98.9% occupancy, 30+ yr Dividend Aristocrat, AFFO/share +6.6%), but its GROWTH engine is a
financing spread — cost of capital vs acquisition cap rate — and higher rates compressed it from both ends, so FY
AFFO/share growth is guided only ~3.0-3.7% (vs its cheap-money compounding past). Frames: spread-business-not-a-
building (external-growth arbitrage on a slow rent stream); rates-throttle-the-engine (cost of debt up + dividend
yield up = expensive equity); size / law-of-large-numbers treadmill ($9.5B deploy to move ~3%); risk-free-yield
competition (4-5% T-bills shrink the income premium); the Apollo/private-capital pivot as a TELL (managing other
people's money because own balance-sheet equity is too dear — Apollo equity cost capped ~6.875% vs 7.1% buy yield =
~0.2% spread, razor-thin); retail-tenant credit risk beneath the pristine occupancy. Honest-nuance:
high-quality/conservative, investment-grade, safe growing monthly dividend, raised investment + AFFO guidance,
attractive 7.1% acquisition yields, spread could re-widen if rates fall ("hard assets shine").

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → sharpened → post commit)

CONFIRMED (Realty Income Q1 2026 8-K / supplement / earnings call / StockTitan, reported ~May 2026):
- **AFFO per share $1.13 (+6.6% YoY)** ✓; **FY2026 AFFO guide $4.41-4.44** (~3.0-3.7% per-share growth) ✓
- **Occupancy 98.9%** ✓
- **Invested ~$2.8B at 7.1% initial weighted-avg cash yield** ✓; **FY investment guide raised to $9.5B** ✓
- **Apollo $1.0B equity investment** into a JV of ~500 single-tenant retail properties, OFF balance sheet; **call
  option (years 7-15) capping Apollo's cost of equity at ~6.875%** ✓ — vs 7.1% buy yield = ~0.2% spread (added to
  article as a vivid illustration of spread compression)
- Issued $800M senior unsecured notes ✓; 2025 dividend per share +2.9% ✓

### Sharpening (pre-publish → post-commit)
- Added the Apollo JV specifics: ~500 single-tenant properties, off balance sheet, and the **6.875% capped cost of
  equity vs 7.1% acquisition yield ≈ 0.2% spread** — a concrete, verified illustration of the razor-thin financing
  arbitrage the whole thesis describes. Not a correction; an added verified fact that strengthens the argument.
