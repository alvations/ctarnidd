# Audit — Abbott's $21 billion Exact Sciences buyout cashed out a never-profitable growth story at $105

## Method

Forensic thesis (pivoted from the assigned angle). The assigned premise — standalone EXAS posting persistent GAAP losses with heavy stock comp while blood-based rivals like Guardant Shield threaten Cologuard — was substantially OBSOLETED by a corporate event: Abbott acquired Exact Sciences for $105/share cash (~$21B equity, ~$23B enterprise value), announced Nov 20, 2025, closed March 23, 2026, and delisted EXAS from the Nasdaq. The article therefore reframes the long-running bear case as an argument that was *foreclosed rather than falsified*: the company ended its public life having never produced a full-year GAAP profit, its growth genuinely real but its independent model never proven, and the structurally more convenient blood-test threat (Guardant Shield FDA-approved, now on Quest's national network) cresting at almost exactly the moment a strategic buyer stepped in. The take-out premium is read as the price of retiring an open question — and the cash exit ($105) sits below the ~$150 post-COVID 2021 peak. Frames used: adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings; denominator illusion; moat-vs-loophole; demonstration-vs-deployment; cyclical-priced-as-secular; single-product concentration; what-the-price-admits.

Because EXAS is delisted, the live badges lead with ABT (the acquirer that now contains the franchise), with peers GH (Guardant), DHR (Danaher), and ILMN (Illumina). The article flags the delisting explicitly.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (SEC 8-K / company press release / Abbott investor relations):
- Abbott acquisition: $105.00 per common share, cash; ~$21B equity value; ~$23B enterprise value. CONFIRMED.
- Announcement date: November 20, 2025. CONFIRMED (Abbott mediaroom).
- Close / delisting date: March 23, 2026. CONFIRMED (Abbott Q1 2026 release; Investing.com).
- EXAS FY2025 total revenue: $3.25B (~$3.247B), up ~18%. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025 Screening revenue: $2.53B, up ~20%. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025 Precision Oncology revenue: $717M. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025 net loss: ~$208M. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025 operating cash flow $491M, free cash flow $357M. CONFIRMED (used as context, not asserted as a loss).
- One-day pop on announcement: ~18%. CONFIRMED (TipRanks).
- 2021 post-COVID peak ~$150. CONFIRMED (secondary; characterized as "around $150," not a precise intraday claim).
- Guardant Shield: first FDA-approved blood-based primary CRC screen for average-risk adults 45+; available via Quest national network Q1 2026; CRC sensitivity ~83%, Stage I detection ~55-65%, misses majority of precancerous lesions. CONFIRMED (Targeted Oncology, MedTech Dive, Guardant IR, ACS 2026 guideline).

CORRECTIONS made during drafting:
- An initial search returned a spurious "$1.62 billion quarterly revenue, +12%, $180M adjusted EBITDA" figure that did not match primary sources (it conflated/hallucinated; EXAS full-year 2025 revenue was $3.25B). This figure was REJECTED and never used in the article.
- Stock-based compensation: a precise FY2025 SBC dollar figure was not located in primary sources, so the article discusses SBC qualitatively (the adjusted-vs-GAAP add-back mechanism) without asserting an unverified number.
