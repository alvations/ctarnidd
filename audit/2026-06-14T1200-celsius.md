# Audit — Celsius (2026-06-14T1200-celsius.md)

Title: "Celsius's Revenue Grew 138%, but Its Flagship Energy Drink Grew Just 6%." Batch 11, article 8 of 20 (target
2,900+; actual ~2,900). Source: beverages. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-14T1200-celsius/`.

## Method
Forensic thesis: CELH revenue +138% to $782.6M but the increase is overwhelmingly acquired (Alani Nu ~$368M +
Rockstar); the FLAGSHIP Celsius brand grew just 6%. Frames: bought-growth-masks-flagship-deceleration (the
hypergrowth lives in M&A, not the core); PepsiCo concentration (59% of revenue, 45.5% of receivables) that ALREADY
crashed the stock via a destocking episode; margin compression -400bps to 48.3% on the lower-margin acquired mix;
Rockstar -13% (acquired a declining brand); maturing/crowded category (Monster/Red Bull/Ghost); acquisition
treadmill (deals lap, true organic rate revealed); international-as-slow-grind vs global incumbents. Honest-nuance:
beloved brand, ~21% category share from a standing start, Alani Nu +100% (genuinely excellent deal), solidly
profitable (net income $110.1M, adj EBITDA $195.5M), powerful Pepsi distribution.

## Fact-check (research + verification → all confirmed)
CONFIRMED (CELH Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q / Investing.com slides / StockTitan / TIKR, reported ~May 2026):
- **Revenue $782.6M (+138%** from $329.3M) ✓; adj EPS $0.41 (beat $0.30) ✓
- **Alani Nu ~$368.1M of sales; flagship CELSIUS brand +6%** ✓; **Rockstar retail sales -13%** ✓; Alani Nu retail
  sales +100% ✓
- **PepsiCo = 59% of revenue, 45.5% of receivables** ✓ ("PepsiCo's energy category captain"); prior Pepsi-driven
  destocking crash is documented history ✓
- ~**20.9% US energy-category dollar share** ✓; portfolio = 45% of zero-sugar category growth
- **Gross margin 48.3% (from 52.3%, -400bps)** on lower-margin acquired mix ✓
- Net income $110.1M; adj EBITDA $195.5M ✓; acquisitions: Alani Nu (Apr 1 2025), Rockstar (Aug 28 2025) ✓

### Editorial care
- Flagship +6% is the load-bearing skeptic figure (company-disclosed organic split). Conceded repeatedly that the
  brand, category leadership, Alani Nu, and profitability are genuinely excellent. Pepsi concentration framed via
  the proven prior crash, not hypothetical. Acquisition treadmill + international framed as structural risks, not
  predictions. Margin compression is the company's own attribution. No exact P/E asserted (premium qualitative). No
  price target; no buy/sell. Not Apple-related.
