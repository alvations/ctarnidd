## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the long-foreseen CRE/office reckoning
has been postponed by "extend and pretend," not resolved — a ~$2T maturity wall (2025-27) is forcing maturity
defaults (can't refinance, not missed payments) onto thinly-capitalized regional banks (900+ at >300%
CRE/capital). The rate-cut "rescue" cures financing not occupancy; the office is the honest indicator you
can't fix with an accounting entry. Cousin of batch-2 "Mark to Myth." Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `d0e901e` → published; title "The Empty Floor" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Office CMBS delinquency record **12.34%** (Jan 2026, Trepp; worse than GFC peak; pushed by Worldwide Plaza
  $940M & One NY Plaza $835M; expected peak ~12-13%); up from **~1.6%** mid-2022.
- Office vacancy **18-22%** major markets; demand destruction viewed as **permanent** (hybrid/remote).
- ~**$5T** total CRE debt; ~**$2T** maturing across **2025-27**; >**$100B** CMBS office maturing 2026, >**half**
  expected not to pay off (vs >75% refinanced pre-pandemic).
- Regional banks bear it; **900+** banks at >**300%** CRE/capital; **~30%** of modified CMBS got maturity
  extensions 2023-24 ("extend and pretend").

Corrections at fact-check:
- Per-year maturity figures ($957B/$539B/$550B from one source) CONFLICT with S&P Global ($998B/$1.148T/
  $1.257T) → softened to "~$2T across 2025-27, annual maturities high-hundreds-of-billions into trillion-
  dollar range." Dropped precise $539B/$550B.
- ">half default" attribution to Morningstar DBRS not independently re-confirmed → softened to "rating-agency
  and industry analyses." Substance well-supported.
Doom-loop, rate-rescue, and wider-CRE sections are analysis built on the verified figures.
