## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Snowflake surged ~30%+ as a celebrated AI
winner, but 3 truths undercut the price: (1) still loses big (FY26 net loss $1.3B on $4.7B rev, a decade public;
GAAP loss largely SBC; non-GAAP "profit" softened by dilution + offsetting buybacks); (2) the consumption engine
is decelerating beneath the headline (NRR 170%+ → ~126%; consumption = a dial customers turn DOWN); (3) the AI
battle is being WON by Databricks (private, ~$165-175B = 2x+ Snowflake, FCF-positive, >$1B AI run-rate vs Cortex
~$100M, ~70% of incremental AI/ML budgets). Snowflake = warehouse/BI; Databricks = lakehouse/ML/AI, where AI
workloads flow. Picks-and-shovels priced like the gold. The Databricks IPO = the side-by-side reckoning. Honest-
nuance: real data-gravity moat, durable core, real re-accel, AI-on-your-data thesis plausible, coexistence.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

- Q1 FY2027 **product revenue $1.33B (+34%)**; total **$1.39B**; **NRR 126%**; stock surged ~30-36%. FY26 full-
  year **net loss $1.3B** on **$4.7B** revenue (quarterly loss narrowed to $295.6M — article cites the full-year
  figure). FY27 product guide raised to $5.84B (+31%).

SOURCED (analyst/industry, hedged "reportedly/something like"): Databricks **$165-175B** / FCF-positive / **>$1B**
AI run-rate / **~70%** AI-budget wins; Snowflake **Cortex ~$100M**; NRR **170%+ → 125-126%**.
