## Method

News-driven: the Paramount–WBD megadeal is a top media/M&A story. Original market-research piece, not
investment advice. Forensic thesis: the deal is not a streaming-future triumph but the consolidation of a
dying past — cord-cutting is killing the cable bundle; combining two melting ice cubes (WBD is itself a failed
2022 merger, latest in the cursed AOL-TW → AT&T → Discovery lineage) doesn't make ice; the streaming war is
already lost to Netflix; the library is the real asset but attention has moved to YouTube/TikTok; the Ellison
billionaires win the trophy IP while public shareholders/bondholders carry the declining-asset and leverage
risk; and media consolidation narrows informational/cultural power. Verified below.
