# Audit — AstraZeneca's 16% oncology engine is real — its 28% GAAP margin and a Beijing courtroom are the tax nobody prices

## Method

Forensic thesis: AstraZeneca (US-listed ADR: AZN) carries a flawless-execution oncology-growth premium that ignores three priced-as-zero tails. (1) Quality-of-earnings — reported (GAAP) EPS of $1.99 sits ~30% below core EPS of $2.58, and the reported operating margin (28%) runs seven points below the core operating margin (35%), a structural amortization/impairment gap baked into a deal-fed operating model. (2) A contested China business of $1.923bn growing only 2% at constant currency while its former country chief, Leon Wang, is under indictment on data, illegal-trade and medical-insurance-fraud charges, with the company having prepaid ~$3.5m in import-tax compensation and warning of further fines. (3) A publicized US pivot — $50bn US investment by 2030 plus a floated London-to-NYSE listing move — that reads as a tell about migrating policy/pricing/tariff risk. Layered on a patent cliff requiring 20+ launches to reach the $80bn-by-2030 ambition. Frames used: adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings, bought-growth-masks-organic-stall, denominator illusion, priced-for-perfection asymmetry, policy/subsidy dependence, customer/partner concentration, cyclical-priced-as-secular.

## Fact-check

Source: AstraZeneca Q1 2026 results, reported 28 April 2026 (Form 6-K / company IR / StockTitan / Investing.com / BusinessWire). All figures constant-exchange-rate (CER) unless noted.

CONFIRMED:
- Total revenue: $15,288m, +13% actual, +8% CER.
- Oncology revenue: $6,798m (~$6.8bn), 44% of total, +16% CER (+20% actual).
- Enhertu: $831m, +34% CER (Daiichi Sankyo alliance). Imfinzi: ~$1.7bn, +30% CER.
- Reported (GAAP) EPS: $1.99 (+8% CER). Core EPS: $2.58 (+5% CER). ~30% gap confirmed.
- Reported operating margin: 28%. Core operating margin: 35%. Seven-point gap confirmed.
- China revenue: $1,923m, +7% actual, +2% CER; ~13% of product revenue; within Emerging Markets $4,398m.
- Rare Disease revenue: ~$2.42bn.
- FY2026 guidance reconfirmed: mid-to-high single-digit revenue growth, low-double-digit core-EPS growth (CER).
- $80bn revenue ambition by 2030 reiterated; ~20+ new launches framing per company.
- US: $50bn US investment pledge by 2030 (Virginia plant + MD/MA/CA/IN/TX expansion); CEO Soriot considering London-to-NYSE primary-listing move; UK HQ expansion paused.
- China legal: former China chief Leon Wang detained Oct 2024, indicted (reported Feb 2026); charges include unlawful collection of personal information, illegal trade, medical insurance fraud; AstraZeneca prepaid ~$3.5m import-tax compensation, warned of possible further fines.

CORRECTIONS / SOFTENING applied:
- Penalty estimates vary by source ($3.5m prepaid import taxes; one outlet cited up to ~$8m potential penalty). Article cites only the confirmed $3.5m prepaid figure and the open-ended "could face additional fines" warning — no single hard fine asserted.
- Whether AstraZeneca the corporate entity (vs only individuals) was formally indicted is reported inconsistently across sources. Article labels corporate exposure as "contested and unresolved rather than adjudicated" and frames all charges as allegations/indictments, not convictions.
