## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: independent power producers
(CEG/VST/NRG/Talen) — the market's century-old defensive sector — have been re-rated from "bond proxies"
into AI growth stocks on a data-center demand forecast that PJM, the largest US grid operator, began cutting
in its January 2026 load report. The risk is multiple de-rating, not insolvency or blackouts. Verified
against the sources below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `f9cdbe4` → published)

Verified as drafted (title "Kilowatt Kings" set at draft and held):
- CEG mkt cap ~**$98B**, fwd P/E ~**26x**; largest US nuclear fleet, ~**90%** carbon-free; **5,650+ MW**
  hyperscaler PPAs; TMI/Crane restart for Microsoft; ~**1.1GW** Meta deal; 2026 adj op EPS guide **$11–12**;
  **20%+** base EPS growth through 2029.
- VST mkt cap ~**$52B**, fwd P/E ~**18x**, net debt ~**$19.6B**; nuclear via 2024 Energy Harbor; large gas
  fleet; Jan 2026 20-yr Meta PPAs ~**2,600 MW** across 3 PJM plants.
- PJM 2026 Load Forecast (posted **Jan 14, 2026**): summer-2027 peak cut ~4GW to **~160GW** (from ~164GW);
  summer-2028 cut 4.4GW (−2.6%); near-term (through ~2032) trimmed on **improved data-center vetting** + EV/
  economic updates. **Nuance honestly reflected in the article:** PJM REAFFIRMED big LONG-TERM growth — only
  the near term was cut. Article argues near-term is what present valuation rests on; long-term is the
  unfalsifiable wedge.
- On the revision: VST **−3.1%**, CEG **−2.3%**, NRG **−2%**.
- Morningstar: VST & NRG High Uncertainty, 1-star (overvalued). Goldman: US DC power ~2x by 2027, +165% by
  2030. SMR commercial deployment may slip past 2030.

Edit made post-draft: added explicit acknowledgment that PJM kept its long-term forecast while cutting the
near term (accuracy), which strengthens rather than weakens the thesis.
