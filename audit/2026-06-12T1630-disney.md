## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Disney's streaming-profit milestone is
real (streaming op income +88% to $582M, margin broke 10% at 10.6%) but largely REPLACES, not adds to, the far
larger/higher-margin LINEAR (cable-bundle) profits melting as cord-cutting drops pay-TV below 50% penetration.
The bundle was the greatest forced-subsidy business in media (100M households paid ~$10/mo for ESPN, most
subsidizing the few who watch); streaming margins are a fraction of peak linear + cancel-anytime → a managed
DOWNGRADE dressed as a turnaround. ESPN = crown jewel AND biggest cliff (forced-subsidy → direct DTC while
sports-rights rise; carriage blackouts eroding linear now). Disney also stopped reporting subscribers (cross-ref
Netflix). Honest-nuance: streaming profitability answered the existential question; PARKS are the real durable
engine (but cyclical/capex-heavy); best IP library; ESPN owns the most valuable content. Two-companies-one-ticker
valuation muddle.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Disney 8-K/SEC / Variety / StockTitan / Alphastreet):
- Q2 FY2026 revenue **$25.17B** (+7%); adj EPS **$1.57** (+8%); FY guide ~12% EPS, $8B+ buybacks.
- **Streaming op income +88% to $582M**; margin **10.6%** (first double-digit). Disney+/Hulu rev **+13% to
  $5.49B**. **Subscriber reporting halted.** Parks +7% (Q2 record), op income +5%.
- Pay-TV **<50%** penetration; ESPN DTC ("Unlimited") + carriage disputes; CFO no-spinoff stance (search 01 /
  industry).

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes the streaming turnaround is real/important and Disney is a great company; thesis is
  replacement-not-addition / mix, not failure. The ESPN "~20-30M direct vs ~100M bundle" figure is explicitly
  ILLUSTRATIVE/hedged ("say, twenty or thirty million"), not a disclosed number. Full honest-nuance section
  (parks crown jewel, IP, ESPN value, real profitability). Parks risks (cyclical, capex) stated as risks, not
  predictions. Subscriber-blackout framed as an industry-wide tell (cross-ref Netflix), not asserted as
  concealment of a specific bad number.

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
