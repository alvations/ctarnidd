## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: GE Aerospace is a superb razor-and-blades
business (sell engines ~at cost, earn decades of high-margin aftermarket on a ~$170B-backlog installed base) but
trades at ~40x earnings (~20%+ above its rich A&D peers, ~2x the market), pricing the aerospace super-cycle as if
cycles were abolished. Aviation is deeply cyclical (travel, orders, and even the aftermarket — parked planes need
no overhaul, cf. 2020). The growth you see DEPRESSES the profit you don't: margin fell 200bp to 21.8% because
low-margin new-engine (OE) deliveries (+43%) front-load cost for back-loaded servicing profit. Boeing dependency
(LEAP→737 MAX, GE9X→777X; cross-ref the Boeing piece). Pratt GTF troubles handed GE share (mean-reverts).
Story-stock premium from the GE breakup. Honest-nuance: aftermarket annuity genuinely elite, installed base/
aging-fleet/visibility real — deserves a premium, just maybe not 40x at a cycle peak.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (GE 8-K/SEC / MSN / Investing.com / TIKR / gurufocus):
- Q1 2026 orders **$23.0B** (+87%); revenue GAAP **$12.4B** (+25%), adj **$11.6B** (+29%); operating profit
  **$2.5B** (+18%); **operating margin 21.8% (−200bp)**; **services +39%**; **engine deliveries +43%**;
  commercial services backlog **~$170B**.
- **CENTRAL THESIS EXPLICITLY CONFIRMED**: margin pressure "driven by the mix of business, with rapid growth in
  lower-margin original equipment shipments offsetting strong services growth."
- Forward P/E ~**37-43x**, ~22.7% above A&D median (search 01). Article: "roughly 40 times" / ">20% above peers"
  — accurate, hedged.

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes the business is superb (one of the best in industrials); thesis is "great business,
  cyclical, priced for an abolished cycle" — price, not quality. Aftermarket conceded as "more defensive... but
  not recession-proof" (2020 parked-fleet example). Boeing dependency tied to the documented Boeing fragility
  (cross-ref). Pratt share-gain framed as competitor misfortune that mean-reverts, not asserted as permanent. P/E
  hedged. No claim GE fails or that a downturn is imminent ("may be years away").

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
