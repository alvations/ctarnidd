# Audit — Marriott trades at 31x forward earnings while its core US RevPAR grows just 4%

Article: `2026-06-20T1630-marriott.md` · Ticker: MAR · Sector: travel

## Method

Forensic thesis: Marriott is an asset-light fee-and-loyalty compounder priced as a secular grower (~31x forward earnings, above its own 10-year average and a premium to lodging peers) while its core organic metric — RevPAR — grows at a cyclical, GDP-like rate. Q1 2026 (reported May 6, 2026) showed worldwide RevPAR +4.2% constant-dollar, U.S. & Canada +4.0%, yet adjusted EPS grew 17%. The article argues that gap is manufactured downstream of the top line by net-unit growth (+4.5% net rooms), a one-time co-branded credit-card royalty step-up, and an aggressive levered buyback — i.e. bought/engineered growth on top of an ordinary, decelerating cyclical base (FY guidance RevPAR 2%–3%). Frames used: bought-growth-masks-organic-stall; cyclical-priced-as-secular; adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings; denominator illusion; priced-for-perfection asymmetry; moat-vs-loophole; commodity-price-taker; pipeline-as-deferred-promise; partner-concentration. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the asset-light model's genuinely superior returns, the durable pipeline/conversions, Bonvoy, and clean execution.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against company release / SEC 8-K / multiple secondary sources:
- Q1 2026 reported May 6, 2026.
- Adjusted diluted EPS $2.72, up 17% from $2.32 (Q1 2025); beat consensus (~$2.55–$2.58).
- GAAP diluted EPS $2.43 (~29c / ~11% wedge to adjusted).
- Total revenue $6.65B, +6% YoY (vs ~$6.59B est).
- Adjusted EBITDA $1.398B, +15% from $1.217B.
- Worldwide RevPAR +4.2% constant-dollar (6.0% actual dollars); U.S. & Canada +4.0%; international +4.6%.
- Net rooms +4.5% YoY; ~15,900 net rooms added in quarter (~7,500 international).
- Pipeline: over 4,100 properties / nearly 618,000 rooms; ~43% under construction; conversions >35% of signings, >40% of openings.
- Segments: U.S. leisure ~+5%, group ~+5%, business transient ~+2% (US&C) / +1% global; government travel soft.
- Buyback: 2.1M shares for ~$0.7B in Q1; plan to return over $4.4B in 2026 via repurchases + dividends.
- FY2026 guidance (raised): worldwide RevPAR 2%–3%; adjusted EBITDA $5.88B–$5.97B (+9%–11%); adjusted diluted EPS $11.38–$11.63 (+14%–16%).
- Valuation: price ~$391; forward P/E ~31x (trailing ~37x); above 10-yr average; EV/EBITDA ~23x.
- Leverage: ~$17B debt vs few hundred million cash; net debt ~$17B; debt/EBITDA ~3.5x (industry median ~2.4x).
- Q2 note: ~50% YoY RevPAR decline guided for Middle East against tough comp.
- Third-party fair value (GuruFocus GF Value) ~$299 vs ~$391 price (>30% above estimate) — labeled as an estimate, not gospel.

Corrections during drafting: none material. Contested/soft figures (forward P/E "~31x", fair-value gap) labeled as estimates or ranges. One secondary source (IndexBox) headlined "RevPAR up 8.4%" which conflicts with the company's 4.2% constant-dollar figure; the company/8-K figure (4.2%) was used and the outlier was discarded.
