# Audit — BNPL phantom debt (2026-06-12T1100-bnpl.md)

Title: "Buy Now, Pay Later Is Booming. So Is the Debt Nobody Can See." Method + fact-check; kept out of the
article. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T1100-bnpl/`. Batch 7, article 6 of 10 (target 2,900+
words; actual ~2,993). Source: Engadget/CNET (consumer-fintech).

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: BNPL (~$70B/yr, +20%/yr) is mostly NOT
reported to credit bureaus — "phantom debt" the financial system cannot measure. The reassuring sub-2% reported
delinquency rate is computed on incomplete data (a LendingTree survey: 41% of users missed a payment; ~63% carry
multiple BNPL loans at once). ~Two-thirds (some studies 78%) of originations go to subprime borrowers stacking
BNPL on credit cards already ~60-66% utilized; financing has drifted to groceries/DoorDash (consumer stress, not
convenience). Funding fragility (originate-to-distribute / securitization) + regulatory limbo compound it. The
risk is the *aggregate invisibility*, concentrated at the lower-quality end.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → CORRECTED → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Affirm 8-K / StockTitan / Investing.com / Richmond Fed / CFPB / Consumer Finance Monitor / Fox):
- Affirm FQ3 2026: GMV **$11.6B** (+35%); revenue **$1,039M** (+33%); net income **$102.9M**; GAAP profit; 30+
  delinq (ex-Peloton) ~2.8%; working WITH FICO. Klarna ticker **NYSE: KLAR**; stock **−~62% since IPO**.
- Industry: ~**$70B**, +**20%/yr**; subprime **~two-thirds / 78%** (2022); **62.7%** carry >1 BNPL loan; ~60-66%
  card utilization; **41%** missed-payment survey; most loans not bureau-reported.

### Correction (stale-data catch — the recurring trap)
- First draft framed Klarna's "net losses doubled to ~$99M" as the **current** tremor. Verification showed the
  **$99M loss was a year-ago figure**; in **Q1 2026 Klarna returned to a ~$1M profit on +44% revenue** while
  consumer credit losses still rose ~17%. **Rewrote** the Klarna section to current data — "credit losses
  climbing while the bottom line balances on a razor-thin ~$1M profit; stock −62% since IPO" — preserving the
  forensic point without the stale loss-doubling claim.
- Added the verified **62.7% multiple-loan** loan-stacking statistic.

### Editorial care
- Affirm explicitly distinguished as the high-quality, transparency-leaning operator ("Affirm is not the
  industry"); the thesis targets the system-wide invisibility, not any single name. BNPL conceded as a genuine,
  largely-benign product ("the product is fine; the invisibility is the problem"). Subprime % stated as a range
  (two-thirds / 60%+ / 78% across studies). The DoorDash "collateralized obligations" line attributed as a wry
  observation, not a formal product name.
