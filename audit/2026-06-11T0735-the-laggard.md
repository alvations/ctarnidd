## Method

News-driven: Apple's WWDC 2026 / Siri-on-Gemini reveal and stock drop. Original market-research piece, not
investment advice. Forensic thesis: the world's most valuable company, priced as the eternal leader at ~30x
earnings, is the AI laggard — renting Siri's intelligence from archrival Google's Gemini (~$1B/yr), unable to
buy its way out (labs taken, antitrust), delaying to ~2027; the healthy iPhone masks the gap, but if the AI
agent (not the device) becomes the platform Apple owns the part that matters less; and the $20B Google search
knot is being cut by antitrust as the AI dependence deepens. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `ac56f69` → published; title "The Laggard" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Market cap ~**$4.3T** ($4.30T, Jun 10 2026; 3rd most valuable); P/E ~**30** (fwd; TTM ~35).
- **iPhone +~20%** (Q2 FY26 +22% to $57B) — explicitly stated NOT stalled (honest nuance).
- WWDC 2026: Siri on **Google Gemini** (~**$1B/yr**, ~1.2T-param custom model) on **Nvidia Blackwell**; full
  availability **fall 2027**; "bridge" to Apple's own models by 2027-28. Stock fell **~2%** post-WWDC on the
  missing launch timeline (Gene Munster).
- **Google pays Apple ~$20B/yr** for default search; central **antitrust** target (Google appealing).
- Buy-vs-build: OpenAI–Microsoft, Anthropic–Amazon/Google, DeepMind–Google → no available frontier lab; a
  transformative Apple AI acquisition would face antitrust (cf. Adobe-Figma). Analysis on verified facts.

No figures cut; no rename; no corrections required. Nuance noted: a partial/beta Siri "later in 2026" vs full
availability fall 2027 — article's "into 2027" is accurate for full availability.
