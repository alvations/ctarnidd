## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: TSMC is the keystone beneath the
keystones — ~70% of foundry, >90% of leading-edge, ~90% of advanced production on one island near China —
and the market prices the magnificent business while rounding the unhedgeable Taiwan tail risk (~$10T
first-year cost) to zero; Arizona is a real but post-2028/decade-end escape hatch that doesn't cover present
exposure; the risk propagates through every chip-dependent megacap and index. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `58930f4` → published; title "The Single Point of Failure" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Market cap ~**$2T** ($2.119T, June 2026; world's 6th most valuable).
- Q1 2026 revenue ~**$36B** (+**35.1%** YoY), profit +**58%**; FY2026 growth forecast **>30%**; capex
  **$52–56B**; advanced nodes (7nm−) = **74%** of wafer revenue; HPC = **58%** of 2025 revenue.
- **>90%** leading-edge, ~**70%** foundry; ~**90%** of advanced chips on Taiwan; China **2027** watch.
- Bloomberg Economics: ~**$10.6T** first-year cost / ~**9.6%** of global GDP (extreme case, > COVID & 2008);
  milder GDP hits 0.5–1.5% to 5–10%.
- Silicon-shield / fab-destruction contingency and workforce-capture risk: sourced (NBC, Milwaukee Independent,
  AEI) and framed as analysis.
- Arizona: **$165B** / 12 projects; first fab **$514M** year-one profit; ≤$20B board injection; 2nm begins
  **~2028** but bulk of leading-edge stays in Taiwan **through end of decade**.

Correction from verification: Arizona timing tightened from "2nm volume ~2030" to "begin ~2028, bulk stays in
Taiwan through end of decade." US-export-control / mutual-dependence and index-contagion sections are
analysis built on the verified concentration facts.
