## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Broadcom, the "anti-Nvidia" custom-
silicon play, is sold as a diversification away from concentration risk but is itself one of the most
concentrated bets in the market (~3 customers); its backlog/RPO is fragile intentions not guaranteed revenue;
its custom XPUs still route through every TSMC/ASML/HBM chokepoint (escapes the margin, not the bottleneck);
and it's a debt-laden VMware roll-up trading at a premium to the larger, faster, more diversified Nvidia.
Pairs with batch-2 "Patient Zero" (Nvidia) and batch-3 #1/#2. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `9a387bc` → published; title "The Custom Job" held)

Verified as drafted:
- AI semi revenue Q1 FY26 **$8.4B** (+106%), Q2 **$10.8B** (+143%), Q3 guide ~**$16B** (+200%); Q2 consolidated
  **$22.2B** (record); market cap ~**$1.87T**.
- **$73B** AI backlog; RPO ~**$164.6B** (May 3, 2026); Hock Tan "line of sight to >**$100B** AI revenue in 2027."
- Custom XPUs for **Google TPU / Meta MTIA**; co-design with Meta & Google **through 2031**; **Anthropic**
  contract; 3-customer SAM **$60–90B** by FY2027; "any program slip … moves the entire AI growth line."
- **VMware** acquired ~**$69B** (late 2023), ~$6.8B rev at ~78% margins; acquisition debt; price-hike roll-up
  model (CA, Symantec, VMware).
- Valuation: forward P/E ~**28x** (range 24.5–31.9x across sources), a **premium to Nvidia's mid-20s** despite
  lower growth / more concentration — sourced to the AVGO-vs-NVDA comparison (28.66 vs 24.76).

The "escapes the margin not the chokepoints" and "ladder customers climb" sections are analysis built on the
verified supply-chain and concentration facts. No figures cut; no rename; no corrections at fact-check.
