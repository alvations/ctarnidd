## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: AMD is an excellent company priced as
the next Nvidia, but the Intel analogy that powers the bull case breaks at the CUDA software moat — a two-decade
ecosystem, not the silicon, is the barrier. The OpenAI 160M-share warrant at $0.01 (and an identical Meta
warrant) is vendor financing that confesses AMD cannot win marquee customers on product merit alone; the
dual-source role structurally caps AMD's margins below the monopolist's; and the >$100B commitment rests on
OpenAI's circular, capital-hungry balance sheet. Honest-nuance throughout (AMD genuinely gaining in inference;
20–25% of a vast market is a great business; warrants require a $600 stock to fully vest, i.e. customer/AMD
alignment) — the dispute is the price, not the company.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title "The Other Chip" held)

Verified:
- **Nvidia**: data-center revenue **$75.2B** in the quarter ended Apr 26, 2026 (+92%); ~**$194B** annual run;
  ~**86%** of DC GPU revenue (down from ~90% in 2024). CUDA (2006) ecosystem vs ROCm fragmentation.
- **AMD**: Instinct ~$7–8B 2026; DC GPU forecast +114% to ~$15B; accelerator share ~13% → projected 20–25% late
  2026; stock +114% on the year. MI400/MI450 on TSMC 3nm.
- **OpenAI warrant**: up to **160M shares at $0.01**, tranche-vested on Instinct purchases (first after 1 GW
  MI450), full vesting on **6 GW**, subject to **stock-price targets up to $600**. AMD mgmt: **>$100B revenue
  over four years**. **Meta holds an identical 160M @ $0.01 warrant** → combined ~20% of AMD.

### Corrections from first draft
1. Revenue framing "$90 billion" → AMD's own ">$100 billion over four years" (4 instances normalized).
2. Added the **Meta warrant** (same terms); dilution language corrected from "a tenth" to "up to a fifth."
3. Added fairness paragraph on the **$600 vesting hurdle** (≈3× deal-date price) so the dilution is not
   overstated as automatic — argument (vendor financing / second-place economics) preserved but balanced.

Framing calls: "the circle inside the circle" (OpenAI funding circularity) and the margin contrast (~70%+ NVDA
DC gross margin vs AMD price-competing second source) are analysis built on verified scale/share facts, flagged
as reasoning rather than asserted precise figures.
