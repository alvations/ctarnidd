# Audit — Rio Tinto's $25 billion EBITDA still rides one rock — and China is letting go

Article: `2026-06-20T1730-rio-tinto.md`
Subject: Rio Tinto (US-listed ADR: RIO) — diversified miner; FY2025 results reported 19 Feb 2026.

## Method

Forensic thesis: Rio Tinto is marketed as a diversified energy-transition house (copper, lithium via Arcadium, Simandou, aluminium) but remains, underneath, an iron-ore price-taker whose cash flows depend overwhelmingly on the seaborne iron-ore price and, through it, on Chinese steel demand. The forensic moves:

- **Commodity price-taker:** revenue/EBITDA growth came from price + cost-out + a strong copper year, not iron-ore volume (Pilbara shipments fell 1%). Margin expansion via unit-cost cuts, not pricing power.
- **Cyclical priced as secular:** copper and lithium are themselves price-takers; adding them swaps one uncontrolled price for another rather than creating pricing power. Copper EBITDA ($7.4bn) confirms copper is the secondary engine within a $25.4bn group.
- **Customer concentration:** seaborne iron ore is effectively a one-customer (China) market, and that customer is in structural decline (steel output -5% YoY Q1 2026; property investment -16.2% YoY Jan-May 2026).
- **Self-inflicted oversupply:** Simandou first ore (Dec 2025) adds ~20 Mt of new supply; consensus cites it as a driver of the expected price fall.
- **Dividend = output of the tape:** 60% payout is a fraction of a swinging earnings number, not a fixed dollar promise.
- **Quality of earnings:** clean accounting but cyclically-peaked, mean-reverting earnings.
- **Asymmetry:** near-term high-conviction downside (China downcycle, falling dividend) vs distant, slow upside (transition optionality).
- **Honest nuance:** lowest-cost producer cushions a falling price; copper has a real demand tailwind; Arcadium may be a shrewd counter-cyclical entry; Simandou is a multi-decade high-grade asset; balance sheet strong; grade-premium (two-track market) tailwind is genuine.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Rio Tinto FY2025 release, 19 Feb 2026, via SEC 6-K and company IR / Investing.com / Globe and Mail):
- Consolidated sales revenue **$57.6bn, +7%**.
- Underlying EBITDA **$25.4bn, +9%**.
- Underlying earnings **$10.9bn** (stable YoY).
- Net cash from operating activities **$16.8bn, +8%**.
- Taxes and government royalties **$10.4bn**.
- Ordinary dividend **$6.5bn at 60% payout**, tenth consecutive year at top of range.
- Copper production **+11% YoY**; copper EBITDA **$7.4bn (+114%)**; copper-equivalent production +8%; annual records for copper and bauxite.
- 5% real-terms unit-cost reduction; ~$650m annualised productivity gains.
- Iron-ore sales **342 Mt**; Pilbara shipments **326.2 Mt (100% basis), down 1%**.
- Simandou: first ore 2025, first WCS-port shipment Dec 2025, cargo in China Jan 2026; 2.3 Mt crushed ore produced 2025 (100% SimFer); 2026 Simandou sales guidance 5–10 Mt (100% basis); full-build ~20 Mt high-grade low-cost supply.
- 2026 Pilbara guidance 323–338 Mt (100% basis); total iron-ore guidance 343–366 Mt.
- Arcadium Lithium acquisition completed **6 Mar 2025 for $6.7bn**.

CONFIRMED (external macro):
- China steel production **-5% YoY Q1 2026 (~250 Mt)**.
- China apparent steel consumption (five major products) **-3.1% week-on-week early June 2026** (vs -0.5% prior week).
- China real-estate investment **-16.2% YoY Jan-May 2026**.
- Consensus iron-ore price **~$101/t (2025) → ~$94/t (2026), -7%**; analysts cite weak China steel demand AND Simandou launch as drivers.

CORRECTIONS / SOFTENING APPLIED:
- No exact iron-ore segment EBITDA figure was publicly confirmed, so the article uses the defensible framing "majority / lion's share" and "anchored by iron ore" rather than asserting a precise iron-ore EBITDA number. Copper's confirmed $7.4bn vs $25.4bn group total supports this without overstatement.
- Price/demand forward figures labelled as consensus/forecast, not facts.
