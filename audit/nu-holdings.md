# Audit — Nu Holdings clears $5B revenue and $871M profit — and its credit book is seasoning faster than the bulls admit

## Method

Forensic thesis: Nu Holdings (NYSE: NU) is priced as a secular compounder (mid-teens forward P/E on 40%-plus earnings growth, 30%-plus ROE) while its income statement is increasingly behaving like a fast-growing, mix-shifting consumer lender entering the seasoning phase of an aggressively expanded, increasingly unsecured loan book — in a Brazilian economy with a Selic policy rate still near 14%. The argument is one of asymmetry, not collapse: gross NIM is flattering while risk-adjusted NIM compresses; the leading delinquency indicator (15–90 day NPL) is rising faster than the lagging one (90+ NPL); the credit-loss allowance is building largely on growth and mix; and the unsecured-credit migration is a one-way ratchet that lifts revenue today and embeds cyclical loss-rate sensitivity tomorrow. The market is paying a secular multiple on a book that has never seen a full Brazilian downturn at this scale and mix.

Frames used: cyclical-priced-as-secular; denominator illusion (gross vs. risk-adjusted NIM); quality-of-earnings (allowance adequacy in a fast-growing book); leading-vs-lagging indicator; product-mix one-way ratchet; policy-rate dependence; priced-for-perfection asymmetry.
