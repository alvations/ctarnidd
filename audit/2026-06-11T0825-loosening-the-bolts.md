## Method

News-driven: Citigroup outperforming after a Trump endorsement, atop a broad bank-deregulation rally. Original
market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the +$600B bank rally is built on loosening the
post-2008 capital rules — the freed capital that juices returns IS the buffer that absorbs the next crisis
(same effect, two angles); the gains are private (buybacks, pay) while the tail losses are socialized
(too-big-to-fail); deregulation-precedes-crisis (S&L, 2008) is one of history's most reliable patterns; and
the shocks (CRE, private credit, oil, fiscal) are documented across the series. Honest nuance: the big banks
are genuinely strong today; the danger is cumulative/late-cycle, not imminent. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `3c716c8` → published; title "Loosening the Bolts" held)

Verified as drafted:
- 6 biggest US banks (**JPM, Citi, BofA, Wells, Goldman, Morgan Stanley**) **+$600B**; combined **$2.37T** from
  **$1.77T** (end-2024).
- Record **~$32-33B** Q1 2026 buybacks (Big Six >$110B in 2025); article uses "$33B" (within range).
- Rules eased: higher leverage allowed, **stress tests** relaxed, loan restrictions pulled back, **Basel III
  Endgame** softened; 2026 proposals cut largest banks' **CET1 ~5-6%** cumulatively (added at fact-check).
- **Citigroup**: stock outperformed after a Trump endorsement (+**14.3%** vs S&P +6.2% in 2026); **$30B**
  buyback (on a prior **$20B**).
- S&L (1980s-90s) and 2008 (Glass-Steagall repeal, light-touch derivatives/capital) as deregulation→crisis
  precedents: historical record.

No corrections; one enhancement (CET1 ~5-6%). Privatized-gains/socialized-losses and "which bolts" sections are
analysis built on the verified facts.
