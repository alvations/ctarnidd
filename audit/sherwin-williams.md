# Audit — Sherwin-Williams sells $5.67B of paint, admits no recovery is coming, and trades like one already arrived

Article: `2026-06-18T1700-sherwin-williams.md` · Ticker: SHW · Sector: materials

## Method

Forensic thesis: Sherwin-Williams' Q1 2026 (reported April 28, 2026) headline growth of 6.8% is bought-and-borrowed, not organic. The growth is driven by (1) the October 2025 Suvinil acquisition (high-teens contribution to Consumer Brands), (2) favorable FX translation, and (3) continued price increases — not by volume. Paint Stores Group same-store sales rose only 2.4%, attributable to price rather than gallons, while North American DIY demand declined. Management explicitly guided to "little to no recovery in most end markets this year." Yet the stock trades at a forward P/E in the mid-20s to low-30s, a premium to the chemicals-industry forward median (~20x), valuing a housing-levered cyclical at a trough as if it were a secular compounder. Frames deployed: bought-growth-masks-organic-stall, price-not-volume, cyclical-priced-as-secular, adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings, denominator illusion (margin expansion on falling input costs now reversing), priced-for-perfection asymmetry, demonstration-vs-deployment (store openings into a frozen market).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (via SEC 8-K earnings release, company IR, StockTitan, Investing.com, PRNewswire, Yahoo Finance):
- Q1 2026 reported April 28, 2026.
- Consolidated net sales $5.67B (specifically $5.667B), up 6.8% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin 49.1%, expanded 90 bps; 14th consecutive quarter of gross-margin expansion. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted diluted EPS $2.35 (beat consensus ~$2.27–$2.28). CONFIRMED.
- GAAP diluted EPS $2.15, up 7.5% from $2.00. CONFIRMED.
- Net income $534.7M, up 6.1%. CONFIRMED.
- Paint Stores Group sales +3.7% to $3.05B; same-store sales +2.4%. CONFIRMED.
- Suvinil acquisition closed October 2025; contributed high-teens growth to Consumer Brands; North American DIY demand declined. CONFIRMED.
- Performance Coatings Group sales +6.5%; adjusted segment profit up mid-single digits; margin roughly unchanged on higher SG&A (incentive comp) + FX. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance reaffirmed: GAAP diluted EPS $10.70–$11.10; adjusted diluted EPS $11.50–$11.90 (Valspar amortization wedge ~$0.80/share); net sales up low-to-mid single digit %. CONFIRMED.
- Management expects "little to no recovery in most end markets this year"; raised raw-material inflation outlook to low-to-mid single digits; flagged propylene. CONFIRMED.
- Plans to open 80–100 new stores in 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Forward P/E ~24.96 (GuruFocus, Jun 2, 2026) up to ~30.94 by other providers; premium to chemicals-industry forward median ~19.84; stock ~$293 early June 2026. CONFIRMED as a defensible range (article states "mid-to-high twenties, by some measures into the low thirties").

Corrections applied: none material. Valuation expressed as a range (25–31x) to reflect provider dispersion rather than a single point. Volume-vs-price split for PSG same-store sales is characterized as price-led — an inference supported by management's no-recovery guidance and the soft-volume environment, labeled as inference rather than a reported volume figure.
