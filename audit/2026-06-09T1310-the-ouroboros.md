## Method

Original market-research piece, not a published news report or investment advice. The Bloomberg seed
("OpenAI Filed Confidentially for IPO as Rivals Race to Market", 2026-06-08) was paywalled (403); the
story was reconstructed from non-paywalled coverage and independent research, then written as an
original piece with a different title and angle. Figures were checked against the sources below; where
they conflicted, the most authoritative figure was used; unverifiable claims were cut.

## Corrections (pre-fact-check draft `0cf7084` → published version)

- **Oracle deal:** the draft's vague "tens of billions buying GPUs to lease" was corrected to the headline **$300 billion five-year cloud deal** (the GPU-leasing/Abilene ~$40B is the sub-mechanic).
- **AMD warrants:** sharpened to the specific terms — **warrants for up to 160 million AMD shares (~10% of the company) at $0.01 each**, performance-based.
- **Revenue ramp added:** $2B (2023) → **$6B (2024)** → **$20B+ (2025)** → company-guided **$30B (2026)** and a **~$200B by 2030** target.
- **2028 operating loss added:** ~**$74 billion** (internal projections), strengthening the loss-trajectory paragraph.
- **Stargate site detail:** added that OpenAI ceded a Stargate site to Microsoft, supporting the "build → rent" pivot.
- **Motif/title:** retitled from the draft's "Spending Money That Doesn't Exist" to **"The Ouroboros"**, matching the circular-financing framing already in the press (American Prospect, "The AI Ouroboros").
