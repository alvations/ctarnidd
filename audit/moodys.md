# Audit — Moody's hits records at 31x earnings on a toll booth that still bends to the bond cycle

Article: `2026-06-21T1300-moodys.md` · Ticker: MCO · Sector: financials

## Method

Forensic thesis: Moody's is a genuinely elite franchise — half of the global ratings duopoly with S&P Global — but its premium, secular multiple (~31x trailing, ~25x forward) is being applied to a record that is, in critical part, the product of a cyclical wave. The argument runs across several forensic frames:

- **Cyclical-priced-as-secular / denominator illusion**: The record $2T+ rated issuance quarter is the denominator of the whole bull case, and it is driven by a refinancing wave, near-record investment-grade volumes, and a $100B+ AI-financing sugar high — none of which is a stable subscription annuity. MIS is a transaction business wearing a subscription valuation.
- **Mix-and-multiple**: The segment meant to justify the secular multiple (MA, ~45% of revenue) is the lower-margin (32.5% vs MIS 67%), slower-growing (8%) half. The cyclical arm did the heavy lifting this quarter.
- **Bought-growth-masks-organic-stall**: MA's $3.6B ARR base was assembled substantially by acquisition (Bureau van Dijk, RiskFirst, Reis, RDC, etc.), flattering the consolidated growth line and adding goodwill/intangibles.
- **Quality-of-earnings (adjusted vs GAAP)**: Adjusted diluted EPS $4.33 vs GAAP $3.73 — a ~16% wedge dominated by amortization of acquired intangibles, i.e. the recurring cost of the build-by-buying strategy.
- **Moat-vs-loophole / demonstration-vs-deployment**: The ratings moat (NRSRO, Basel) is regulatory and durable; the analytics moat is economic and exposed to generative AI that collapses the cost of producing analysis. AI-as-weapon is so far a demonstration, not proven durable pricing power.
- **Pro-cyclical capital return**: $1.7B returned and buyback raised to $2.5B at ~31x near a cycle high.
- **Guidance tell**: FY26 adjusted EPS guidance reaffirmed (not raised) after a record quarter — the implicit "don't extrapolate."

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the durable ratings moat, working diversification, elite cash conversion, and the credible proprietary-data AI rebuttal.

## Fact-check

All figures from Moody's Q1 2026 earnings release (reported April 22, 2026) and corroborating coverage. CONFIRMED:

- Q1 2026 revenue: **$2,079 million**, up **8%** YoY. CONFIRMED.
- MIS (ratings): record revenue **~$1.2 billion**; rated issuance **>$2 trillion** (first time in a quarter); MIS adjusted operating margin **67%**; AI-related financing **>$100 billion**; private credit-related revenue **+80%+** YoY. CONFIRMED.
- MA (analytics): revenue grew **8%**; ARR **$3.6 billion**; adjusted operating margin **32.5%**, up **250 bps**. CONFIRMED. (MA is ~45% of total revenue.)
- Adjusted diluted EPS: **$4.33**, up **13%**. GAAP diluted EPS: **$3.73**. CONFIRMED.
- Company-wide GAAP operating margin **44.3%**; adjusted operating margin **53.2%** (up 150 bps). CONFIRMED.
- Operating cash flow **$939M** (+24%); free cash flow **$844M** (+26%). CONFIRMED.
- Capital returns: **$1.7B** in Q1 ($1.5B buybacks + $185M dividends); buyback guidance raised to **~$2.5B** from $2.0B. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 adjusted diluted EPS guidance reaffirmed at **$16.40–$17.00**; high-single-digit revenue growth outlook. CONFIRMED.
- Valuation: P/E (TTM) **~31** (gurufocus 30.88 as of mid-May 2026; trailing ~31.08), forward P/E **~25.37**. CONFIRMED (labeled as approximate).
- Balance sheet: ~$2.45B cash, ~$7.44B debt (net debt position). CONFIRMED.
- Duopoly: Moody's ~**35–40%** of global ratings share, near-duopoly with S&P Global; ratings required by regulation/indentures. CONFIRMED.

Corrections made during drafting: none material. All numbers stated as approximate ("roughly," "about") where rounding from primary figures; the GAAP-vs-adjusted wedge stated as ~16% (4.33/3.73 − 1 = 16.1%).
