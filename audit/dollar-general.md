# Audit — Dollar General's margin recovery runs on shrink math, not a richer customer

## Method

Forensic thesis: Dollar General's Q1 fiscal 2026 (reported June 2, 2026) earnings beat and guidance raise are real but are quality-of-earnings driven. The operating-profit growth (+10.8%) vastly outran sales growth (+3.4%) and same-store growth (+2.0%) because the margin gain came overwhelmingly from finite, non-repeatable internal cost recoveries — lower shrink, lower inventory damages, better markups — not from a healthier customer. Frames deployed: quality-of-earnings, denominator illusion (10.8% profit on 2.0% comps), cyclical-priced-as-secular, bought-growth-masks-organic-stall (new-store vs same-store), commodity/tariff price-taker, moat-vs-logistics-erosion, demonstration-vs-deployment (can fix costs, cannot deploy a richer customer). The original assignment premise (margins squeezed, weak SSS) was partly outdated — DG beat and raised — so the thesis was PIVOTED to attack the *composition and durability* of the beat rather than denying it.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Q1 FY2026, reported 2026-06-02, businesswire / 8-K / multiple outlets):
- Net sales +3.4% to $10.79 billion — CONFIRMED
- Same-store sales +2.0%; traffic +1.4%; average transaction +0.5% — CONFIRMED
- Gross margin 31.6%, +65 bps — CONFIRMED
- Operating profit $638.5M, +10.8% (from $576.1M) — CONFIRMED
- Operating margin 5.9%, ~+40 bps via shrink mitigation / improved damages — CONFIRMED
- Diluted EPS $2.00 (+12.4%); net income $444.1M (+13.3%) — CONFIRMED
- FY2026 guidance: EPS raised to $7.20–$7.45 from $7.10–$7.35; net sales growth 3.7%–4.2%; SSS 2.2%–2.7%; capex $1.4B–$1.5B — CONFIRMED
- Prior-year context: FY2024 net sales +5.0% to $40.6B; SSS +1.4%; gross margin 29.6% (-70bps); SG&A 25.4% (+140bps) — CONFIRMED
- Year-ago quarter SG&A 25.8% vs 24.6% (+121 bps) — CONFIRMED
- Tariff commentary (reduce China exposure, price increases "as a last resort"), 27%+ operating cash flow rise, lower interest expense — CONFIRMED via call/coverage

CORRECTION applied: initial draft stated prior EPS guidance as "$7.10 to $7.45"; corrected to "$7.10 to $7.35" per businesswire/coverage.

All figures are company-reported, adjudicated facts (not allegations). Forward risks (tariff impact, shrink lapping, productivity) are framed as analysis, not asserted outcomes.
