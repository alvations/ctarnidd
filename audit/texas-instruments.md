# Audit — Texas Instruments trades near 49x as a 90%-growth data-center sliver reprices the whole cycle

## Method

The suggested angle (FCF-per-share collapse under fab capex + soft industrial/auto demand) was tested against the most recent quarter and found stale. As of Q1 2026, the 300mm capex cycle is winding DOWN (now ~83% complete; 2025 capex ~$5B, 2026 guided to roughly $2–3B), free cash flow is RECOVERING (TTM FCF $4.35B = 23.6% of revenue; >$8/share targeted for 2026), and the data-center line grew ~90% YoY. The premise was therefore PIVOTED.

The accurate forensic thesis used: **cyclical-priced-as-secular + denominator illusion + priced-for-perfection asymmetry.** Texas Instruments printed a genuine, beat-the-guide cyclical recovery (revenue +19% YoY) and has been re-rated to a trailing P/E near 49 — ~99% above its ~24.65 ten-year average. The re-rating is powered overwhelmingly by a data-center segment that is only ~11–12% of revenue, while automotive (a historically largest end-market) is flat-to-down with China softening into H2. The $8+ FCF/share figure is a 2026 forecast (partly underwritten by $630M CHIPS Act cash), not a printed result; even if hit, the stock trades ~36x it. Elevated 209 days of inventory cut two ways. Supporting frames: policy/subsidy dependence (CHIPS), moat-vs-already-priced (300mm cost edge real but telegraphed), peer-multiple convergence risk (ADI/NXPI/MCHP all far below 49x).

## Fact-check

- Q1 2026 revenue $4.83B, up 19% YoY and 9% sequentially, above guidance — CONFIRMED.
- EPS $1.68 (vs ~$1.36 consensus; included a 5-cent benefit not in original guidance) — CONFIRMED.
- Net income $1.55B, up 31% — CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin 58%, +210 bps sequential — CONFIRMED (per call/coverage).
- Analog revenue +22% YoY to $3.92B, operating margin 41.7% — CONFIRMED.
- Data-center segment ~+90% YoY; ~11–12% of total sales — CONFIRMED.
- Industrial ~+30% YoY — CONFIRMED.
- Automotive flat-to-slightly-down sequentially; China-driven softening flagged as H2 risk — CONFIRMED (call).
- Days of inventory 209, down 13 sequentially — CONFIRMED.
- TTM (to Mar 31, 2026) operating cash flow $7.82B, capex $4.10B, FCF $4.35B incl. $630M CHIPS incentives = 23.6% of revenue — CONFIRMED.
- 2025 FCF $3.23/share (+97% YoY); >$8/share targeted for 2026 — CONFIRMED.
- Capex cycle ~83% complete; 2026 capex guided ~$2–3B (down from ~$5B in 2025) — CONFIRMED.
- Q2 2026 guidance: revenue $5.0–$5.4B; EPS $1.77–$2.05 — CONFIRMED.
- Valuation: trailing P/E ~49 on ~$288 price / ~$5.87 EPS (as of early-mid May 2026); ~99% above ~24.65 ten-year mean; forward P/E ~35; stock up ~58% in 2026 — CONFIRMED per fullratio / stock trackers; softened with "roughly/near" qualifiers in-text as point-in-time.

CORRECTIONS: Suggested-angle premise (FCF collapse) reversed to FCF recovery; thesis re-centered on valuation/multiple risk rather than cash-flow distress. The insider-selling section is framed as a structural argument about marginal buyers/sellers at a re-rating, not an assertion of specific disclosed transactions.
