# Audit — MercadoLibre's revenue roared 49% in Q1 — so why did operating profit just fall 20%?

Article: `2026-06-21T1100-mercadolibre.md` · Ticker: MELI · Sector: e-commerce

## Method

Forensic thesis (bought-growth-vs-credit-risk + cyclical-priced-as-secular + denominator-illusion + quality-of-earnings): MercadoLibre's Q1 2026 headline of 49% revenue growth (its fastest since 2022) masks a 20% YoY decline in operating income and ~600bps of margin compression to 6.9%. The acceleration is substantially manufactured by a credit book that grew 87% YoY to $14.6bn — the fastest-growing, most cyclically exposed, capital-hungry part of the company. In lending, revenue is recognized immediately while losses are provisioned over time, so an 87%-growing book mechanically flatters present results and defers the reckoning. A "stable" 15-90 day NPL (~8%) on a near-doubled book reflects seasoning, not durable underwriting. Management itself attributed margin compression to a richer mix of lower-spread credit cards and "higher provisions in Brazil due to longer-duration loans and broader reach for personal loans," with NIMAL falling to 17.8% from 22.7%. Priced ~40-43x earnings as a secular compounder, MELI is an increasingly cyclical LatAm consumer lender exposed to Brazil/Argentina FX and rates. The mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the genuine marketplace moat (logistics, 84.1M buyers), the fintech flywheel (82.9M Mercado Pago users, $19.9bn AUM +77%), 149% provision coverage, and a 15-year underwriting track record.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported May 7, 2026; sources: company 8-K/IR via BusinessWire, StockTitan, Investing.com, Motley Fool transcript, Yahoo/Quiver):
- Net revenues & financial income: $8,845M (~$8.85bn), +49% YoY, +46% FX-neutral — fastest growth since Q2 2022. CONFIRMED.
- Income from operations: $611M, 6.9% margin, down ~20% YoY from $763M (Q1 2025 margin 12.9%); ~600bps compression. CONFIRMED.
- Net income: $417M, 4.7% margin (down from $494M / 8.3%; reported as a 16% YoY decline). CONFIRMED. (Article labels the 20% figure to operating income and the slip from $494M to net income — consistent.)
- Diluted EPS: $8.23, missed consensus by ~12%. CONFIRMED.
- Total credit portfolio: $14.6bn, +87% YoY (largest-ever nominal quarterly increase). CONFIRMED (company headline metric). NOTE: on-balance-sheet "loans receivable, net" was ~$10.7bn vs $9.365bn at YE2025 — a net-of-provision balance-sheet line, distinct from the $14.6bn total managed portfolio. Article uses the $14.6bn total portfolio figure, consistent with company reporting.
- Credit-card sub-portfolio: more than doubled, +104% YoY to ~$6.6bn; 2.7M cards issued in quarter; ~mid-40s % of portfolio (up from low-40s). CONFIRMED.
- NIMAL: 17.8%, down from 22.7% YoY (~4.9ppts). CONFIRMED.
- 15-90 day NPL: ~8.0%, broadly stable YoY; provision coverage 149% of 15+ day past-due. CONFIRMED via earnings call. (One secondary source labeled 17.6% as 15-90d and 8.0% as 90+; article softens to "in the area of 8%" and frames NPL as a ratio — defensible.)
- GMV $19.0bn (+42%); items sold 721.7M (+47%); TPV $87.2bn (+50%); unique active buyers 84.1M (+26%); Mercado Pago MAU 82.9M (+29%); AUM $19.9bn (+77%). CONFIRMED.
- Valuation: trailing P/E ~43, forward P/E ~31-40 (source-dependent), market cap ~$82-85bn, stock ~$1,631 (June 17, 2026). CONFIRMED. (Article uses "low-to-mid 40s trailing / low-40s forward" and "low-$80-billion" cap — within verified ranges; original ~50x premise softened to defensible range.)
- Brazil: management cited higher Brazil provisions, longer-duration loans, broader personal-loan reach. CONFIRMED. Argentina: 15-90d NPL fell sequentially while spreads widened despite rising sector delinquency. CONFIRMED.

Corrections applied vs. the suggested angle: the "priced ~50x" premise was softened to the verified ~40-43x trailing / ~31-40x forward range. NPL framing softened given source discrepancy. Net-income decline correctly characterized (operating income -20%, net income -16%).
