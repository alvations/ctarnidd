# Audit — Caterpillar near $975 at 42x earnings, pricing a data-center engine boom as if the machinery cycle had been repealed

Article: `2026-06-20T0830-caterpillar.md` · Ticker: CAT · Sector: industrials

## Method

The suggested angle (construction/mining/energy capex rolling over, retail-sales declines, falling backlog) was tested against the most recent quarter and found materially false: Q1 2026 revenue rose 22% and backlog hit a record $63B. The thesis was therefore PIVOTED to an accurate forensic frame: **cyclical-priced-as-secular**. Caterpillar, a late-cycle, price-taking heavy-machinery maker, trades near a record ~$975 at a trailing P/E above 42x — roughly double its decade-long mid-teens average — on growth concentrated in one demand source (data-center power generation). Forensic moves used: (1) one-segment concentration of the re-rating (Power & Energy / data center); (2) cyclical-earnings-vs-secular-multiple category error; (3) the dealer-inventory "tell" ($1.5B Construction channel build = borrowed growth); (4) margin compression despite 22% revenue growth (tariffs); (5) Resource Industries as the cyclical canary (profit -39%); (6) capacity-not-yet-built timing gap (2027–2029 deployment); (7) adjusted-vs-GAAP quality of earnings; (8) peak-denominator illusion; (9) priced-for-perfection asymmetry. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes real backlog, durable dealer/services moat, and strong operational execution.

## Fact-check

All figures sourced from Caterpillar's Q1 2026 8-K / earnings release (report date April 30, 2026) and June 2026 market-data sources. CONFIRMED:

- Q1 2026 revenue $17.4B, up 22% YoY (from $14.3B) — CONFIRMED (8-K, PRNewswire).
- Adjusted EPS $5.54 vs $4.25 prior-year Q1 — CONFIRMED.
- Operating profit margin 17.7% vs 18.1% prior year; adjusted 18.0% vs 18.3% — CONFIRMED.
- Record backlog $63B, +$28B / +79% YoY — CONFIRMED (all three segments contributed).
- Construction Industries segment profit $1.535B (~$1.54B), +50%; margin 21.4% vs 19.8%; sales +38% to $7.2B — CONFIRMED.
- Power & Energy segment profit $1.450B, +13%; margin 20.6% vs 22.3%; retail sales +32%, power generation +48% — CONFIRMED.
- Resource Industries segment profit $378M, -39%; margin 10.0% (-700 bps); sales +4% to $3.8B — CONFIRMED.
- Construction dealer inventory build ~$1.5B in Q1 2026 vs slight decrease prior year — CONFIRMED.
- Q1 tariff cost ~$600M (vs internal ~$800M estimate); full-year tariff guide $2.2–$2.4B (revised down from $2.6B) — CONFIRMED.
- Full-year revenue guidance raised to low double-digit growth — CONFIRMED.
- Large reciprocating-engine capacity target ~3x 2024 levels, deployment 2027–2029 — CONFIRMED.
- Stock all-time high $975.64 on June 17, 2026; prior close ~$956; ~+32% YTD; trailing P/E >42x, forward ~34.5x; market cap ~$430B; TTM revenue $67.6B; TTM net income ~$8.9B; TTM diluted EPS $18.81 — CONFIRMED (June 2026 market-data sources).
