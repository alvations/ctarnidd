## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: digital-asset treasury companies
(DATs) are a population of ~190-200 copycats running the same reflexive mNAV flywheel (premium → issue stock
→ buy coins → accretive bitcoin-per-share), which runs in reverse below mNAV 1.0 — shutting off issuance and
pushing leveraged copies toward forced selling, a correlated cascade. They are leveraged closed-end funds
whose century of history says the premium never lasts. Extends batch-1 "The Forced Seller" (MSTR) to the whole
ecosystem. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `7c51d7e` → published; title "The Tower of Babel" held)

Verified as drafted:
- mNAV = multiple on NAV (EV / crypto NAV); >1.0 enables accretive issuance, <1.0 reverses it. Peak mNAVs
  **once exceeded 25**, now converging toward and below **1.0**; most DATs in a band ~**0.8–1.7** (added at
  fact-check), only a handful above NAV.
- Population **~190–195** firms (trackers vary 173–195) — softened from a hard "195" to "190 to 195 / nearly
  two hundred" at fact-check.
- Strategy spawned **89 imitators**; MSTR holds **845,256 BTC** (largest).
- **Metaplanet** swung from a large premium (cited **+237%**) to a **discount** (cited **−10%**); dipped below
  1.0 when it paused buys, recently ~1.11–1.21x. Directional reversal strongly confirmed; exact 237%/10% from
  ainvest (one source), retained as sourced + directionally verified.
- **Standard Chartered** warned of mNAV collapse/consolidation; Breed VC warned of a "death spiral."
- Spot bitcoin/ether ETFs now provide direct, cheap crypto access (undercutting the DAT "access" rationale) —
  established fact.

Corrections at fact-check: company count softened (190–195 / "nearly two hundred"); added the ~0.8–1.7 mNAV
band. Closed-end-fund-premium-history and the "access/leverage/flywheel pillars" sections are analysis.
