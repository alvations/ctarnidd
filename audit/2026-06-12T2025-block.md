# Audit — Block (2026-06-12T2025-block.md)

Title: "Block Reports Record Profit Margins and a $309 Million Loss in the Same Quarter." Method + fact-check;
kept out of the article. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T2025-block/`. Batch 9, article 7 of 10
(target 2,900+ words; actual ~2,934). Source: WSB.

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Block leads with adjusted metrics (adj OI
+56% to record $728M, record 25% margin, adj EPS $0.85 +52%) painting a profitable fintech — while GAAP shows a
$172M operating loss and $309M net loss in the SAME quarter. The ~$900M bridge = $852M "restructuring and other"
charges + $173M bitcoin remeasurement loss. Quality-of-earnings / adjusted-vs-real frame (same family as the
DoorDash piece, more extreme: "record-profitable AND loss-making"). Stacked frames: (1) large "one-time" charges
that warrant scrutiny re recurrence; (2) bitcoin treasury bolts founder-driven price volatility onto the income
statement (capital-allocation distraction); (3) two-engine divergence — Cash App GP +38% carries it, Square
decelerates to +9%; (4) SBC adjustment hides dilution behind adjusted EPS; (5) narrative/conglomerate valuation
risk. Honest-nuance: Cash App is a genuine, high-reach consumer-finance franchise; real operating leverage;
strong 2026 guidance (adj EPS +62%); restructuring may genuinely be one-time; bitcoin is a real asset.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Block Q1 2026 8-K/10-Q via SEC + StockTitan + The Block + AMBCrypto, reported May 7/8 2026):
- Gross profit **$2.91B (+27%)**; **Cash App GP +38%**, **Square GP +9%** ✓
- Adj operating income **$728M (+56%), record 25% margin** ✓; **Adj diluted EPS $0.85 (+52%)** ✓
- **GAAP $172M operating loss; $309M net loss** ✓ — the load-bearing contradiction, verified
- **$852M "restructuring and other" charges** ✓ — tied to a Feb 2026 workforce reduction (>10,000 → ~6,000, a
  40%+ cut). **$173M bitcoin remeasurement loss** ✓ (nearly double the $93M a year earlier)
- Bitcoin holdings (corporate + customer) **28,355 BTC ~$2.2B**; bitcoin revenue **down 31% YoY** ✓
- 2026 guidance: GP **$12.33B (+19%)**, adj OI **$3.34B (27% margin)**, adj EPS **$3.85 (+62%)** ✓

### Note (no correction)
- The $852M charge being a single Feb 40%+ layoff cuts in BOTH directions and the article already concedes it:
  it supports the "genuinely one-time" honest-nuance reading (real, discrete event), while the article's broader
  point — that charges this large turn the adjusted-vs-GAAP gap into the central risk — stands regardless. No
  fabricated claim that the charges ARE recurring; the article poses it as the question an investor must ask.
- "tens of thousands of coins worth billions" = exact 28,355 BTC / ~$2.2B; article rounds conservatively, fine.

### Editorial care
- Adjusted figures presented as Block's real, impressive operating results, not dismissed; thesis = the GAP and
  the price's reliance on the adjusted side, not that the business is bad. Bitcoin framed as founder conviction
  (Dorsey) that may prove visionary, with volatility as the documented mechanical effect on earnings — not a
  prediction of bitcoin's price. SBC/dilution point framed as standard practice, not malfeasance. Full
  honest-nuance section. No price target; no buy/sell language.

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
