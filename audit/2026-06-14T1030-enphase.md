## Method
Forensic thesis: ENPH is a high-quality microinverter leader but in a policy-driven downturn — Q1 2026 revenue
-21% to $282.9M, adj EPS -31%, IQ Battery MWh -39%, GAAP gross margin 35.5% (depressed by a loss selling $235M of
45X manufacturing tax credits; underlying non-GAAP margin 43.9%), GAAP net loss $7.4M. Double policy dependence:
DEMAND on the residential solar tax credit (expired) + MARGINS on IRA Advanced Manufacturing Production Tax Credits
(value falling) — both being repealed by the current administration/Congress. Frames: earnings-built-on-policy-
credits-politics-can-revoke; demand cliff; margin distortion via unsellable credits; channel-inventory bloat (more
destocking, Q2 guide $280-310M = continued trough); interest-rate wall on financing; SolarEdge cautionary collapse;
subsidy-fueled boom now bust (cyclical/policy priced as secular). Honest-nuance: best-in-class tech, net-cash
balance sheet (survives + consolidates), $83M FCF even in the trough, healthy 43.9% non-GAAP margin, EU
diversification, reshored manufacturing, real long-run solar growth.
