## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Hims built a high-margin GLP-1 business
inside the FDA-shortage compounding provision (legal copies of Wegovy/Zepbound). The shortage ended → mass-
compounding became illegal; the "personalization loophole" (adding B12) failed; HHS referred Hims to the DOJ;
Novo sued for patent infringement. The forced pivot to reselling BRANDED GLP-1s is low-margin, and the numbers
prove it (revenue +4%, gross margin 73%→65%, net income +$49.5M → −$92.1M). A regulatory ARBITRAGE mistaken for
a moat. Honest-nuance: genuinely diversified platform (most 2025 revenue non-GLP-1), 2.6M subs, FY guide raised
$2.8-3.0B, branded partnerships + peptide optionality — platform survives, the high-margin engine doesn't. The
title says "FDA"; the precise referral actor was HHS (clarified in the body).

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Hims 8-K/10-Q / Yahoo / Grafa / Fierce / CNBC / Healthline / DrugDiscoveryTrends):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$608.1M** (+4%); gross margin **65%** (from 73%); net loss **$92.1M** vs +$49.5M y/y;
  **$33.5M** restructuring (+$15M legal settlement). **2.6M** subscribers; FY guide **$2.8-3.0B**.
- Stock −**29.2%** after the lawsuit + DOJ referral.
- Loophole mechanics (FDA shortage → 503A/503B compounding → shortage ended → illegal); "personalization"/
  significant-difference rule. Shortage resolved; branded pivot via Novo/Lilly.

### Corrections (precision)
1. DOJ referral attributed in the draft to "the FDA" → corrected to **HHS** (the Dept of Health and Human
   Services' general counsel, Feb 6 announcement). Title retains "FDA" as the broad regulator shorthand; body is
   precise.
2. Specified Novo's suit as **patent infringement** (Feb 9); added the coexistence of Novo litigation + the
   Novo/Lilly branded-resale collaboration (reinforces the legal-overhang section, fairly noted as "awkward").

### Editorial care
- DOJ referral and Novo suit explicitly labeled **allegations/processes, not verdicts** ("a DOJ referral is not
  a conviction... Hims is entitled to defend itself and may well prevail"). Diversification and bull case given a
  full honest-nuance section. The patient-safety rationale presented as the regulators' stated concern.
