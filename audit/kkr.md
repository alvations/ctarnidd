# Audit — KKR posts record $758B AUM and $1.13 fee earnings — but $0.41 GAAP profit tells the real story

Article: `2026-06-21T1200-kkr.md` · Ticker: KKR · Sector: financials · Quarter: Q1 2026 (reported May 5, 2026)

## Method

Forensic thesis: KKR is increasingly valued on durable, capital-light fee-related earnings (FRE) while the economically meaningful payoffs — carried interest realizations and insurance investment income — are frozen in a slow-exit, mark-to-model environment the firm cannot control. The piece contrasts the two parallel income statements (adjusted vs. GAAP), foregrounds the gap between $1.47 total operating earnings per share and $0.41 GAAP basic EPS, and examines four pressure points: (1) realized carry ($197.2M) dwarfed by unrealized carry ($10.2B) in a stalled exit market; (2) a denominator-illusion mix shift toward lower-carry credit/insurance AUM; (3) the Global Atlantic insurance balance sheet as a leveraged, rate/credit-sensitive book whose $652.7M net investment-related losses drove the adjusted-vs-GAAP gap; (4) mark-to-model valuation circularity when realizations (the external price-discovery mechanism) go quiet. Frames used: adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings, denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular, mark-to-model, priced-for-perfection asymmetry. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes durable FRE, perpetual capital, cyclical (not structural) realizations drought, the genuine insurance flywheel, and candid management guidance.
