## Method

News-driven: Lennar's Q2 earnings landed June 11; mortgage demand +11% and the Berkshire–Taylor Morrison deal
made housing a top story. Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the mortgage
"lock-in" has frozen the housing market into a paradox (low volume + record prices + worst affordability since
the early 1980s); homebuilders are the only open door but buy volume with margin (rate buydowns) — a generational
fracture and a labor-mobility drag — while the unlock rate is set by the bond market (term premium, oil,
inflation), tying back to the series. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `a34eac3` → published; title "The Lock-In" held)

Verified as drafted:
- **80.3%** of mortgaged US homeowners <6% (85% <5%) — lock-in. $350K 2021 @3% ≈ **$1,475**/mo vs @6.75% ≈
  **$2,790** (illustration). Existing-home sales ~**4M** pace; resale inventory suppressed; "frozen, not broken."
- Affordability worst since the **early 1980s** (NAR HAI ~90-95; era of ~18% rates).
- Builders: rate buydowns + price cuts (64% incentives / 37% cuts; 32% cut / 61% incentive in May). Lennar Q2
  reported **June 11**; EPS est **$1.23** (−**35%** from $1.90); deliveries 20-21K; missed 4 straight quarters;
  **LEN −47% / DHI −27%** from highs; Buffett owns LEN/DHI.
- **Berkshire–Taylor Morrison**: **$8.5B** all-cash, $72.50/share (24% premium), ~June 2026.

CORRECTIONS at fact-check:
- Mortgage rates dipped below 6% (first time in 3+ years) in Feb 2026; Fannie forecasts ~6.3% → changed
  "near 6.75-7%" to "briefly dipped just below 6% then settled into the low-to-mid 6s"; "~7%" → "6%-plus/mid-6%";
  kept the $2,790 figure explicitly "at, say, 6.75%."
- Lennar margins: "operating margin 9.1%→5.1%" → "roughly halved YoY to ~5%, gross into the mid-teens" (Q1'26
  gross 15.2%, net-on-home-sales 5.3%); added EPS $1.23 vs $1.90.
- Added Berkshire–Taylor Morrison "$8.5 billion."
