## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the alt managers (Blackstone, Apollo, Blue
Owl, KKR, Ares) extended once-institutional, illiquid private credit to retail/mass-affluent investors via
"semi-liquid" evergreen funds (non-traded BDCs / interval funds) promising QUARTERLY redemptions on loans that
mature in 3–7 years — a structural maturity/liquidity mismatch (same mechanic as open-ended property funds and,
in the limit, a bank run). Q1 2026 is the live stress test. Frames stacked: (1) the maturity mismatch (liquidity
is borrowed from "not everyone redeems at once"); (2) manager-set NAV marks — investors redeem at a price the
manager computes; smooth/low-vol returns partly a marking artifact, not true stability; (3) "the gate IS the
product" — the 5% quarterly limit is the load-bearing feature, so liquidity was always conditional; (4) even the
flagship (BCRED) felt it AND needed a sponsor capital injection to meet redemptions; (5) AUM-fee incentive drove
retail distribution (institutional channel tapped out); (6) coupled credit+liquidity risk (BDC ~16.7% software
concentration; default fears). Honest-nuance: legitimate valuable asset class; sophisticated well-capitalized
managers; BCRED met 100%; gates genuinely protect patient holders from fire-sales; loans mostly perform; system
did NOT break — resilience as much as fragility. NOT a collapse prediction.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; one strengthening detail added pre-post-commit)

CONFIRMED (CNBC + FSB "Vulnerabilities in Private Credit" 6 May 2026 + CAIA + Markets Group + Fortune + US Bank):
- Loan maturities **3–7 years**; semi-liquid funds offer **quarterly** redemptions — the core mismatch ✓
- Q1 2026 non-traded BDC redemption requests **~9–10% of NAV** vs **5% limits** ✓
- **Blackstone BCRED ~$82B**; investors sought **record 7.9% / ~$3.8B**; Blackstone met 100% — by **injecting
  ~$400M of its own + senior executives' capital** (verified, added to article) ✓
- **Blue Owl**: some vehicles saw redemption requests **40.7%** (tech-focused) / **21.9%** (credit income) ✓; Blue
  Owl drew ~40% of its >$300B AUM from individuals (retail-heavy) ✓
- **BlackRock restricted withdrawals on its ~$26B HPS lending fund** after requests ~**9.3%** ✓
- Listed BDC sector **~16.7% software exposure (22.0% weighted avg)** ✓
- FSB flagged private-credit vulnerabilities (regulator attention) ✓; Fortune framed a "$265B private credit"
  stress episode ✓

### Strengthening detail added (pre-publish → post-commit)
- The BCRED "met 100%" claim was sharpened with the verified mechanism: Blackstone injected ~$400M of its own and
  executives' capital to do so — which the article uses to make the point that a genuinely liquid fund wouldn't
  need sponsor house money. Not a correction of an error; an added verified fact that strengthens the thesis.

### Editorial care
- Explicitly NOT a collapse/fraud prediction: repeated concessions that the asset class is legitimate, managers
  sophisticated, gates protective, BCRED honored in full, loans mostly performing, "the system did not break."
  Marks-conflict framed structurally (incentive + discretion), not as proven misvaluation. Blue Owl 40%+ attributed
  to "some vehicles" (matches 40.7% tech-vehicle figure), not the whole firm. No claim any specific fund is
  insolvent. No price target; no buy/sell language. Tickers (BX/APO/OWL/ARCC) are listed proxies; BCRED itself is
  non-traded.

No remaining corrections to load-bearing facts.
