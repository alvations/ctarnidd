## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Tesla's ~$1.5T valuation at ~190–400x
earnings is almost entirely an "autonomy premium" — but the car business it sits on is shrinking, and on the
only robotaxi scoreboard that exists, Waymo (3,067 cars, 500k+ paid rides/week, 10 cities) is lapping Tesla
(~20 cars, shrinking). Bull case (camera-only fleet-scaling, "every Tesla a robotaxi," margin recovery) is
stated fairly, then weighed against decade-old unfulfilled FSD promises, sub-scale unit economics, and
winner-take-all/commoditization risk. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `29ccbdf` → published; title "The Autonomy Premium" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Tesla market cap ~**$1.5T** ($1.57T); trailing P/E ~**407**, forward P/E ~**195** (article: "past 300 …
  near 190" — consistent). Priced as AI/robotics, not a carmaker.
- Deliveries **fell to ~1.64M from ~1.79M** (≈ **8%**), second straight annual decline from the 2023 peak
  (1.81M). [Corrected from an initial "7.7%" to the verified ~1.64M/1.79M figures.] Op margin 10.8%→5.8%,
  gross margin recovery ~21.1%.
- Waymo: **3,067 robotaxis** (5th-gen, NHTSA Dec 2025); **500,000+ paid rides/week**; **10 US cities**;
  $16B raise at $126B.
- Tesla unsupervised fleet ~**20** (Austin, shrinking); Dallas **3**, Houston **6** (zero growth since April);
  whole Austin metro (~245 sq mi) June 3 2026; unsupervised launches Austin Jan 22, Dallas/Houston Apr 18.
- Pricing: Tesla **$8.17**/ride SF vs Lyft **$15.47**; Tesla waits ~**15 min** vs Waymo **5.7 min**.
- Bull case (fleet-scaling, camera-only) presented as Tesla's argument and clearly flagged as unproven at scale.
