# Audit — Medtronic's "Best Growth in a Decade" Was Mostly Currency, and Constant-Currency EPS Fell 2%

Article: `2026-06-21T0830-medtronic.md` · Ticker: MDT · Sector: healthcare

## Method

Forensic thesis: Medtronic's June 3, 2026 fiscal-2026 results were headlined as "highest annual revenue growth in 10 years" (+8.4% reported revenue to $36.364B), but the strength is substantially manufactured by items management asks investors to look past:

1. **Currency did the heavy lifting** — reported revenue growth of 8.4% falls to 5.8% organic once ~$824M of favorable FX is removed; Q4's 9.9% reported falls to 6.6% organic.
2. **Constant-currency earnings shrank** — adjusted diluted EPS of $5.53 grew just 0.7%, and on a constant-currency basis the company's own disclosure says it *decreased 2.0%*.
3. **GAAP-to-adjusted wedge** — GAAP diluted EPS was $3.73 vs $5.53 adjusted, a $1.80 (~one-third) gap; GAAP operating margin 17.8% vs adjusted 24.4%, and adjusted op margin fell ~130 bps YoY.
4. **The spin that didn't subtract** — MiniMed (diabetes) IPO'd ~10% on March 9, 2026; Medtronic still owns ~90.03% and consolidates the unit's ~7.9% organic growth into the very rate the spin was meant to flatter.
5. **Segment divergence** — only Cardiovascular (9.3% organic) is robust; Neuroscience (3.1%) and Medical Surgical (2.9%), ~half the company, grow near GDP.
6. **Denominator** — ~1.288B diluted shares cap per-share progress; buyback can't manufacture EPS growth against flat constant-currency operating earnings.
7. **Valuation asymmetry** — ~$79/share, ~$102B cap, forward P/E ~12; FY27 guide of 6.75–7.25% organic leans on ~125 bps from an extra 53rd selling week.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (primary: Medtronic IR press release Jun 3, 2026 + SEC 8-K exhibit 99.1):
- FY26 revenue $36.364B, +8.4% reported, +5.8% organic. CONFIRMED.
- FY26 FX benefit ~$824M (Medtronic IR page); a separate INN/wire summary cited $819M — same range; article uses $824M from the IR page. Softened to "roughly" / "$824 million." Both defensible.
- Q4 revenue $9.807B, +9.9% reported, +6.6% organic; Q4 FX +$308M. CONFIRMED.
- FY26 GAAP net income $4.801B, GAAP diluted EPS $3.73 (+3.3%). CONFIRMED.
- FY26 non-GAAP net income $7.120B, non-GAAP diluted EPS $5.53 (+0.7%); ~$0.15 FX benefit; constant-currency adjusted EPS **−2.0%**. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP operating margin 17.8%; adjusted operating margin 24.4% (−130 bps YoY). CONFIRMED.
- Segments (FY26 revenue / organic): Cardiovascular $13.976B / 9.3%; Neuroscience $10.287B / 3.1%; Medical Surgical $8.815B / 2.9%; Diabetes $3.112B / 7.9%. CONFIRMED.
- Operating cash flow $7.330B (+4.1%); free cash flow $5.426B (+4.6%). CONFIRMED.
- Diluted weighted-avg shares 1,288.1M. CONFIRMED.
- Dividend: $0.72/qtr, $2.88 annualized, 49th consecutive annual increase. CONFIRMED.
- MiniMed IPO completed March 9, 2026, ~$538M raised, Nasdaq; Medtronic retains ~90.03%. CONFIRMED.
- FY27 guidance: organic revenue 6.75–7.25% (incl. ~125 bps extra selling week, ~25 bps diabetes); adjusted EPS $5.90–$6.00. CONFIRMED.
- Valuation: ~$79.10/share, ~$101.9B market cap, trailing P/E ~21, forward P/E ~12.2, dividend yield ~3.5% (early June 2026). CONFIRMED via stockanalysis/gurufocus.
