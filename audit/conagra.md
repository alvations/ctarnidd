# Audit — Conagra's Earnings Fell 23% as a Held Dividend Strains a $7.3 Billion Balance Sheet

Article: `2026-06-18T1200-conagra.md` · Ticker: CAG · Sector: consumer-staples

## Method

Forensic thesis: Conagra's Q3 FY2026 "return to growth" headline (organic net sales +2.4%) masks a deteriorating reality — total net sales fell 1.9%, adjusted EPS fell 23.5%, adjusted operating margin collapsed 213bps, adjusted EBITDA fell 14.9%, and full-year guidance was cut to the low end of its range. The 2.4% organic growth was driven by price/mix (+1.9pts) over volume (+0.5pts), the signature of exhausted pricing elasticity in a commodity price-taker. The central tension: management is holding a $1.40 annual dividend (~82% of forward adjusted-EPS guidance of ~$1.70, higher on a trailing basis) against falling earnings and $7.3B net debt at 3.83x leverage — a payout the market's double-digit yield is pricing as at-risk.

Forensic frames deployed: bought-growth/divestiture denominator illusion; cyclical-priced-as-secular; commodity-price-taker; quality-of-earnings (adjusted-worse-than-GAAP inversion); promotional-spend tell; demand erosion (stretched consumer + GLP-1); dividend-coverage arithmetic.
