## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Built from Circle Q1 2026 results coverage, IPO
history, the Coinbase distribution disclosures, and GENIUS Act commentary. Forensic thesis: Circle is a
money-market fund (94% reserve income) priced like a software firm (~7x sales), with three interlocking
exposures it does not control — Fed rates (revenue), Coinbase (cost, ~54c/$), and the GENIUS Act
(statutory protection of its zero-interest model). Verified against the sources below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `9196301` → published)

Verified as drafted (no figures cut, no rename — title "Other People's Money" set at draft and held):
- IPO priced **$31** (6/4/25), listed 6/5/25, valued ~$6.2B; first-day close **$83.23** (+168%); intraday
  high $103.75; all-time high **$298.99** on **6/23/25**; now ~**$79**, down ~75% from peak; mkt cap ~**$20B**.
- USDC circulation ~**$79B** (Mar 2026); mgmt guides ~40% CAGR.
- Q1 2026 total revenue + reserve income **$694.1M** (+20% YoY, beat $677M); reserve income **$652.5M = 94%**.
- FY2025 total revenue + reserve income **$2.747B**; reserve income **$2.637B**; other revenue **$110M**;
  net loss to common **$70M**; compensation expense ~**$845M**.
- Coinbase: **100%** of interest on on-platform USDC, **50%** of residual off-platform; 2024 distribution
  costs ~**$1.01B**, of which **$907.9M** to Coinbase (~**54c** of every dollar); agreement reviewed ~every
  3 years, next due **2026**; costs driven by Coinbase policies Circle "neither controls nor oversees."
- Forward **P/S ~6.93** vs industry ~2.51; negative P/E (net losses).
- GENIUS Act prohibits issuers paying interest to holders; invites regulated competitors. USDC #2 behind
  Tether USDT (~**$140B** vs ~$79B), leads regulated/US/institutional venues.
