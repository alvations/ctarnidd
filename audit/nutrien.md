# Audit — Nutrien's record quarter is a potash-price bet dressed as a food-security story (NTR)

## Method

Forensic thesis: Nutrien (NTR) is a commodity price-taker whose Q1 2026 "record" was driven by benchmark prices it does not set, not by a durable, secular franchise. The article applies several forensic frames:

- **Adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings:** GAAP diluted EPS of $0.27 versus the headlined adjusted EPS of $0.51 (roughly double); adjusted EBITDA flatters a capital-heavy miner by excluding depreciation, impairments, and facility-closure costs.
- **Commodity-price-taker:** Potash EBITDA grew on a 21% rise in realized price ($264/tonne) and nitrogen EBITDA grew on a 13% price rise ($381/tonne) despite a 5% volume decline — earnings tracked benchmarks, not company-specific action.
- **Cyclical-priced-as-secular:** Potash peaked at ~$1,202/tonne in April 2022, fell to ~$311 by end-2023, and sits near $310–$380 in spring 2026 — a mean-reverting commodity arc, not secular growth.
- **Denominator illusion:** The +30% adjusted EBITDA and +1,090% net-income-to-shareholders gains were measured off a depressed post-trough 2025 base.
- **Cash-flow asterisk:** Q1 2026 operating cash flow was negative $851M (seasonal working capital), invisible in the EBITDA headline.
- **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry:** Revenue beat (~$6.05B vs ~$5.35B est.) yet the stock fell ~7.36% — the market discounted the cycle.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the genuine low-cost Saskatchewan moat, the stabilizing Retail franchise, the real demand floor under potash, and management's cost/capital discipline.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported April 28, 2026; some sources cite the underlying earnings date of May 6, 2026):

- Total revenue/sales: $6,046 million (~$6.0B), up ~18.6%–19% YoY — CONFIRMED (Nutrien IR, StockTitan, Businesswire, SEC 6-K).
- GAAP net earnings: $139 million; diluted GAAP EPS: $0.27 — CONFIRMED.
- Net income attributable to common shareholders: $131M, up ~1,090.9% YoY — CONFIRMED (per Nutrien release).
- Adjusted net earnings per share: $0.51 — CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $1.105 billion (~$1.11B), up ~30% YoY — CONFIRMED.
- Gross profit / gross margin: ~$1.6B ($1,646M), up ~24.7% — CONFIRMED.
- Potash adjusted EBITDA: $578M; record potash sales volume 3.51M tonnes; avg realized potash price +21% to $264/tonne — CONFIRMED (StockTitan, Investing.com).
- Nitrogen adjusted EBITDA: $482M; volumes -5%; price +13% to $381/tonne — CONFIRMED (Investing.com).
- Retail adjusted EBITDA: $108M (~+135% YoY) — CONFIRMED.
- Operating cash flow: negative ~$851M (seasonal) — CONFIRMED (StockTitan).
- Total debt: ~$12.6B ($2.77B short-term + $9.86B long-term) — CONFIRMED (StockTitan).
- Dividend: $0.55/share declared, ~$264M; buybacks ~$148M — CONFIRMED.
- 2026 guidance reaffirmed: Retail adj EBITDA $1.75–$1.95B; potash volume 14.1–14.8M tonnes; nitrogen volume 9.2–9.7M tonnes; capex $2.0–$2.1B — CONFIRMED.
- FY2025: net earnings $2.30B; revenue $19.59B (+8.23%); adjusted EBITDA $6.05B — CONFIRMED.
- Potash price history: ~$1,202/tonne peak April 2022; ~$311 by end-2023; ~$310–$380/ton U.S. Corn Belt spring 2026 — CONFIRMED (World Bank, GrainBrief, Natural Resources Canada).
- Stock reaction: fell ~7.36% post-print to low-$70s — CONFIRMED (Quiver, Investing.com).

CORRECTIONS / CALIBRATIONS:
- The suggested angle ("earnings collapsed from the 2022 spike") was PARTLY STALE: Q1 2026 was a cyclical *rebound*, not a collapse. Thesis pivoted to "commodity-price-taker / cyclical-priced-as-secular" which remains fully accurate and is the stronger forensic frame.
- EPS vs consensus is presented as contested: beat the Zacks $0.48 estimate on one tally but reportedly missed a $0.53 forecast on another (Investing.com). Article labels this as a contested figure rather than asserting a single verdict.
