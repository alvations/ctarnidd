# Audit — DoorDash (2026-06-12T1955-doordash.md)

Title: "DoorDash Moves $31 Billion of Food a Quarter and Keeps Almost None of It." Method + fact-check; kept out

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: DoorDash is the dominant US food-delivery
operator and looks great on the metric it highlights (adj EBITDA +28% to $754M, revenue +33% to $4.04B, orders
+27% to 933M, record Marketplace GOV $31.6B) — but the harder numbers run the other way: GAAP net income FELL to
$184M (from $193M) and free cash flow FELL to $420M (from $494M) despite +33% revenue. Quality-of-earnings /
thin-take frame: keeps ~0.6% of GOV as bottom-line profit; adj EBITDA = the number that makes a thin-margin
logistics business look like fat-margin software. Recurring frames stacked: declining-profit-on-rising-revenue
(anti-operating-leverage); regulatory-arbitrage on gig labor (largest cost depends on contractor classification +
NYC/Seattle min-pay laws); acquisition-flattered growth (Deliveroo/Wolt inflate GOV/rev numerator while depressing
profit); duopoly-permanent-tax (DoorDash vs Uber Eats both must keep spending); autonomous-delivery
disintermediation wildcard (who owns the robots). Honest-nuance: clear category leader, real scale/network moat,
genuine adj EBITDA growth, high-margin advertising upside (the Amazon/Uber ad-business analogy), credible intl
expansion + execution track record.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (DoorDash Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q / BusinessWire / StockTitan, May 6 2026):
- Revenue **$4.04B (+33%)**. GAAP net income (to common) **$184M, DOWN from $193M** Q1'25. **FCF $420M, DOWN from
  $494M** Q1'25. **Adj EBITDA $754M (+28% from $590M)**. **Marketplace GOV $31.6B (record)**. **Adj EBITDA % of GOV
  = 2.4%** (from 2.6%). Total orders 933M (+27%).
- Net income / GOV = 184/31,600 = **0.58%** ("about half a percent" / "six-tenths of one percent") — confirmed.
- Adj EBITDA / GOV = **2.4%** ("~2.4% take") — confirmed by filing.

### Correction made (this article)
- Draft said FCF fell "from a much higher figure a year earlier to $420 million." Verification: prior year was
  **$494M** (a ~15% decline, not "much higher"). Corrected to the exact figure "$494 million." All other
  load-bearing numbers verified as written.

### Editorial care
- Deliveroo/Wolt acquisition-distortion of GOV growth flagged as a frame, not asserted as a precise organic-rate
  number (no fabricated organic figure). Gig-labor reclassification framed as live regulatory risk (NYC/Seattle
  min-pay laws are real, passed), not a prediction of outcome. Autonomous-delivery section explicitly two-sided
  (margin tailwind OR disintermediation), labeled long-horizon. Full honest-nuance section concedes leadership,
  moat, real adj EBITDA growth, advertising upside. Thesis = thin/declining real profit + rich price, not failure.

No remaining corrections to load-bearing facts.
