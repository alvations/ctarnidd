# First Solar's record quarter rests on $2.1 billion of tax credits Washington keeps poking at

## Method

Forensic thesis: First Solar's headline Q1 2026 profitability (record net income, ~46.6% gross margin, reaffirmed $2.6–$2.8B full-year adjusted EBITDA guidance) is overwhelmingly a function of Section 45X advanced-manufacturing tax credits, not durable pricing power. The company guides to $2.10B–$2.19B of 45X credits in 2026 — roughly 75–80% of guided full-year adjusted EBITDA. The forensic moves: (1) quality-of-earnings — subsidy is roughly the size of the profit; (2) cash-monetization risk — credits sold for cash but dependent on a transferable-credit market; (3) policy/statute risk — 45X phase-down, FEOC sourcing rules, an actively rewritten tax code; (4) bookings-cliff — 1.7 GW gross bookings in Q1 vs 47.9 GW backlog being depleted; (5) denominator illusion in the margin; (6) customer concentration via the BP 6.6 GW contract-termination litigation; (7) tariff exposure as a double-edged policy bet (Section 232). A mandatory bull-concession section credits exemplary disclosure, genuine manufacturing/CdTe achievement, large contracted backlog, and the bipartisan durability of manufacturing (vs project) credits.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (multiple primary/secondary sources, report date April 30, 2026):
- Q1 2026 net sales: $1.04 billion, +24% YoY (vs $844.6M). CONFIRMED.
- Net income: $347 million, $3.22 diluted EPS (vs $210M / $1.95). CONFIRMED (also reported +65% YoY).
- Adjusted EBITDA: $520 million (vs $379M). CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin: 46.6% (vs 40.8%); some sources round to 47%. CONFIRMED.
- Modules sold Q1: ~3.8 GW; 96% US utilization. CONFIRMED.
- Full-year 2026 guidance reaffirmed: 17.0–18.2 GW volume; $4.9–$5.2B net sales; $2.6–$2.8B adjusted EBITDA. CONFIRMED.
- Section 45X credits 2026 guidance: $2.10B–$2.19B. CONFIRMED. (Q2 45X forecast $330M–$400M.)
- 2025 45X monetization ~$1.6B; Oct 2025 sale of $699.7M of credits for $668.2M cash; cumulative transfer deals >$2B. CONFIRMED.
- Backlog 47.9 GW at $14.4B aggregate transaction price (as of 3/31/2026), deliveries through 2030. CONFIRMED.
- Q1 gross bookings ~1.7 GW; debookings 0.1 GW. CONFIRMED. "~28 quarters to refill" is the author's arithmetic (47.9 / 1.7), labeled as such in the article.
- New US utility booking ASP ~$0.35/W inclusive of adjusters. CONFIRMED.
- BP litigation: terminated 6.6 GW contract "for customer default"; suit seeks not less than $405M, termination payments >$400M. CONFIRMED as First Solar's ALLEGATIONS — labeled un-adjudicated in the article.
- Section 232 polysilicon tariff seen as likely positive/insulating catalyst for CdTe First Solar. CONFIRMED as analyst characterization.

Corrections applied: none required; the "~75–80% of EBITDA" and "~28 quarters" are explicitly framed as derived ratios, not reported figures. FEOC/phase-out timing softened to "early 2030s / 2032" range per CRS and trade-press sourcing.
