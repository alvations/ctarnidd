# Audit — Floor & Decor sells more stores than flooring as comps fall 3.7% on a frozen housing market

## Method

Forensic thesis: Floor & Decor (NYSE: FND) is a cyclical, housing-turnover-dependent hard-surface flooring retailer being valued as a secular grower. Q1 FY2026 (quarter ended March 26, 2026, reported April 30, 2026) showed comparable-store sales down 3.7% and net sales down 0.7% even as the store base grew to 276 warehouses with six new openings. The "denominator illusion" — new stores masking same-store decline — holds total revenue roughly flat while the underlying franchise deteriorates. The company is capitalizing a housing-recovery TAM (long-term ~500-store target, 20 new stores in FY2026) against a housing market frozen at a 30-year-low 4.06M existing-home sales by rate lock-in. At ~22x forward / ~24x trailing P/E vs. a specialty-retail average ~11x, the premium is underwritten entirely by a recovery that has been forecast and missed for three consecutive years. Frames used: denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular, quality-of-earnings (operating deleverage), priced-for-perfection asymmetry, moat-vs-loophole, TAM-deployment gap.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 FY2026, quarter ended March 26, 2026, reported April 30, 2026):
- Net sales $1,152.3M, down 0.7% YoY — CONFIRMED (8-K / StockTitan / Investing.com).
- Comparable-store sales -3.7%; transactions -5.5%; average ticket +1.9% — CONFIRMED.
- Monthly comps: Jan +0.4%, Feb -6.9%, Mar -4% — CONFIRMED (earnings call).
- Diluted EPS $0.37, down 17.8% from $0.45 prior year — CONFIRMED.
- Net income $39.7M, down 18.8% from $48.9M — CONFIRMED.
- EPS missed consensus $0.41 (~9.76% miss) — CONFIRMED.
- Gross margin 44.0%, up despite lower sales — CONFIRMED.
- Six new warehouse stores opened; ended with 276 warehouses, 5 design studios, 5 distribution centers — CONFIRMED.
- $400M share repurchase authorization — CONFIRMED.
- Q2 comps running ~4.5% lower (per call) — CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: net sales ~$4,770M–$4,990M; comps (4.0)% to flat; diluted EPS ~$1.83–$2.08; 20 new stores; 4%–7% sales growth target — CONFIRMED.
- FY2024 comps -7.1%; Q2 FY2025 comps +0.4% (first positive since Q4 FY2022); Q1 FY2025 comps -1.8% — CONFIRMED.
- Valuation: ~$43 share price after ~16% drop; market cap ~$5.6B; trailing P/E ~24, forward ~22; specialty-retail avg forward ~11x; TTM revenue ~$4.68B, net income ~$199M, EPS ~$1.84 — CONFIRMED (Simply Wall St / stockanalysis.com / macrotrends).
- Pro mix ~50% vs ~45% prior year — CONFIRMED.
- Existing-home sales 4.06M in 2025, flat vs 2024 (lowest since 1995, 30-year low); ~70% of mortgage holders below 5% entering 2026; 30-yr fixed ~6.19% in December; NAR projects +14% 2026 sales, Fannie Mae 4.46M — CONFIRMED (Yahoo/AP, NAR, mortgage research).

Corrections made: None required after verification. Prior-year EPS ($0.45) and net income figures confirmed in the second fact-check pass. Long-term ~500-store target labeled as a long-run target, not a dated commitment.
