# Roku finally turned a GAAP profit — but stock comp still dwarfs it, and the boxes still sell at a loss

Article: `2026-06-17T0930-roku.md` · Ticker: ROKU · Sector: streaming

## Method

The suggested angle (unprofitable / thin platform-and-ad economics) was tested against the most recent quarter and found partly stale: Roku reached its **first full-year GAAP profit since 2021** in FY2025, and posted another profitable quarter in Q1 2026. The thesis was therefore PIVOTED from "Roku doesn't make money" to a **quality-of-earnings / priced-for-perfection** forensic frame:

1. **Profit is real but thinner than the SBC bill** — FY2025 GAAP net income (~$88M) is roughly one-quarter of full-year stock-based compensation, a genuine economic cost that already sits in the GAAP number and leaves almost no cushion.
2. **Hardware loss-leader is deepening, not fading** — Devices gross margin fell to **negative 16.3%** in Q1 2026 while device revenue fell ~16%; management guides "negative mid-teens" for full-year 2026. Paying more per unit to acquire fewer units.
3. **Denominator illusion** — streaming hours grew 8% while platform revenue grew 28%; the user base is maturing and growth is now price-driven, exactly the variable competition attacks.
4. **CTV competition** — Amazon (Prime Video ads), Netflix (ad tier), and Google (YouTube CTV) all now sell the premium connected-TV inventory Roku depends on, each with first-party data and/or owned premium content.
5. **Moat-vs-loophole** — Roku's advantage is the home-screen tollbooth, structurally bypassable in an app-first viewing world, unlike a content moat.
6. **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry** — current valuation requires ad rates, hardware drag, and SBC reduction to all resolve favorably at once.

A mandatory honest-nuance section concedes that the profit reversal is real and accelerating, SBC is genuinely falling, the platform compounds near 28%, 100M-household scale is real, and FY2026 guidance comes from a team that beats its own numbers.

- **Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA $148.4M, +165% YoY** — CONFIRMED.
- **Q1 2026 platform revenue $1.13B (+28%); advertising $613M (+27%); subscriptions $519M (+30%)** — CONFIRMED.
- **Streaming hours 38.7B, +8% YoY; 100M streaming households on Roku OS** — CONFIRMED.

- **Devices loss-leader since 2021; avg ~−12.1% gross margin 2023–2025; FY2026 devices guide ~$610M at "negative mid-teens"** — CONFIRMED.
- **FY2026 guidance: revenue $5.5B; net income $325M; adjusted EBITDA $635M (margin ~11.6%)** — CONFIRMED.

CORRECTION / SOFTENING:
- **Stock-based compensation FY2025:** one source reported **$354.2M** (down 8.1% from $384.7M in 2024), another reported **~$410M**. Article anchors on the smaller $354.2M figure and frames the relationship as SBC being "roughly four times" net income — defensible under BOTH figures (354/88 ≈ 4.0x; 410/88 ≈ 4.6x). The load-bearing claim ("SBC exceeds GAAP profit by a multiple") holds regardless. FY2026 SBC guided ~$325M; "clear path to fully offset dilution in 2026" — CONFIRMED as company language.

No invented numbers. All figures sourced to SEC filings, Roku shareholder letters/IR, or established financial press.
