## Method
Forensic thesis: IRM, the legacy records-storage REIT, has re-rated to a data-center/AI growth valuation on a
genuinely strong quarter (rev +22% to $1.94B, organic +17.2% — best in 25+ yrs, data center +47%, ALM +92%, AFFO/sh
$1.43 +22%, adj EBITDA $707.9M +22%, raised guidance, leverage 4.8x best since 2014) — but data centers are only
~$255M of $1.94B (~13%); ~87% is still the legacy records/services/recycling business. Frames: data-center-tail-vs-
legacy-dog (narrative vs mix); sticky-but-secularly-challenged records core (not a melting ice cube near-term, but
mature/slow-declining long-term); capital-intensive leveraged data-center race ($17.1B debt) vs better-funded
Equinix/Digital Realty/hyperscalers; REIT dividend-vs-capex tension (must lever/dilute to fund the build);
ALM is cyclical/volatile (+92% off a small base, IT-refresh + commodity dependent) not a smooth secular engine;
pricing-lever ceiling on the core; re-rating asymmetry. Honest-nuance: genuinely impressive reinvention, 17%
organic, real high-margin (52% EBITDA) data center, sticky high-cash core, raised guide, deleveraged to best since
2014, ALM taps a real circular-economy trend.
