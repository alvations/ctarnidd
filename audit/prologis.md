# Audit — Prologis trades at 21x FFO while its rent-rollover engine has lost two-thirds of its thrust

Article: `2026-06-21T1700-prologis.md` · ticker PLD · sector reits · report date Q1 2026 (April 16, 2026)

## Method

Forensic thesis: Prologis is priced at a premium AFFO/FFO multiple (~21x Core FFO at ~$128 on ~$6.15 midpoint guidance) as a secular e-commerce compounder, while the company's defining organic-growth engine — the re-leasing spread (net effective rent change on rollover) — has decelerated by roughly two-thirds from its 3Q23 peak, and the embedded lease mark-to-market (the reserve of future rent upside) is most of the way spent. Frames deployed: cyclical-priced-as-secular; denominator illusion (depleting mark-to-market); quality-of-earnings (cash SSNOI propped by "unusually low bad debt"); demonstration-vs-deployment (data-center pivot as a new narrative); customer concentration (Amazon as largest tenant); adjusted-vs-GAAP (Core FFO vs net earnings; promote add-back); priced-for-perfection asymmetry. The mandatory honest-nuance section concedes irreplaceable infill assets, fortress balance sheet (4.8x debt/EBITDA), strong current operating metrics, and the genuine possibility the cyclical-inflection call is correct.
