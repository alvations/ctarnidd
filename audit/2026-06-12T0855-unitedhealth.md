# Audit — UnitedHealth (2026-06-12T0855-unitedhealth.md)

Title: "How UnitedHealth Went From Wall Street's Safest Bet to Its Most Feared Stock." Method + fact-check; kept
out of the article. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T0855-unitedhealth/`. Batch 7 (CNBC/Bloomberg
headline titles), article 2 of 10 (target 2,900+ words; actual ~2,922). Source: news.

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: UNH lost ~half its value because the
medical cost ratio — stable ~82% for two decades — blew out ~600bp to ~88-89%, AND the DOJ is running criminal +
civil probes into the exact diagnosis-coding mechanism (Medicare Advantage + Optum vertical integration) that
made the model so profitable. The two crises reinforce: the usual margin-defense lever (aggressive risk coding)
is the thing under investigation. Vertical integration (insurer + clinics + PBM + data) = moat turned regulatory
target. Social-license erosion (handled soberly, incl. the Brian Thompson murder as a tragedy, not a data point)
raises political/MA-rate risk. Honest nuance: still hugely profitable, favorable 2027 MA rate notice, buybacks,
47% recovery — a broken *narrative*, not a broken company.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (UNH 8-K / SEC / CNBC / CNN / NBC / Fierce / TIKR / companiesmarketcap):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$111.7B**; earnings from operations **$9.0B**; EPS **$6.90** / adj **$7.23**; FY2026 adj
  guide **>$18.25/sh**.
- MCR: ~**82%** historical norm → ~**88%** in 2025 (some quarters **89.9%**), FY2025 **89.1%**, 2026 guide
  ~**88.8%** (~600bp swing); driver = rising senior utilization.
- Stock ~**−50%** (>$600 → ~$310); mkt cap **$465B → ~$300B**; +47% off March 2026 lows.
- **Witty** out, **Hemsley** back; 2025 guidance suspended; "public controversy over long-standing practices."
- **DOJ criminal + civil** MA-billing probes (inflated diagnoses; clinicians to raise diagnoses; Optum Rx).
- **Brian Thompson** (UnitedHealthcare CEO) murdered **Dec 4, 2024**, Manhattan. Optum UK sold; $2B+ buyback;
  favorable 2027 MA rate notice.

### Editorial care
- The murder is stated as a tragedy and explicitly *not* a market data point; used only to evidence the depth of
  public hostility / political risk. Allegations are labeled as investigations, not adjudicated facts ("no
  charges filed; company says it is complying").
- The inconsistent "Q1 2026 MCR 83.9%" figure (one source, conflicts with the 88.8% FY guide) was deliberately
  **omitted**; article uses only the well-corroborated full-year/historical MCR figures.

No corrections required.
