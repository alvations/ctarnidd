# Audit — CarMax's Q1 Beat Was Bought, Not Earned (KMX)

Article: `2026-06-16T0930-carmax.md`
Subject: CarMax Inc. (NYSE: KMX), used-car superstore / auto retail
Most recent quarter: Q1 fiscal 2027, ended May 31, 2026; reported June 17, 2026.

## Method

Forensic thesis: CarMax's Q1 FY2027 report read as a clean recovery (revenue +6.2% to $8.0B, EPS $1.31 vs ~94c consensus, combined units +3.3%), but the beat was engineered below the operating line and the cleaner organic metrics deteriorated. Frames used:

- **Bought-growth-masks-organic-stall** — combined unit growth (+3.3%) leans on store count + wholesale mix; comparable-store used units actually FELL 0.8%.
- **Margin-bought-volume** — GPU per retail used unit fell $230 to $2,177 (a record a year earlier); management explicitly attributes this to "pricing actions to drive an improved sales trend."
- **Quality-of-earnings / EPS engineered below the line** — net earnings fell 11.8% ($210.4M → $185.6M); EPS fell only ~5% ($1.38 → $1.31) thanks to buybacks; SG&A cut $24.5M (-3.7%). The "beat" vs consensus was a YoY decline.
- **Captive-finance / credit risk** — CAF income $140.2M, down 1.0%; CAF pushing down credit spectrum (Tier 2/3); loss provision flattered by a one-time $25.1M allowance release.
- **Cyclical-priced-as-secular** — affordability/rates/used-demand pressure is cyclical, risks being extrapolated as a structural turn off one flattered quarter.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against CarMax IR press release / 8-K / multiple secondary sources (StockTitan, Investing.com, Yahoo Finance, Zacks, Business Wire):

- Q1 FY2027 ended May 31, 2026; reported June 17, 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Net revenues $8.0B, +6.2% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- EPS $1.31 (diluted), vs Zacks consensus ~$0.94; prior-year EPS $1.38. CONFIRMED — EPS beat consensus but declined YoY.
- Net earnings $185.6M, down 11.8% from $210.4M. CONFIRMED.
- Combined retail+wholesale unit sales 392,357, +3.3%. CONFIRMED.
- Comparable-store used unit sales -0.8%. CONFIRMED.
- Gross profit per retail used unit $2,177, down $230 from prior-year all-time record. CONFIRMED.
- Gross profit per wholesale unit $1,046, ~flat. CONFIRMED.
- SG&A $635.2M, down 3.7% / $24.5M; SG&A per total unit $1,619, improved $118 / 6.8%. CONFIRMED.
- CAF income $140.2M, down 1.0%; tied to lower loans outstanding after $900M non-prime securitization (Q3 prior FY) with most residual interest sold. CONFIRMED.
- CAF financed 43.3% of units after 3-day payoffs, up from 41.8%. CONFIRMED.
- Q1 FY2027 provision for loan losses $95.6M vs $101.7M prior year, INCLUDING a $25.1M release for allowance on loans reclassified held-for-sale. CONFIRMED — characterized in article as flattering the headline provision.
- Q4 FY2026 provision $73.9M vs $68.3M prior year, tied to higher Tier 2 penetration from CAF credit-spectrum expansion. CONFIRMED (used as the cleaner cross-check).

Corrections applied during fact-check: clarified that EPS $1.31 was a YoY DECLINE from $1.38 (not "held up or rose"); reframed the EPS paragraph to show the buyback cushion (11.8% net-income drop vs ~5% EPS drop) rather than implying EPS rose.

No invented figures. The ~$50M forgone-gross-profit estimate is explicitly labeled an order-of-magnitude calculation (≈230k retail units × $230/unit) derived from disclosed figures, not a reported number.
