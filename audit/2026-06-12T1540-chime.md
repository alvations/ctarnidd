# Audit — Chime (2026-06-12T1540-chime.md)

Title: "Chime's Free Banking Runs on a Loophole, Not a Moat." Method + fact-check; kept out of the article. See
`../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T1540-chime/`. Batch 8, article 7 of 10 (target 2,900+ words; actual
~2,900). Source: CNET/fintech. (Timing hook: today = 1-yr anniversary of the June 12, 2025 IPO.)

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Chime (largest US neobank, 10.2M members,
first GAAP profit Q1'26) earns ~76% of revenue from interchange and collects an ABOVE-market rate only because it
routes through tiny partner banks (Bancorp, Stride) EXEMPT from the Durbin Amendment debit caps the big banks
obey. The "free" product is bait; interchange is the catch; the catch is lucrative only via a regulatory LOOPHOLE
(arbitrage-not-a-moat, same pattern as Hims/Kalshi). Merchants + periodic Congress want it closed → one law could
cut ~3/4 of revenue. Diversification (MyPay EWA) migrates into another regulatory thicket. Free product easily
copied. Priced (since the $27/~$11.6B IPO) for a durable platform it hasn't yet become. Honest-nuance: real
product, real profit, real distribution, diversifying, Durbin survived 15 yrs.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Chime 8-K/10-Q/IR / StockTitan / Yahoo / PYMNTS / Capital.com / sacra):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$647M** (+25%); **GAAP net income $53M** (first GAAP profit); **10.2M** members (+19%); ARPAM
  **$263**. FY26 guide rev **$2.66-2.69B**, adj EBITDA **$416-431M** (16% margin).
- Partner banks **The Bancorp Bank + Stride Bank** ("technology company, not a bank") — confirms Durbin-exempt
  small-bank model. ~**76%** interchange-of-revenue (2024, from $80% two yrs prior). IPO **June 12, 2025 / $27**
  / ~$11.6B.

### Notes (no correction)
- The "76% transaction margin" in one source is a DIFFERENT metric from the article's "76% of revenue =
  interchange" (2024); not a conflation — the interchange-share figure is independently sourced.
- One secondary source loosely dated the IPO "2024"; article uses the well-sourced **June 12, 2025 / $27**
  (Capital.com), consistent with the 1-yr-anniversary framing.

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes the product is genuinely good and the profitability/distribution real; thesis is the
  fragile regulatory-arbitrage foundation, not a bear call on the app. Durbin-exemption framed as legal and
  long-surviving (honest-nuance). MyPay/EWA-as-disguised-lending framed as a regulatory debate. Regulatory-
  change risk stated as a risk, not a prediction of imminent action.

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
