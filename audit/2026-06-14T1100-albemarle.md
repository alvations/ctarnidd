## Method
Forensic thesis: ALB posted a strong recovery quarter (Q1 2026 sales +33% to $1.4B, adj EBITDA $664M +148%, adj
EPS $2.95 vs $1.19 est, $1.3B debt cut) — but its own 2026 outlook reveals it is a leveraged bet on one
uncontrollable commodity price: FY adj EBITDA $0.9-1.0B at $10/kg lithium, $2.4-2.6B at $20/kg, $4.2-4.4B at
$30/kg. Frames: earnings-are-a-range-not-a-number (price-taker); one-quarter-is-a-bounce-not-a-turn (lithium
crashed ~80% from its 2022 peak; Q1 rode a recovery to ~$17/kg); structural oversupply overhang (China lepidolite,
African spodumene, partly price-insensitive Chinese/state supply); balance-sheet-repair-as-a-tell (mgmt sold
Eurecat + Ketjen stake, cut $1.3B debt — fortifying against the cycle, not betting it's over); Chile/sovereign
resource risk; commodity stocks feel safest at the top of the cycle. NOTE: corrected my outdated premise — lithium
has RECOVERED, so this is NOT a "price crash" article; the angle is the price-leverage + don't-extrapolate-the-bounce.
Honest-nuance: world-class low-cost leader (Greenbushes/Atacama), genuine recovery (price+volume+cost), de-risked
balance sheet, enormous long-term electrification demand + spectacular upside leverage if price sustains high.
