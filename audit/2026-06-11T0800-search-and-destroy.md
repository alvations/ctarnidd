## Method

News-driven: Google antitrust appeals + AI-Overviews/zero-click + $180B capex. Original market-research piece,
not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Alphabet fights a two-front war beneath strong numbers — a regulator
dismantling its search moat (Mehta monopoly ruling, banned default deals, forced data-sharing) and AI
dissolving its product (zero-click). The innovator's dilemma: defending against the machine cannibalizes the ad
profit; AI was both the excuse that spared it from breakup and the threat; the data-sharing remedy arms its AI
rivals; intent stays but the ad real estate shrinks; Cloud/YouTube/Waymo hedge revenue but not the profit.
Honest nuance: search is still GROWING (+19%), so this is "threats emerging beneath strong numbers," not
collapse. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `edcad60` → published; title "Search and Destroy" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Alphabet Q1 2026 rev **+22%** to **$109.9B** (11th straight double-digit qtr); Search & other **+19%**; YouTube
  **>$60B**. Market cap ~**$4.5T** ($4.49T, Jun 8); P/E ~**28** (discount to peers).
- **Mehta** ruling Sept 2, 2025: illegal monopoly, **no breakup**, **banned exclusive default-search contracts**,
  prohibited exclusive Search/Chrome/Assistant/Gemini distribution, ordered **search-index + user-interaction
  data sharing** (not ad data), kept Chrome; cited **GenAI** as a reason a breakup was hard to justify. Google
  appealed **Jan 16 2026**; DOJ cross-appealed **Feb 3 2026** (seeking divestitures).
- **Zero-click ~60%**; AI Overviews in ~**58%** of queries (from ~12% in 2024), AIO queries ~**83%** zero-click.
- 2026 capex **$180-190B** (~2× the $91.4B 2025); stock sank on the announcement.

Enhancements at fact-check (no corrections): added market cap (~$4.5T, ~28x), AI-Overviews penetration stat,
and the $180-190B/stock-sank detail. "Toll bridge"/intent-ads and Cloud/YouTube/Waymo-hedge sections are
analysis built on verified facts.
