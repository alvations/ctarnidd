# Audit — Texas Roadhouse trades at 27x earnings while beef inflation quietly eats its margin

Article: `2026-06-18T1100-texas-roadhouse.md` · Ticker: TXRH · Sector: restaurants

## Method

Forensic thesis: Texas Roadhouse is a genuinely best-in-class operator with sector-leading traffic (4.5% guest-count growth in Q1 2026), but its premium ~27x trailing P/E prices it as a secular grower while its core profitability is a commodity price-taker. Restaurant margin slipped to 16.3% of sales from 16.6% a year earlier as food costs rose 122 bps on 6.2% commodity inflation, with beef — the largest component of its basket — named by management as the swing factor. The value-positioning strategy (1.9% menu price vs 6.2% commodity inflation) is the deliberate choice that drives traffic AND guarantees margin compression. Diluted EPS growth was flattered by labor efficiency, unit growth, and buybacks — not by improving per-restaurant economics. The cattle herd sits near multi-decade lows; rebuilding runs years, not quarters; so the multiple bets on a commodity forecast management itself revised.

Forensic frames deployed: the truth-telling margin line; commodity-price-taker; the pricing-power paradox / moat-vs-loophole; cyclical-priced-as-secular valuation; adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings; denominator illusion (margin dollars +10.5%); the finite labor lever.
