# Audit — Charles Schwab (SCHW)

Article: `2026-06-21T1130-charles-schwab.md`
Subject: The Charles Schwab Corporation (NYSE: SCHW), financials sector.
Reporting period analyzed: Q1 2026 (quarter ended March 31, 2026), reported April 16, 2026.

## Method

The assigned angle — a cash-sorting/deposit-flight overhang and a TD Ameritrade-inherited NIM squeeze, with reliance on high-cost FHLB/CD supplemental funding — was **largely resolved as of Q1 2026** and would have been factually false if asserted as a current condition. The thesis was therefore **pivoted** while staying faithful to the same balance-sheet machinery.

The forensic thesis as published: Schwab's record Q1 2026 (revenue +16% to $6.48B, GAAP net income +30% to $2.48B, NIM 2.88% vs 2.53%, supplemental funding cut 77% to ~$5.1B) is a **cyclical normalization being priced as a secular new baseline**. The core forensic moves:
1. **Cyclical-priced-as-secular** — the deposit/NIM recovery is real but cyclical; the market capitalizes the recovered run-rate as a floor.
2. **Inverted rate bet** — management's EPS upside now explicitly requires *no* Fed rate cuts in 2026 (CFO cited benefit from a "lack of rate cuts"); the macro regime that nearly broke Schwab in 2023 is now the one its forecast leans on.
3. **Quality-of-earnings / hidden bond losses** — AFS-to-HTM reclassification carries underwater bonds at amortized cost; $496M of prior-transfer losses amortized in Q1; AOCI opt-out election excludes most AOCI from regulatory capital.
4. **Thin capital cushion** — adjusted Tier 1 leverage 6.8% sits at the bottom of the 6.75–7.00% target band, down from 7.1%, while buybacks ($2.4B) and a 19% dividend hike continue.
5. **Denominator illusion** — $11.77T client assets (+19%) is mostly custodied/market-driven; the funded spread book (deposits $253.0B) grew just 3%.
6. **Cyclical trading revenue** — 9.9M DATs (+34%) is a froth proxy, not structural growth.
7. **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry** — ~15x forward looks cheap only if the cyclical-peak denominator persists; the stock fell on the record print.

A mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the genuine deposit recovery, franchise durability, completed TD integration, and the legitimate higher-for-longer upside case.
