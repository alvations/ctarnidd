# Audit — ServiceNow (2026-06-12T2225-servicenow.md)

Title: "ServiceNow's AI Is Under a Tenth of the Business and the Whole of the Story." Method + fact-check; kept out

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: ServiceNow is celebrated as a top agentic-
AI winner and valued at a premium on that story, but the AI (Now Assist) is a minority slice of the business: AI
ACV tracking toward ~$1.5B in 2026 (raised from $1B; bookings more than doubling YoY) vs ~$15.75B subscription
revenue = under a tenth on a BOOKINGS basis, and less in recognized revenue (ACV ≠ revenue). Frames: demonstration-
vs-deployment (story dwarfs revenue), seat-model innovator's dilemma (the agentic AI it sells automates the human
seats it bills — same family as the b8 Salesforce piece, but distinct angle/title here), gentle deceleration (cc
~19-21%, down from earlier years; law of large numbers), consumption-pricing transition as hopeful base case (with
the CIO unpredictable-bill catch), AI-as-retention-cost, competition closing on the workflow surface
(Copilot/Agentforce). Honest-nuance: deeply embedded high-retention moat, real cRPO forward visibility, real FCF,
already DE-RATED (fwd P/E ~24, ~$105B cap — NOT a nosebleed), best-positioned to monetize AI; the ACV raise is a
genuine outperformance.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrections applied → post commit)

CONFIRMED (ServiceNow Q1 2026 8-K / earnings release / Futurum / Motley Fool transcript, reported Apr 22 2026):
- Subscription revenue **$3.671B (+22%; 19% cc)** ✓
- cRPO **$12.64B (+22.5%; 21% cc)** ✓
- FY26 subscription guidance **$15.735–15.775B** (~20.5–21% cc) ✓
- Now Assist customers >$1M ACV **+130% YoY** ✓; deals with 3+ Now Assist products +~70%; 36 deals with 5+ ✓
- **Now Assist AI ACV target RAISED from $1B to ~$1.5B for 2026; ACV more than doubled YoY** ✓
- Valuation: ~**$101/share, ~$105B market cap, trailing P/E ~61, forward P/E ~24** (companiesmarketcap /
  financecharts, Jun 2026). Stock has de-rated materially from its peak.

### Corrections made (pre-publish → post-commit) — IMPORTANT
- Draft originally framed the AI as "~$1 billion in 2026" and "6% of sales / six cents of every revenue dollar."
  Verification showed (a) the figure is **AI ACV (annual contract value, a BOOKINGS measure), not recognized
  revenue**, and (b) the target was **RAISED to ~$1.5B** (ACV more than doubling). Both the title and ~8 body
  references were rewritten: title "6% of Sales" → "Under a Tenth of the Business"; body now says "~$1.5B AI ACV
  (bookings) vs ~$15.75B subscription revenue = under a tenth on a bookings basis, less in recognized revenue,"
  and explicitly flags ACV≠revenue. Conceded the raise as genuine outperformance (honest-nuance). Net effect:
  thesis preserved (AI story >> AI revenue) but the number is now accurate and not cherry-picked to the lower old
  target.
- Valuation framing corrected pre-draft: ServiceNow is NOT a nosebleed multiple after its de-rating (fwd P/E ~24);
  the piece leads with the AI-proportion + seat-model thesis, NOT "absurdly expensive," and says so explicitly.

### Editorial care
- ACV-vs-revenue distinction stated plainly (no claim that $1.5B is recognized revenue). Seat-cannibalization
  framed as a navigable tension, not a death sentence; consumption pricing credited as a credible answer.
  Competition + retention-cost points framed as risks/possibilities, not asserted facts. No price target; no
  buy/sell language. Salesforce/Workday/SAP are related-ticker comparisons, not subjects.

No remaining corrections to load-bearing facts.
