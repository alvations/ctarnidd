## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the TikTok "divestiture" was a
restructuring engineered to the legal decimal — ByteDance keeps 19.9% (one-tenth of a point under the 20%
foreign-adversary trigger) and **licenses** the recommendation algorithm to the new U.S. entity to be
"retrained," so the seller retained the only asset that ever mattered. The $14B shell valuation against the
$550B ByteDance parent confirms what was actually transferred (the brand/data/servers, not the engine). Oracle
is the data landlord and a 15% owner whose stock needs marquee reference customers to justify ~$124B of capex
debt. Honest-nuance section ("What would have to be true") states the conditions under which the official
story holds.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `<commit>` → published; title "The Algorithm" held)

Verified as drafted (two search passes; nothing softened beyond what sources support):
- Deal closed **Jan 22, 2026** (TikTok USDS Joint Venture LLC). ByteDance retains **19.9%**; 80.1%
  American/global. TikTok US valued **~$14B**; Oracle 15% stake ~$2B (Mar 2026 filing). **170M+** US users.
- Consortium Oracle / Silver Lake / MGX = 50% (15% each); existing ByteDance investors 30.1%.
- Algorithm **licensed** (not sold), "retrained solely on U.S. data"; Oracle stores data + JV moderates.
  Compliance criticism quoted accurately (House Select Committee on CCP; Foreign Policy; Time).
- ByteDance parent ~**$550B** valuation; 2024 revenue $155B; larger than Meta by revenue.
- Oracle RPO **$523B**; FY26 capex ~$50B; total debt >**$124B**; Stargate $500B partner. (Oracle reported
  fiscal Q4 on 2026-06-10; article uses the established RPO/debt/capex framing, not unverified post-earnings
  deltas.)

No corrections required. The 19.9%-vs-20%-threshold, license-not-sale, and Oracle-incentive framings are
analysis built on verified facts; the surveillance and "algorithm won't change" lines are attributed to named
critics, not asserted as adjudicated fact.
