## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Alibaba's AI/cloud comeback is genuine
(cloud +38%, Qwen, ~$15B FY26 net income, $41B net cash, ~22x), but the "China discount" the bulls call
temporary is structural and permanent because of risks that live *above* the business, in law and politics:
(1) the VIE structure — foreign holders own a Cayman shell plus contracts untested in PRC courts, not the China
operating businesses; (2) state subordination of shareholders (the 2020-22 crackdown erased ~$1T, killed the Ant
IPO, drove BABA −80%); (3) the Xi/Jack Ma handshake is the risk in its purest form (value as a function of
party-state favor); (4) the HFCAA delisting tail (genuinely mitigated by the HK dual-listing). Peer-wide
(PDD/JD/KWEB) ⇒ jurisdiction/structure tax, not company-specific. The AI re-rating is being bought with the very
profit that anchors the cheap multiple. Honest-nuance section credits the strong operational/value case.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → strengthened → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Alibaba 6-K/SEC / StockTitan / Morningstar / AI Weekly / phemex / CII):
- FY2026 net income to ordinary shareholders **RMB105,904M (~$15.35B)**; cloud **+38%** to ~**$6.1B**; AI
  triple-digit 11 straight quarters; **$41B** net cash; ~**22x** earnings; +**70%** off 2024 lows.
- Capex FY26 ~**RMB126B**; will **exceed the $52B three-year** AI commitment.
- **VIE** (Cayman shell + untested contracts), **HFCAA** delisting tail + **HK dual primary listing** backstop,
  2020 **Ant IPO killed** / ~**$1T** crackdown / BABA **−80%** — all confirmed.

### Strengthen (not a correction of error)
- Draft's cloud "operating earnings toward zero" line upgraded to confirmed harder figures: group **non-GAAP net
  income −62%** FY26; **Q4 FY26 adjusted net income −~99.7%** (~RMB86M); **first operating loss since the COVID
  era**. Makes the "AI re-rating bought with profit" point more precise.

### Editorial care
- VIE/state risks framed as disclosed structural risks and historical fact, not predictions of imminent
  expropriation; the bull case (20-yr VIE function, HK backstop, fortress balance sheet, real growth) is given a
  full honest-nuance section. No claim that delisting/VIE-collapse is a base case (explicitly called a tail).

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
