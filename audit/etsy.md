# Audit — Etsy Returned to Growth by Taking More From Its Sellers, Not Its Buyers

## Method

Forensic thesis: Etsy's Q1 2026 "turnaround" (reported April 29, 2026) is real on the income statement but substantially engineered rather than organic. The headline marketplace growth (revenue +7.6%, GMS +5.5%, first sequential active-buyer growth in two years, GAAP profit of $104.7M) is flattered by (1) take-rate expansion to 25.7% (+180bps YoY), of which roughly 130bps came from the Reverb divestiture rather than a healthier marketplace; (2) a denominator illusion in GMS-per-active-buyer ($122, "first YoY growth since 2022") driven partly by two years of buyer attrition shrinking the base; (3) divestitures (Reverb sold 2025; Depop being sold to eBay for $1.2B) that simplify the company and mechanically lift the take rate; and (4) a weak-dollar tailwind (currency-neutral GMS growth only 3.6%). On a continuing-operations basis consolidated GMS was DOWN 3.9% YoY and revenue grew only 3.1%. The strategy of raising the rake on sellers to offset flat-to-declining buyer volume is self-limiting against Temu/Amazon Handmade competition. Frames used: denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular, bought-growth-unwound, quality-of-earnings, moat-vs-loophole, priced-for-perfection asymmetry, self-limiting take-rate spiral.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported April 29, 2026):
- Total revenue: $631.3 million (rounded $631M in coverage). CONFIRMED.
- Take rate: 25.7%, up 180 basis points YoY; ~130bps attributable to Reverb divestiture; drivers Etsy Ads + marketplace changes. CONFIRMED.
- Etsy marketplace GMS: $2.46 billion, up 5.5% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Continuing-operations basis: consolidated GMS -3.9% YoY; revenue +3.1%. CONFIRMED via 8-K summary.
- Currency-neutral marketplace GMS growth: 3.6% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Marketplace revenue growth: 7.6% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Net income from continuing operations: $104.7 million (vs $(35.1)M loss in Q1 2025). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $184.7 million ($185M rounded), 29.3% margin. CONFIRMED.
- Active buyers: sequential growth for the first time in two years (exact Q1 2026 count not separately disclosed in summaries; trailing-12-mo ~86.6M cited in one transcript summary). Stated cautiously in article.
- GMS per active buyer (TTM): $122, first YoY growth since 2022. CONFIRMED.
- Active-buyer history: 95.5M (end 2024) -> 88.5M (Q1 2025) -> 87.3M (Q2 2025) -> ~86.5M (Q4 2025). CONFIRMED.
- Mobile app GMS: 47% of total, +11.2% YoY. CONFIRMED (context).
- Depop sale to eBay: $1.2 billion; closing pushed to end of Q3 2026; U.S. and Germany clearance received. CONFIRMED. Depop bought 2021 for ~$1.6B.
- Reverb sold June 2025. CONFIRMED.
- Transaction fee raised 5% -> 6.5% in April 2022 (a 30% increase, prompted seller strike). CONFIRMED.
- Q2 2026 guidance: GMS $2.48–2.53B; adj EBITDA margin 27–29%; FY low-single-digit GMS growth. CONFIRMED.

Corrections made during drafting: characterized headline 5.5%/7.6% growth as marketplace-segment figures and explicitly contrasted with the negative -3.9% continuing-operations consolidated number and 3.6% currency-neutral mark, to avoid overstating reacceleration. Active-buyer count for Q1 2026 stated as "sequential growth" rather than a hard number, since the exact figure was not cleanly confirmed across sources.
