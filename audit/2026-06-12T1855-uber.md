## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Uber's profitability inflection is real
(Q1'26 bookings $53.7B, FCF $2.3B, non-GAAP NI $1.5B, Uber One 50M), and the market re-rated it as a quality
compounder — but the profit rests on a two-sided MARKETPLACE of HUMAN drivers, and AVs replace the
infinite/powerless/capital-free driver pool with a handful of powerful AV operators who own their supply and can
DISINTERMEDIATE the marketplace (build their own app: Tesla Robotaxi does; Waymo hedges with Waymo One). Uber's
own $10B+ into owning robotaxi alternatives = its admission the partnership model is fragile. The market priced
the favorable (Uber-as-demand-layer) outcome as SETTLED; it's the opposite of settled. Honest-nuance: real
profit/cash, real human-driver network moat, Waymo-via-Uber works at small scale, slow transition (250k/wk vs
3.6B trips/qtr; decade-out — cuts both ways), delivery (Eats +23%) hedge, Khosrowshahi credible.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Uber 8-K/SEC / CNBC / StockTitan / TIKR / Fortune / Gizmodo / Electrek):
- Q1 2026 gross bookings **$53.7B** (+25% reported / +21% cc); revenue **$13.2B** (+14%); **FCF $2.3B**;
  non-GAAP NI **$1.5B** (+39%); **Uber One 50M** (~half of GBs); Mobility +20% / Delivery +23%.
- Autonomous trips >10x YoY; ~25-30 AV partners; **Waymo 250k+/wk** (US total, share via Uber); **Tesla Robotaxi
  own app** (Austin Jun 2025) → bypasses Uber; **Uber $10B+ into owning robotaxi alternatives** (Rivian etc.).

### Corrections/additions

2. Added Uber's **$10B+ captive-AV investment** (hedge = admission disintermediation risk is real) — strengthens
   thesis.
