## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Spotify's profitability inflection is
genuinely real (Q1'26 rev €4.53B, record 33% gross margin, €715M op income, €3.2B LTM FCF, 293M subs) — but
structurally capped because ~70-72% of music revenue goes to the labels/publishers who OWN the songs. Core music
gross margin is mid-single-digits; the 33% blend is achieved by diluting music with higher-margin non-music. The
big-3 labels hold the leverage (1-3yr licenses, squeeze at renewal, own the one un-substitutable input). Margin
gains are BORROWED (price hikes + mix), not won from the royalty war Spotify has never durably won. The escape
route (audiobook bundling) already triggered a royalty fight (~€410M MLC liability). Priced like a platform,
structured like a distributor; rivals (Apple/Amazon/YouTube) can run music as a loss-leader, Spotify can't.
Honest-nuance: scale (761M MAU) gives real bargaining power, pricing power proven, non-music real, €3.2B FCF real.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Spotify 6-K/SEC / Quartr / StockTitan / Variety / Billboard / MBW):
- Q1 2026 revenue **€4.53B** (+8%); **gross margin 33.0%** (+133bp); op income **€715M** (15.8% margin, beat
  €660M guide); **FCF €824M** (+54%); **293M** Premium subs; **761M** MAU.
- **MLC / audiobook-bundling royalty fight CONFIRMED** (MLC suit; NMPA "undercutting songwriters"; Spotify calls
  it "nonsensical/wasteful").

WELL-SOURCED (analysis/industry, hedged in body): **~70-72%** royalty share (long-documented Spotify economics);
**~€410M** MLC liability ("potential," contingent). Both hedged ("roughly").

### Note

  total (293M, +9% YoY) and does not claim NA growth — no distortion.

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes the profitability is real and Spotify is a far better business than the 2010s version;
