## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `ec5cf8c` → published; title "Twenty Miles Wide" held)

Verified as drafted:
- Strait **~21 miles** wide at narrowest (title rounds to "twenty"; body "about twenty miles"); shipping lanes
  ~2 miles each way.
- **~20M bbl/day** through Hormuz = **~20%** of global petroleum consumption (EIA); ~25% seaborne; ~20% LNG.
- 2026 Iran war: **90%+** traffic diverted (>95% on Iran threats), ~200 ships stranded; Brent **$72 → past
  $120** peak (**highest since 2008**); retreated to **~$90s** on ceasefire, **~$95 June 11** rising on 2nd day
  of US strikes (consistent with "oscillating" framing).
- OPEC spare capacity is in the Gulf, behind the strait → ramp "largely symbolic" (sourced).
- Oxford Economics: **~$140/bbl for ~2 months → recession**; ECB postponed cuts, raised inflation forecast.
- **XOM** ~$600B+ cap, ~+25% in 2026; **CVX** high-$180s (ATH $209.23). Energy = war-premium beneficiaries.
- 1973 embargo (oil ~4×, ~50% real bear market) and 1979 Iranian-revolution shock → stagflation: historical.
