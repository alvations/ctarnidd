## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Cloudflare grows 34% (rev $639.8M) at one
of the richest sales multiples in software (~28x FY26 rev), a valuation that assumes pristine/expanding software
gross margins — yet gross margin is FALLING (GAAP 71.2%; non-GAAP 73%, down from 77% a year earlier), the "tell"
that the newer AI-inference/compute growth carries structurally worse, more capital-intensive unit economics than
the legacy CDN/security business. Frames stacked: (1) falling-gross-margin-as-mix-shift (the core diagnostic for a
software multiple); (2) non-GAAP-vs-GAAP gap — $94M non-GAAP net income vs a ~$62M GAAP operating LOSS, >$150M gap,
mostly SBC; (3) FCF capital-intensity risk as AI capex rises; (4) AI pivot fights hyperscalers (AWS/Azure/GCP) on
capital-heavy turf; (5) 20% layoff "dressed as confidence" (visionary OR admission the economics weren't scaling);
(6) priced-for-perfection double-hit asymmetry; (7) Fastly cautionary tale (edge darling that collapsed).
Honest-nuance: beloved developer bottom-up moat, durable 30%+ growth at scale, real FCF (13%), 73% growth in $1M+
deals (real enterprise traction), genuine network asset + edge/AI optionality.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; corrections applied before post-commit)

CONFIRMED (Cloudflare Q1 2026 8-K/8-K/A/10-Q via SEC + Investing.com + StockTitan + TIKR, reported May 7 2026):
- Revenue **$639.8M (+34%)** ✓; FY26 guide ~**$2.81B (~30%)** ✓; Q2 guide $664–665M ✓
- **GAAP gross margin 71.2%**; **non-GAAP gross margin 73%, down from 77% Q1'25** ✓
- Non-GAAP net income **$94M** ✓; non-GAAP operating income $73.1M (11.4% margin) ✓
- **GAAP loss from operations ≈ $(62.0)M** ✓ — the load-bearing adjusted-vs-GAAP gap
- FCF **$84.1M (13% of revenue)** ✓
- **4,416 customers >$100K/yr**; **$1M+ deals +73% YoY** ✓
- **20% workforce reduction (~1,100 employees)** for agentic-AI operating model ✓
- Stock ~$227 at analysis → mkt cap ~$78–79B / ~$2.81B FY rev ≈ **~28x sales** (supports "twenty or thirty times
  revenue" / "richest sales multiples in software")

### Corrections made (this article, pre-publish→post-commit)
1. Gross margin YoY: draft said "fell to 71.2% from nearly 75% a year earlier" (the 74.9% was a Q2'25 figure, not
   the year-ago quarter). CORRECTED to the verified, precise form: "GAAP gross margin 71.2%; non-GAAP gross margin
   73%, down from 77% a year earlier." Applied in dek + body.
2. GAAP profit: draft said GAAP "has hovered near breakeven or loss" (vague). STRENGTHENED to the exact verified
   figure: "$94M non-GAAP net income vs a GAAP loss from operations of ~$62M in the same quarter, a >$150M gap,
   mostly SBC."

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes Cloudflare is exceptional/beloved; thesis = price + the margin tell, not failure. Fastly
  comparison explicitly flatters Fastly / disclaims that Cloudflare = Fastly (uses it for the category MECHANIC,
  not as an equivalence). AI-margin causation framed as "likely explanation," not asserted certainty. Layoff
  framed two-sidedly. No exact P/S printed in the article (kept qualitative: "twenty or thirty times revenue");
  exact ~28x computed only here in the audit. No price target; no buy/sell language.
