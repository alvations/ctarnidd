# Sarepta's Elevidys revenue cratered 73% to $102 million as a boxed warning and three deaths gutted the gene-therapy story

## Method

Forensic thesis: Sarepta (SRPT) was valued as a secular gene-therapy compounder, but Elevidys revenue is a regulatory-contingent line, not an annuity. After three deaths attributed to acute liver failure (two ambulatory-status-related Elevidys patients plus one Phase 1 SRP-9004 LGMD patient), the FDA imposed a boxed warning, removed nonambulatory eligibility, revoked the AAVrh74 platform designation, placed clinical holds on SRP-9004/SRP-9003, and pressured a voluntary U.S. shipment halt. Elevidys Q1 2026 net product revenue collapsed ~73% YoY to $102.0M from ~$375M. The piece reframes a "secular" franchise as a binary safety/regulatory bet: the denominator (eligible patients) is set by the FDA's tolerance, not biology. Forensic frames used: denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular (in reverse), quality-of-earnings (GAAP profit built on milestones/collaboration revenue), moat-vs-loophole, restructuring-as-confession. Honest-nuance section concedes real unmet need, resumed ambulatory shipments, $748.3M cash runway, and the Roche royalty stream.

- Cash and investments $748.3M as of March 31, 2026 — CONFIRMED
- $40.0M Roche/Chugai Japan-launch milestone (March 2026) — CONFIRMED
- April 2026 sNDAs to convert Amondys 45 + Vyondys 53 to traditional approval — CONFIRMED
- Restructuring: ~500 jobs / ~36% workforce cut; ~$400M targeted annual savings (~$120M cash from headcount, ~$300M non-personnel) — CONFIRMED
- Boxed warning (acute serious liver injury / acute liver failure); nonambulatory eligibility removed; AAVrh74 platform designation revoked; clinical holds on SRP-9004/SRP-9003; voluntary U.S. shipment halt; FDA later recommended removing hold for ambulatory patients, shipments resumed for ambulatory — CONFIRMED
- Three deaths attributed to acute liver failure (2 Elevidys, 1 SRP-9004 Phase 1) — CONFIRMED
