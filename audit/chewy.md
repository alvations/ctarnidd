# Audit — Chewy's growth slides to 7.7% as guidance cut sends shares to a new floor

Article: `2026-06-18T1800-chewy.md` · Ticker: CHWY · Sector: e-commerce

## Method

Forensic thesis: Chewy has quietly transitioned from a hyper-growth pet e-commerce compounder into a maturing, subscription-anchored retailer. Net-sales growth has decelerated to 7.7% YoY (from 8.3% a year earlier) and full-year guidance was cut; active customers grew only 3.6% to 21.5 million and management warned net adds would trend to the low end of its range "given the current environment." The growth that remains is being extracted from a near-flat loyal cohort (NSPAC up ~4.6% to $597) rather than from new households — a margin/monetization story dressed as a growth story. Record GAAP net income ($94.8M) is materially flattered by a $73.4M stock-based-compensation-and-related-taxes line (≈ three-quarters of reported profit), so quality of earnings is thinner than adjusted EBITDA implies. Despite all this, the stock still carried a trailing P/E near 49 while sitting ~45% below its three-months-prior high. Forensic frames used: bought/extracted-growth-masks-organic-stall, denominator illusion (flat customer count flatters NSPAC), cyclical-priced-as-secular, adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings, priced-for-perfection asymmetry, competitive vise (Amazon/Walmart), margin-runway-has-an-end.

- Gross margin: 30.1%, +50 bps YoY. Article says "roughly 30%." CONFIRMED.
