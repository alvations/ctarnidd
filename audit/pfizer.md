# Audit — Pfizer's 6% dividend leans on a $43B oncology bet and a GAAP payout near 130%

Article: `2026-06-21T1000-pfizer.md`
Subject: Pfizer Inc. (PFE) — pharma

## Method

Forensic thesis: Pfizer's headline Q1 2026 beat ($14.45B revenue, +5.4%; adjusted EPS $0.75; FY guidance reaffirmed) masks a structurally strained setup. The ~6%+ dividend yield is marketed as a certainty but is, on the company's own cash-flow statement, a contingency. The piece tests four load-bearing claims:

1. **Quality-of-earnings / adjusted-vs-GAAP** — the comfortable ~53% adjusted payout ratio sits against a GAAP payout near 126%, and FY2025 dividends ($9.771B) exceeded free cash flow (~$9.076B) by ~$695M.
2. **Balance-sheet deterioration** — total debt rose (~$63.6B → ~$67.4B) while liquid assets fell (~$20.5B → ~$13.6B) in the same year.
3. **Cyclical-priced-as-secular / patent wall** — the COVID windfall funded a debt-heavy pivot (Seagen $43B, Metsera, Biohaven) that must ramp before Eliquis (EU patent expired May 2026; US LOE 2027-28) and Ibrance roll off; cumulative LOE exposure ~$17-18B (2025-2030), ~$1.5B in 2026.
4. **Cost-cuts-carry-EPS** — the $7.7B cost program (raised from $4.5B) is propping EPS while R&D is itself being restructured.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes: real beat, reaffirmed guidance, +7% ex-COVID operational growth, 20% Seagen oncology growth, Padcev +13% to $464M, oncology ~27% of revenue, ~53% adjusted payout gives board room, and the stock is genuinely cheap (not priced for perfection).
