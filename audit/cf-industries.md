# Audit — CF Industries posts a record $615M quarter — and a war, a lawsuit, and cheap gas did most of the lifting

Article: `2026-06-16T1030-cf-industries.md` · ticker CF · sector agriculture

## Method

Forensic thesis: CF Industries is a textbook commodity price-taker on both inputs (North American natural gas) and outputs (global nitrogen — ammonia, urea, UAN). Its record Q1 2026 print was driven almost entirely by three factors outside management's control and unlikely to recur on demand: (1) a global nitrogen price spike, tightened by Iran-related supply disruption, contributing ~$401M to the EBITDA increase; (2) a one-time ~$170M litigation settlement gain folded into adjusted EBITDA; (3) a structurally low North American gas cost ($4.57/MMBtu) versus global rivals. Frames deployed: commodity-price-taker, quality-of-earnings (adjusted-vs-GAAP, one-time settlement), cyclical-priced-as-secular, denominator illusion (settlement inflating the EBITDA the multiple capitalizes), moat-vs-loophole (cost-curve position is real but erodable), priced-for-perfection asymmetry, demonstration-vs-deployment (99% utilization = ceiling not runway), policy/geopolitics dependence (the "tight through 2027" thesis is a bet on continued conflict). The mandatory honest-nuance section concedes: real and durable cost-curve position, legitimate clean-ammonia optionality, and strong TTM cash generation (~$2.66B operating, ~$1.65B free).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (CF Q1 2026 results, reported May 6, 2026; primary sources Business Wire / company IR / SEC 8-K):
- Net sales $1.99B, up ~19% from $1.66B in Q1 2025 — CONFIRMED.
- Net earnings $615M; diluted EPS $3.98 — CONFIRMED.
- EBITDA $1.01B; adjusted EBITDA $983M (nearly double Q1 2025) — CONFIRMED.
- Litigation settlement gain ~$170M with Orica and Nelson Brothers — CONFIRMED. Orica agreed to pay ~$169.5M cash by April 30, 2026; gain recorded in Q1, cash received April. (Orica press release / Capital Brief reported the broader Orica settlement at A$243m / "$243m" including the Nelson Brothers explosives acquisition — the cash to CF is ~$170M; article uses the ~$170M figure correctly.)
- Natural gas cost in cost of sales $4.57/MMBtu vs $3.68 prior-year quarter — CONFIRMED (Business Wire / 8-K).
- EBITDA bridge: price +$401M, volume -$39M, realized gas -$76M — CONFIRMED (Business Wire / 8-K).
- Gross ammonia production ~2.5M tons, ~99% utilization — CONFIRMED.
- Share repurchases ~155,000 shares for ~$15M; ~$1.7B remaining authorization — CONFIRMED.
- 2026 capex ~$1.3B consolidated; Blue Point JV / growth infrastructure included — CONFIRMED.
- TTM operating cash flow ~$2.66B; TTM free cash flow ~$1.65B — CONFIRMED (company IR page).
- "Tight through 2027" nitrogen outlook; ~50% of first-quartile global urea capacity exposed to geopolitical conflict — CONFIRMED (company commentary).

Corrections made: none required; all figures softened to "approximately/roughly" where the press summaries rounded. The article does not assert the $401M or $4.57 figures appear in every source — they originate in the Business Wire release and SEC 8-K exhibit, the primary disclosure.
