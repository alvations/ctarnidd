# Audit — Ulta Beauty Beat With 5.3% Comps — Then Guided the Rest of the Year to Half That

Article: `2026-06-20T1130-ulta-beauty.md`
Ticker: ULTA (Nasdaq). Sector: retail (specialty beauty).

## Method

Forensic thesis: ULTA's Q1 FY2026 (period ended May 2, 2026) was a genuine beat — net sales +11.1%, comps +5.3%, EPS +15.5% — yet management reaffirmed full-year comparable-sales guidance of just 2.5%–3.5%, less than half the quarter it posted. The piece argues the market is pricing re-acceleration while the company's own guidance forecasts deceleration. Supporting frames: (1) beat-vs-guide divergence; (2) the comp is ticket-led (3.7% ticket vs 1.6% transactions), a more fragile mix; (3) cyclical post-pandemic normalization priced as secular growth; (4) operating margin off its ~16% 2022 peak toward ~12.4% (FY2025); (5) the Target shop-in-shop lever flattening; (6) Sephora-at-Kohl's competition; (7) borrowed-moat brand-access erosion; (8) denominator illusion (11.1% headline vs 5.3% organic comp); (9) priced-for-perfection asymmetry at a low-20s P/E. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the beat-and-raise, the loyalty engine, services differentiation, clean balance sheet, durable traffic, and buyback-driven EPS.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Q1 FY2026, reported June 2, 2026; period ended May 2, 2026), corroborated across CNBC, Business Wire/Ulta IR, Globe and Mail, StockTitan, Investing.com:
- Net sales +11.1% to ~$3.16 billion (vs ~$3.10B est.).
- Comparable sales +5.3% (vs StreetAccount ~4.6%); driven by average ticket +3.7% and transactions +1.6%.
- Operating income +11.6% to $448.3 million, = 14.2% of net sales.
- Diluted EPS $7.74, +15.5% (vs ~$6.86 expected).
- Gross margin 40.1% of net sales vs 39.1% prior year (lower shrink, higher merchandise margin).
- FY2026 guidance reaffirmed/updated: net sales growth 6%–7%; comparable sales growth 2.5%–3.5% (UNCHANGED); operating income growth 6.5%–9% (low end raised from 6%); diluted EPS $28.36–$28.80 (raised from $28.05–$28.55).
- FY2025 full-year comps +5.4% (ticket +3.3%, transactions +2.0%); FY2025 operating income ~$1.5B, ~12.4% of net sales.
- Operating margin peaked near ~16% on a full-year basis at the post-pandemic high (FY2022 era) and has compressed since.
- Stock ~$509 in late May 2026; trailing P/E low-to-mid 20s.

CORRECTIONS / CALIBRATION: Article states operating margin "roughly 16%" at peak and "toward 12%" — kept deliberately approximate ("roughly," "toward") because one source cited an 18.7% figure that appears to be a quarterly, not full-year, peak; the ~16% full-year characterization is the defensible framing and FY2025's 12.4% is confirmed. Target shop-in-shop "flattening" and Sephora-at-Kohl's framed as competitive/structural observations, not as precise unit counts.
