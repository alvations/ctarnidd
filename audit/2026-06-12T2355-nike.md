# Audit — Nike (2026-06-12T2355-nike.md)

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the market prices Nike's recovery as a
REBOUND while Q3 FY2026 shows it still in a REBUILD with numbers pointing down — revenue −3% currency-neutral
($11.3B), gross margin −130bps to 40.2% (tariffs + promotions), Greater China guided ~−20% (headwinds into FY27),
Q4 revenue guide −2-4%, EPS $0.35 (−35% YoY), and management's own words: recovery "taking longer than
anticipated." Frames: turnaround-vs-rebound; self-inflicted wounds (DTC over-pivot + neglected innovation) that are
slow to undo; China structural decline (Anta/Li-Ning + nationalism + weak consumer, not cyclical); two-sided margin
squeeze (tariffs up, clearance down); competitors (On/Hoka/New Balance/Adidas) holding ground Nike vacated;
inventory ($7.5B, flat, units up) showing the cleanup unfinished; multiple-runs-ahead-of-results. Honest-nuance:
most powerful sport brand, fortress balance sheet, respected returned leader making demonstrably correct moves
(wholesale re-engagement, innovation, sport focus), Q4 modest NA growth (stabilizing), has come back before.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; all confirmed)

CONFIRMED (Nike Q3 FY2026 8-K / 10-Q / CNBC / WWD / SGI, reported Mar 31 2026; quarter ended Feb 28 2026):
- Revenue **$11.3B, flat reported / −3% currency-neutral** ✓
- **Gross margin 40.2%, −130bps** (tariffs + promotions) ✓
- **Greater China guided ~−20%** in Q4; headwinds continue into FY2027 ✓; warned sales fall rest of calendar year
- **Diluted EPS $0.35, down from $0.54 = −35% YoY** ✓ (article cites $0.35; the −35% is an even stronger data point,
  noted here; margin compression already conveys it)
- Inventories **$7.5B, ~flat vs May 31 2025** (units up, offset by mix) ✓
- Q4 guide **revenue −2-4%**, modest NA growth (lapping prior value liquidation) offset by China + Converse ✓
- Beat analyst revenue + EPS estimates, but shares FELL on the China warning ✓

### Editorial care
- "Turnaround not rebound" is the analytical thesis, supported by the company's own "taking longer than
  anticipated." China decline framed as structural (named: Anta/Li-Ning, nationalism, weak consumer) — defensible,
  widely reported. Competitors named (On/Hoka/New Balance/Adidas Samba) as having taken share — factual and
  uncontested. Conceded the right-leadership/right-moves + NA stabilization explicitly (honest-nuance). No exact
  P/E asserted (valuation kept qualitative as "rallied on turnaround hope"). No price target; no buy/sell language.
  Tariff attribution kept as "tariffs + promotions" per the filing (not tariffs alone).

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
