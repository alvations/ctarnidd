## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Kalshi (~$22B) offers functionally
nationwide sports betting as CFTC-regulated "event contracts," bypassing the state-by-state licensed/taxed
sportsbook regime — a regulatory ARBITRAGE. It is genuinely disrupting DraftKings (Kalshi sports ≈ a quarter of
DKNG sportsbook revenue per FT; exchange model → better payouts → margin compression; expansion into high-margin
props; DKNG forced to enter via a CME-routed CFTC exchange). But the entire $22B rests on ONE unsettled legal
proposition — CEA federal preemption of state gambling law — in active litigation: MA enjoined Kalshi; the
divided Third Circuit (Apr 6 2026) sided WITH Kalshi vs NJ; circuit split → likely SCOTUS; Minnesota's
first-in-nation felony ban drew suits from both Kalshi and the CFTC. Same "arbitrage ≠ moat" pattern as Hims
(#7). "Everything becomes a bet" widens the opposition. Honest-nuance: genuine info-aggregation value, favorable
CFTC, real 3rd-Cir win — the category may endure even if the $22B price got ahead of the unsettled law.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Wikipedia/Britannica / Holland & Knight / Paul Weiss / Fortune / Sportico / NPR / MN Reformer / Yahoo):
- Kalshi **$22B**; sports **>90%** of activity. Exchange model; expansion into spreads/totals/props.
- **Third Circuit Apr 6, 2026**: prelim injunction FOR Kalshi vs NJ; contracts are **"swaps" under the CEA**;
  field + conflict preemption; CFTC exclusive jurisdiction; divided panel; conflicts with district courts →
  likely **SCOTUS**. **MA** prior injunction against Kalshi.
- **Minnesota** first **felony** ban (SF4760, May 18 2026, effective Aug 1 2026); **Kalshi** + **CFTC** each sued.
- DraftKings entered predictions via a **CME-routed CFTC exchange**.

### Correction (data conflation)
- The "**$1.3 billion**" figure was ambiguous — cited both as Kalshi's annualized sports revenue (FT/KuCoin, ~25%
  of DKNG sportsbook) AND as DraftKings' OWN Predictions volume (+24% MoM to $1.3B, May 2026). Article **dropped
  the precise $1.3B** and kept only the robust FT-sourced **proportion** ("approaches roughly a quarter of
  DraftKings' sportsbook revenue").

### Editorial care
- All legal items framed as **pending/unsettled** (preliminary injunctions, circuit split, likely SCOTUS, a ban
  not yet in effect) — explicitly NOT final verdicts. Bull case (info aggregation, favorable CFTC, 3rd-Cir win,
  "everything exchange" potential) given a full honest-nuance section. Election-betting concerns stated as
  scrutiny, not adjudicated harm.

No corrections to load-bearing facts beyond the $1.3B conflation fix.
