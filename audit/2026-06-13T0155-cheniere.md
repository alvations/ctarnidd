## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the market prices Cheniere as a serene,
contracted, utility-like compounder, but its record Q1 2026 margins (adj EBITDA $2.33B +25%, raised FY guide
~$7.25-7.75B) lean substantially on a wide global gas spread + margin optimization on uncontracted/merchant volumes
— the very spread that the largest LNG supply build in history (Qatar North Field, US Gulf Coast incl Venture
Global, Cheniere's own Corpus Christi/Sabine Pass expansions) is positioned to compress. Frames: contracted-floor
(durable 20-yr take-or-pay SPA fees) vs cyclical-spread-upside (priced as if it were the floor; deserves a far
lower multiple); commodity cycle / synchronized building at the top produces the glut; the $3.5B GAAP loss = $5.4B
non-cash derivative fair-value marks (mark-to-model; commodity-exposed accounts); capital-intensive multi-decade
FID bets; contract recontracting into an unknown 2040s market; infinite-demand TAM framing. Honest-nuance: premier
US LNG exporter, genuinely de-risked contracted core, superb execution (record cargoes, expansions ahead of
schedule, disciplined capital returns), real multi-decade structural demand (Europe off Russian gas, Asia
coal-switching, power/data-center demand), contracted floor survives a downturn.

### Editorial care
- The $3.5B GAAP loss explicitly framed as NON-CASH derivative marks / noise (not a bad quarter) — fair to the
  company; used to make the mark-to-model + commodity-exposure point, not to imply distress. Contracted floor
  repeatedly conceded as genuinely durable + deserving a premium; the critique targets the CYCLICAL merchant margin
  being valued as if it were the floor. Supply-wave (Qatar/US Gulf/Cheniere) is factual + widely documented; the
  spread-compression is framed as the historical base rate of commodity cycles, not a dated prediction. Demand
  growth conceded as real + multi-decade. No exact valuation multiple asserted (kept qualitative). No price target;
