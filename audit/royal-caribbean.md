# Audit — Royal Caribbean rides record bookings into a $22 billion debt headwind it can't out-sail

Article: `2026-06-20T1700-royal-caribbean.md` · Ticker: RCL · Sector: travel

## Method

Forensic thesis: Royal Caribbean's Q1 2026 (reported April 30, 2026) was a genuine beat — revenue +11.3% to $4.45B, adjusted EPS $3.60, adjusted EBITDA $1.70B at a 38.2% margin, ships above 100% occupancy, $1.1B returned to shareholders — yet in the same release management *cut* full-year adjusted EPS guidance to a $17.30 midpoint, citing a $0.74/share fuel headwind and softening Mediterranean bookings after late-quarter geopolitical shocks.

The original suggested angle (a "re-rated multiple") was PIVOTED: RCL is NOT trading at a stretched multiple (forward P/E ~15.7, trailing ~17, below its 3-year average), and net debt has come down materially. The defensible forensic thesis is therefore not "overvalued bubble" but: a high-quality, fairly-valued, still-heavily-levered ($22B gross debt, 2.2x debt-to-equity) discretionary-travel operator is priced for the *continuation* of a benign environment (calm geopolitics, contained fuel, resilient consumer, cresting deferred-demand wave) at the exact moment two of those four inputs have already turned against it. Frames used: cyclical-priced-as-secular (denominator illusion on peak-cycle E), commodity-price-taker (fuel), quality-of-earnings (buyback-supported EPS; adjusted vs GAAP wedge), pull-forward demand, capacity-deployed-into-the-unknown (Icon orderbook through 2030), priced-for-perfection asymmetry.
