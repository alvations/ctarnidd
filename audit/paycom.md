# Audit — Paycom (PAYC): growth deceleration after Beti cannibalization

## Method

Forensic thesis: Paycom Software is a founder-controlled HCM/payroll company whose flagship self-service payroll engine, Beti, was designed to let employees run and approve their own checks — and in doing so it automated away the implementation and service revenue Paycom used to bill. CEO/founder Chad Richison conceded on the Q3 2023 call that Beti was cannibalizing service revenue; the company missed and cut guidance. The thesis traces the resulting multi-year revenue-growth deceleration (30% in 2022 → ~6.5% guided for 2026) and argues that the stock's already de-rated low-teens forward multiple may be a value trap rather than a bargain, because a low multiple on a structurally decelerating business is the market pricing in lower terminal value, not a margin of safety.

Original suggested angle premise check: the angle described a "premium multiple." That is no longer accurate — PAYC has de-rated to a forward P/E reported around 12.6–15.5, at or below the software median. The article was PIVOTED to reflect this: the bear case is now value-trap/deceleration, not premium-multiple-froth. All other premise elements (Beti cannibalization, deceleration, founder control) verified true.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (primary: SEC Form 8-K / 10-Q, company IR, dated May 7, 2026):
- Q1 2026 revenue: $571.9M, up 7.8% YoY. CONFIRMED (8-K, StockTitan 10-Q).
- Recurring & other revenue: >95% of sales. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP net income: $155.7M; GAAP diluted EPS $3.04 (up from $2.48). CONFIRMED.
- Non-GAAP net income $161.3M; non-GAAP EPS $3.15 (beat consensus ~$565M rev / EPS). CONFIRMED. (Article references $3.15 as the beat figure — non-GAAP, correct.)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $275.4M; margin >48% (48.2%). CONFIRMED.
- Operating income: $210.2M (36.8% margin). CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: revenue $2.175B–$2.195B (~6.5% midpoint growth); adj. EBITDA $950M–$970M (~44% margin midpoint); reaffirmed after Q1 beat. CONFIRMED.
- Buyback: 8.38M shares for ~$1.07B in Q1; $17.7M dividends; new $2B authorization. CONFIRMED.
- Revenue history / growth rates: 2021 ~25%, 2022 ~30%, 2023 ~23%, 2024 ~11%, 2025 ~9% (FY2025 rev ~$2.05B). CONFIRMED (stockanalysis.com, macrotrends).
- Beti cannibalization admission: Q3 2023 call, Richison; Q3 2023 revenue $406.3M vs prior $410–412M projection; FY2023 guide cut from $1.715–1.717B to $1.679–1.684B. CONFIRMED (Morningstar, class-action notice, transcript).
- Co-CEO: Chris Thomas elevated to Co-CEO alongside Richison amid Beti deemphasis. CONFIRMED (Morningstar).
- Founder control: Richison founded Paycom 1998, CEO throughout, Chairman since Aug 2016, multi-million-share holder directly + indirect via Ernest Group and family trusts. CONFIRMED (IR governance page, gurufocus, Form 4 filings).
- Valuation: forward P/E reported ~12.6 (GuruFocus, Jun 7 2026) to ~15.5 across providers; software median ~18.6; EV/EBITDA ~15.4. CONFIRMED (figures softened to a "low-to-mid teens" range in article to reflect provider variance).

Corrections made: none required after draft; all figures matched primary sources. Valuation framed as a defensible range, not a single point, because providers disagree.
