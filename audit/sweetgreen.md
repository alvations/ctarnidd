# Audit — Sweetgreen posts a $125.8M "profit" while same-store sales crater 12.8%

Article: `2026-06-15T1230-sweetgreen.md`
Ticker: SG (NYSE) — restaurants / premium fast-casual

## Method

Forensic thesis: Sweetgreen's Q1 FY2026 GAAP net income of $125.8M is an accounting artifact, not an operating turnaround. It is driven almost entirely by a one-time $160.6M gain on the disposal of the Spyce robotics business. Stripping the gain, the underlying restaurant company lost money at every honest line — operating loss of $34.3M (margin -21.3%, worse than the prior year's -17.2%) and adjusted EBITDA of negative $8.1M (versus roughly break-even a year earlier). The article tests the "still-unprofitable growth story" using overlapping forensic frames:

- Quality-of-earnings / adjusted-vs-GAAP: the only positive number (net income) is non-recurring; every operating metric deteriorated YoY.
- Bought/one-time gain masks organic stall: selling Spyce — the seed of the Infinite Kitchen automation story — to print a profit.
- Cyclical-priced-as-secular: a 12.8% comp decline (11.2% traffic) framed by management as weather + tough comp, but full-year guidance still concedes negative comps all year.
- Demonstration-vs-deployment: capital-intensive Infinite Kitchen automation de-levers when throughput falls; payback stretches as traffic drops.
- Denominator illusion: restaurant-level margin (10.0%) excludes overhead, SBC and depreciation; the consolidated company still loses money.
- Priced-for-perfection / market-verdict: ~90% drawdown from the $53 IPO; consensus targets in high single digits.
- Consumer-discretionary exposure: a premium ($15+) salad is among the first discretionary lunch purchases cut.

Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes: pre-Spyce cash cushion (~$156.8M) plus disposal proceeds buy runway; Infinite Kitchen is a real differentiated asset; digital mix is strong and rising (67.2% total digital, 38.9% owned digital); weather and Ripple Fries comp headwinds are genuine and partly transient.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against the company's Q1 FY2026 release (8-K, period ended March 29, 2026; announced May 7, 2026), StockTitan summary of the release, and the earnings-call transcript:

- Total revenue: $161.5M, down 2.9% YoY (from ~$166.3M). CONFIRMED.
- Same-store / comparable sales: -12.8% (prior year -3.1%). CONFIRMED.
- Comp composition: -11.2% traffic, -2.3% mix, +0.7% price. CONFIRMED.
- Loss from operations: $(34.3)M, margin (21.3)% vs $(28.5)M / (17.2)% prior year. CONFIRMED.
- Net income: $125.8M, including a $160.6M gain on disposal of Spyce. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $(8.1)M vs +$285K prior year. CONFIRMED.
- Restaurant-level profit: $16.2M; restaurant-level margin 10.0% (down from 17.9% prior year). CONFIRMED.
- Restaurant base: ~285 restaurants; 4 net new openings in the quarter. CONFIRMED.
- Cash & equivalents: ~$156.8M. CONFIRMED.
- Digital: total digital 67.2% (from 59.9%); owned digital 38.9% (from 31.9%). CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: comp -4% to -2%; restaurant-level margin 14.2%-14.7%; adjusted EBITDA $1M-$6M; ~13 net new restaurants (about half Infinite Kitchen). CONFIRMED.
- Stock: IPO at $53.00 on Nov 19, 2021; trading ~$6 in early 2026; 52-week low $5.00; ~90% drawdown from IPO; market cap ~$700M; average analyst target high-single-digits, one Neutral ~$7.50. CONFIRMED (Investing.com / Macrotrends / WallStreetZen / MarketBeat).

Corrections made during drafting: none material. The suggested angle's "decelerating same-store sales" was strengthened to "sharply declining" (-12.8%), which the data supports. Article avoids asserting a precise current share price (uses "around $6" / "near all-time lows") and labels weather/comp explanations as management's attribution rather than adjudicated fact.
