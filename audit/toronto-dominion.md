# Audit — TD's Record Quarter Hides the Felony Asset Cap Strangling Its Only Growth Engine

## Method

Forensic thesis: TD's headline Q2 2026 beat (adjusted EPS $2.38, +21%) is being presented as a growth recovery but is structurally a run-off story. A meaningful slice of per-share growth comes from a shrinking share count (buyback) against a U.S. franchise that is *legally capped*, not growing. The OCC's indefinite asset cap (~US$434B), tied to TD's historic felony guilty plea for conspiracy to commit money laundering and ~US$3.09B in penalties, freezes the bank's primary long-term growth engine. The balance-sheet repositioning (selling up to $50B of securities, shrinking U.S. assets ~10%) is forced contraction marketed as optimization; AML remediation costs run through 2027 and are largely scrubbed from "adjusted" figures; and Canadian consumer credit is migrating higher (impaired provisions +9% to $465M) into a mortgage-renewal cliff. Frames used: quality-of-earnings / adjusted-vs-GAAP, denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular, moat-vs-loophole, demonstration-vs-deployment, priced-for-perfection asymmetry.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (TD Q2 2026, reported May 28, 2026; primary: td.mediaroom.com, TD IR PDF, SEC Form 6-K):
- Adjusted earnings $4.2B (+15% YoY); adjusted diluted EPS $2.38 (+21% YoY). CONFIRMED.
- Revenue $16.04B (vs ~$14.5B expected). CONFIRMED.
- Canadian P&C net income $1,925M, +15% YoY; revenue +5%; loans +6%. CONFIRMED.
- U.S. Banking adjusted net income $960M (US$702M), +8% YoY / +12% USD. CONFIRMED.
- Wealth Management & Insurance $837M, +18%. CONFIRMED.
- Dividend raised 4% to $1.12/share; ~19M shares repurchased under $7B buyback. CONFIRMED.
- CET1 ratio 14.3%, down 26bps QoQ; buyback consumed ~41bps. CONFIRMED (Q2 reported ratio; note a separate forward target of ~13% by Oct 31, 2027 also exists — not conflated in article).
- PCL ~$1,001M (~43bps); FY2026 guidance 40–50bps; impaired provisions +9% to $465M on consumer "credit migration"; gross impaired loan formations -5bps QoQ; gross impaired loans -4bps QoQ. CONFIRMED.
- Real-estate-secured lending volumes +5% YoY. CONFIRMED.

CONFIRMED (AML settlement, Oct 2024; primary: DOJ/OCC/FinCEN, American Banker, CBS, ICIJ):
- ~US$3.09B total penalties: ~$1.8B DOJ, ~$1.3B FinCEN, $450M OCC. CONFIRMED.
- First bank in U.S. history to plead guilty to conspiracy to commit money laundering. CONFIRMED.
- Indefinite OCC asset cap ~US$434B on U.S. retail subsidiaries; OCC may compel up to additional 7%/year reduction if remediation obligations unmet. CONFIRMED.
- Five-year probation, independent monitors, U.S. compliance office, AML remediation milestones through 2027. CONFIRMED.
- US assets to shrink ~10%; up to $50B of lower-yielding securities being sold/reinvested. CONFIRMED.
- ~$9B mortgage portfolio offload reported. CONFIRMED (Banking Dive).

VALUATION (labeled as range/contested in article):
- Trailing P/E ~18.2x cited vs N.A. bank-industry avg ~11.6x and peer avg ~15.8x (Simply Wall St); a separate ~12.5x figure (forward/alt basis) also cited. Presented as a range, not a single asserted number.

No invented figures. Allegations vs adjudicated facts: the felony plea and penalties are adjudicated (guilty plea entered); "profits over compliance" is American Banker's framing of the DOJ case, attributed as such.
