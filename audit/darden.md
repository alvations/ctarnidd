# Audit — Darden's 4.2% comp is 3.5% price: a pricing engine wearing a traffic suit

## Method

Forensic thesis: Darden's fiscal-2026 Q3 blended same-restaurant sales gain of 4.2% was read by the market as organic momentum, but a component teardown shows it is roughly 3.5 points check (menu price) plus ~0.1 point mix, leaving a fraction of a point of traffic. Olive Garden's flagship +3.2% comp rests on only ~100bps of traffic, which itself leaned on a ~130bps catering benefit (i.e., underlying dine-in traffic was negative), with offsetting drags from lighter-portion mix (~60bps) and Uber delivery fees (~50bps). Total-sales growth of 5.9% is further inflated by 31 net new units and the absorbed Chuy's/Ruth's Chris roll-up (bought-growth-masks-organic-stall + denominator illusion). Margins compressed at the flagship (Olive Garden segment margin -10bps; fine dining -50bps) on commodity inflation and menu investment — the opposite of the operating leverage a ~22x multiple implies. Frames deployed: pricing-led-comp, denominator illusion, bought-growth, cyclical-priced-as-secular, quality-of-earnings (53rd week ~$0.25 EPS; buyback arithmetic), priced-for-perfection asymmetry, moat-being-rationed (lighter portions).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against Darden 8-K / official press release (Q3 FY2026, quarter ended Feb 22, 2026, reported March 19, 2026):
- Total sales +5.9% to $3.345 billion — CONFIRMED.
- Blended same-restaurant sales +4.2% — CONFIRMED.
- Olive Garden +3.2%; LongHorn Steakhouse +7.2%; Fine Dining +2.1%; Other Business +3.9% — CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted diluted EPS from continuing operations $2.95, +5.4% YoY — CONFIRMED.
- 31 net new restaurants — CONFIRMED.
- Quarterly dividend $1.50/share (= $6.00 annualized) — CONFIRMED.
- $1 billion share repurchase authorization; ~0.7M shares repurchased for $127M in quarter — CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: total sales growth ~9.5%, same-restaurant sales ~4.5%, adjusted EPS $10.57–$10.67 incl. ~$0.25 from the 53rd week — CONFIRMED.
- Comp decomposition: check growth ~3.5%, positive mix ~10bps — CONFIRMED via earnings-call coverage.
- Olive Garden traffic ~+100bps; catering benefit ~+130bps; lighter-portion mix drag ~-60bps; Uber fees ~-50bps — CONFIRMED via earnings-call coverage (sourced from call transcript reporting; labeled as management-disclosed bridge, not independently audited).
- Olive Garden segment margin -10bps; fine-dining profit margin -50bps — CONFIRMED via earnings-call coverage.
- Valuation: ~$214 share price, ~$24B market cap, trailing P/E ~21.5–22.6, forward P/E ~18.6, EV/EBITDA ~16, dividend yield ~2.8–2.9% (as of ~June 19, 2026) — CONFIRMED via stock-data aggregators; presented as approximate ranges.

Corrections from the suggested angle: the original premise ("Olive Garden/LongHorn traffic roughly flat... soft organic comp") was PIVOTED — LongHorn comps are strongly positive (+7.2%) and Olive Garden's reported comp is +3.2%, so "flat comp" is false. The accurate, defensible thesis is that the comp is price-led (not traffic-led) and underlying dine-in traffic is near-flat-to-negative once catering is stripped. Margin/valuation portions of the angle hold.
