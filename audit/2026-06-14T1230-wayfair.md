# Audit — Wayfair (2026-06-14T1230-wayfair.md)

Title: "Wayfair's Best Quarter in Years Still Ended in a $105 Million Loss." Batch 11, article 9 of 20 (target
2,900+; actual ~2,900). Source: e-commerce. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-14T1230-wayfair/`.

## Method
Forensic thesis: W has genuinely improved (Q1 2026 revenue +7.4% to $2.9B, adj EBITDA $151M at a 5.2% margin, best
Q1 in 5 years) but still posted a $105M GAAP net loss; active customers grew just 1.4% (flat); it carries ~$3B
(long-term debt $2.93B) of debt; and its category sits on a frozen housing market + stretched consumer. Frames:
adjusted-EBITDA-vs-GAAP-loss gap (perennially unprofitable across its public life); flat-customers (growth is
spend-per-customer, not more buyers — flywheel stalled); debt makes the losses heavier (interest is part of the
GAAP-vs-adjusted gap); consumer-cliff (big-ticket discretionary + housing-linked = hit first); structural
thin-margin online-furniture model (shipping/returns/acquisition costs); Amazon competition. Honest-nuance:
category leader, real cost discipline, CastleGate logistics, adequate liquidity ($1.5B), big operating leverage to
a housing/consumer recovery.

## Fact-check (research + verification → all confirmed)
CONFIRMED (Wayfair Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q / TradingView / Quartr, reported Apr 30 2026):
- Net revenue **$2.9B (+7.4%, +$201M)**; US $2.6B (+7.5%), intl $319M (+6%) ✓
- **21.4M active customers (+1.4%)** ✓
- Gross profit **$880M (30.0%)**; **net loss $105M** (diluted loss/share $0.80); **adj EBITDA $151M (5.2% margin,
  best Q1 in 5 years)**; adj diluted EPS $0.26 ✓
- Cash $1.1B; total liquidity $1.5B; **long-term debt $2.93B (~$3B)** ✓
- Q2 guide: revenue mid-single-digit growth; gross margin 29.5-30.5%; adj EBITDA margin 6-7% ✓

### Editorial care
- The $105M GAAP net loss vs $151M adj EBITDA gap is the load-bearing, company-disclosed contrast. Conceded
  repeatedly that the cost discipline, category leadership, logistics, and recovery operating-leverage are genuine.
  Flat customers (+1.4%) is the company's own figure. Debt stated as "roughly $3 billion" (long-term debt $2.93B) —
  accurate. Consumer/housing cyclicality + structural online-furniture economics + Amazon are real, documented
  pressures, framed as structural risks not predictions. No exact P/E asserted (the company loses money on GAAP).
  No price target; no buy/sell. Not Apple-related (Amazon appears only as a related-ticker competitor).

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
