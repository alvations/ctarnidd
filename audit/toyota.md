# Audit — Toyota posts record revenue and a North American loss — the math that record obscures

Article: `2026-06-20T1100-toyota.md`
Ticker: TM (Toyota Motor Corporation, NYSE ADR)
Sector: autos

## Method

Forensic thesis (activist short-seller register, Bloomberg/CNBC feature clothing): Toyota's fiscal 2026 results (year ended March 31, 2026, reported May 8, 2026) lead with an all-time-record net revenue figure that conceals a structural margin collapse. The "record profit" headlines of the prior boom years were substantially manufactured by a historically weak yen (a translation tailwind) and a price-hike window, both of which have now reversed. The piece argues the market is pricing a tariff-and-currency-battered, BEV-light profit base as if it were durable.

Forensic frames deployed: denominator illusion (record revenue vs −21.5% operating income); cyclical/policy-flattered earnings priced as secular; quality-of-earnings (adjusted optics vs GAAP reality); demonstration-vs-deployment (BEV roadmap vs income statement); moat-vs-loophole / reputation moat erosion (Daihatsu/Hino/Toyota Industries certification tail); price-hike crutch; China price-war exposure; FX-tailwind-turned-headwind. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the hybrid vindication, fortress balance sheet, conservative-guidance history, and the exogenous/reversible nature of tariff + FX hits.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (FY2026, ended March 31, 2026; reported May 8, 2026):
- Net revenue: 50.684 trillion yen, up 5.5% YoY — record. CONFIRMED (Toyota USA newsroom / global.toyota summary).
- Operating income: 3.8 trillion yen, down ~21.5% from ~4.795 trillion yen prior. CONFIRMED.
- Net income attributable to Toyota: 3.848 trillion yen vs 4.765 trillion prior = −19.2% (≈ $25.7B vs $31.7B). CONFIRMED (Toyota Times Q&A; investing.com).
- North America operating loss: ≈ 298.6 billion yen ≈ $1.9 billion; first regional loss in 16 years (prior: 2008–09 GFC + recalls). Regional margin ≈ −1.4%. North America sales +8.5% to 2.934 million units. CONFIRMED (WardsAuto; Toyota Times).
- U.S. tariff impact on consolidated operating income: 1.38 trillion yen (1,380.0 billion yen). CONFIRMED (investing.com; tradingkey).
- Unfavorable FX reduced earnings by ~$13.5 billion. CONFIRMED (WardsAuto).
- FY2027 guidance: operating income 3.0 trillion yen, down ~766.2 billion yen (~20%); FX assumption 150 yen/USD; BEV sales projected to more than double to 598,000 units. CONFIRMED (global.toyota presentation; Yahoo Finance).
- TM ADR trading near $188 at time of FY2026 coverage. CONFIRMED (tradingkey headline).

Corrections made during drafting: none material. Net income stated as "roughly $25.7 billion" and "near 19%" — consistent with the confirmed 3.848T/4.765T yen figures. North America loss stated as "$1.9 billion" / first in 16 years — consistent with ¥298.6B at ~150/USD. All dollar conversions labeled "roughly/about."

Allegations vs adjudicated facts: Daihatsu certification (manipulated safety-test data, shipment halts), Toyota Industries engine-certification problems, and Hino emissions-data falsification in the U.S. (which drew a substantial settlement) are presented as the company's own disclosed/adjudicated governance episodes, not new accusations. No invented numbers.
