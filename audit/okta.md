# Audit — Okta's growth halves to 11% while the GAAP-to-adjusted gap swallows half its profit

## Method

Forensic thesis: Okta has completed the transition from hypergrowth identity vendor (50%+ revenue growth in its peak years) to a single-digit/low-double-digit grower, while parts of its valuation still price in a reacceleration its own guidance does not promise. The piece develops several forensic frames against verified facts:

- **Bought/long-dated denominator illusion** — management foregrounds total RPO past $4.084B while near-term current RPO grows only 12%, in lockstep with revenue.
- **Adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality-of-earnings** — GAAP net income $74M vs non-GAAP net income $168M; the ~$94M wedge is led by stock-based compensation, a real dilution cost excluded from the headline.
- **Moat-vs-loophole** — Microsoft Entra ID bundled into M365/Azure licenses pressures Okta's vendor-neutral identity proposition.
- **Demonstration-vs-deployment** — new-product catalog (Governance, Privileged Access, Device Access, FGA, ISPM, ITP) cited as growth driver but not yet visible as acceleration in current RPO; privileged access now collides with CyberArk-as-Palo-Alto.
- **Cyclical-priced-as-secular** — 2023 support-system breach left a reputational/trust discount that is structural, not cyclical.
- **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry** — ~$14B market cap, ~$100 median PT, multiple dispersion reflects GAAP-vs-adjusted disagreement; FY27 guidance of 9–10% points slower than the 11% just printed.
- **Seat-count ceiling / AI cross-current** — per-seat billing exposed to flat/declining enterprise headcount; machine-identity opportunity is a thesis, not yet a line item.

Honest-nuance section concedes: strong free cash flow, large/sticky backlog and switching costs, durable 26% non-GAAP margins, and credible new products in growing security categories.
