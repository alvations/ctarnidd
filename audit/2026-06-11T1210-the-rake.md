## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Coinbase is sold via the casino
metaphor ("the house always wins, you're buying the toll booth") but posted a $394M loss the quarter trading
volume fell — a real casino has a floor, Coinbase has none, because it is the rake, not the house. Three
sub-arguments: (1) the "record 44% subscription mix" is a denominator illusion — both lines fell, transaction
faster; (2) the largest "diversified" leg, stablecoin yield, is a disguised T-bill/interest-rate business
generous-to-Coinbase under active revenue-share attack (Hyperliquid took 90% of its deposits' reserve income);
(3) all three engines correlate to one underlying — the BTC price — so the "diversification" cuts almost none
of the variance that matters. Honest-nuance section credits 13 straight positive adj-EBITDA quarters, 8.6%
share ATH, regulatory tailwind, and a real (but venture-grade, not annuity-grade) payment-rails option.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title "The Rake" held)

Verified (two passes):
- Q1 2026 revenue **$1.41B** (−30.5% YoY); net loss **$394M** (−$1.49/sh). Transaction revenue **$755.8M**
  (−23% QoQ); spot trading −37%. Subscription & services **$584M** (−16% QoQ); **44% of net revenue** (record
  mix) / ~41% of total. Stablecoin revenue **$305M**; avg USDC held ATH **$19B**. Adj EBITDA **$303M**, 13th
  straight positive quarter. Trading share ATH **8.6%**.
- Circle/USDC: Coinbase captures ~**50%** of USDC economics (incl off-platform); USDC mkt cap ~$80B; ~1/5 of
  revenue. **Hyperliquid** deal → up to **90%** of reserve income on its USDC deposits; ~**$160M** potential
  shift from Coinbase/Circle.

### Correction from first draft
- Denominator-illusion section initially implied subscription revenue "held roughly flat" while transaction
  collapsed. Verification showed subscription fell **16% QoQ**; the mix rose because transaction fell **faster**
  (−23%). Reworded to state both lines fell — argument strengthened (the "record mix" reflects the trading
  collapse, not subscription growth).

Framing calls: the BTC-correlation, cycle-history (2021 boom / 2022 bust + layoffs / 2026 slowdown), and
take-rate-compression sections are analysis built on verified facts; no specific unverified numeric claim is
asserted in them (e.g., 2022 layoffs are referenced qualitatively, not with a fabricated figure).
