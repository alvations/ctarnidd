## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: humanoid robots are the purest
"someday" trade since quantum — Figure AI ~$39B with negligible revenue, Tesla converting its Model S/X line to
build an Optimus its maker admits isn't doing useful work yet — priced on an "infinite TAM" (all human labor),
with the demonstration-vs-deployment chasm, China's cheaper humanoids (EV/solar-style margin destruction), the
unit-economics of replacing a worker, the robotics graveyard (Boston Dynamics), and the same TSMC/chip
chokepoints. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `3d31b83` → published; title "The Hands" held)

Verified as drafted:
- **Figure AI ~$39B** post-money Series C (>$1B, Sept 16 2025); investors incl. Nvidia/Intel/Qualcomm.
- **Tesla Optimus**: 2026 target **50,000-100,000** units; **Fremont Model S/X line converting** to Optimus;
  **zero external customers**; Musk Q4'25 call (Jan 28 2026) — robot "not in usage in our factories in a
  material way" / no "useful work" yet; ~$30k targeted price (long-term <$20k).
- **Apptronik ~$5B** ($520M raise). Boston Dynamics (Google→SoftBank→Hyundai), 1X, Chinese makers — established.
- "Infinite TAM," China-commoditization (cf. EVs/solar), unit-economics, and chip-chokepoint sections = analysis.

CORRECTION at fact-check:
- Figure HAS early commercial deployments (Figure 02 to BMW Spartanburg, reportedly UPS) → softened "no
  commercial revenue / hasn't sold to outside customers" to "early pilot deployments; revenue a rounding error
  against $39B; not at scale." Keeps the forensic point (price >> commercial substance) while being accurate.
