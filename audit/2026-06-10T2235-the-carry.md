## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the yen carry trade — the largest,
oldest, least-measured leverage in global finance — is being structurally drained for the first time in 30
years as the BOJ tightens and JGB yields hit multi-decade highs, with the carry differential narrowing from
both ends as the Fed eases. August 2024 is presented as the documented dress rehearsal. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `ea74b4d` → published; title "The Carry" held)

Verified as drafted:
- **Size:** narrow $350–500B (Morgan Stanley ~$500B outstanding post-Aug-2024); Deutsche Bank ~**$20T**
  broad global measure (2024). Japanese institutions hold ~**$5T** overseas assets (repatriation risk).
- **BOJ:** policy rate **0.75%** (raised Dec 19, 2025; highest since 1995/30-yr high); held 0.75% Jan 2026
  (8-1) and April 2026 (6-3, dissenters wanted 1%); JGB purchases cut **¥5.7T (Aug 2024) → ¥2.9T (Q1 2026)**;
  inflation >2% for 4th year; next hike possibly June 2026; PM Takaichi tilts to easing (policy tension).
- **Yields:** 10y JGB ~**2.77%** (May 2026, ~30-yr high, up from ~1.1% early 2025); 20y **3.555%** (May 2026);
  risk of disorderly unwind if 20y → 4.00%.
- **Aug 2024 unwind:** trigger BOJ hike to **0.25%** + weak US jobs; Nikkei **−12.4%** (worst since 1987);
  BTC **$64k → $49k** in 48h; ~$270B crypto wiped; forced hedge-fund liquidation.
- USD/JPY ~**159** (May 2026).
- "Differential closes from both ends" framing reflects Japan hiking while Fed/ECB widely expected to cut.

Note: one older source put the 10y at ~1.95% (18-yr high) — superseded by the May-2026 2.77% reading used.
