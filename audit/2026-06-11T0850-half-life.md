## Method

News-driven: nuclear/uranium stocks rallying on AI power demand (Cameco, Oklo-Nvidia, SMRs). Original
market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the AI power bid revived nuclear, but the SMR
pure-plays (Oklo ~$13B at zero revenue, NuScale ~$5B) are priced on the AI clock (quarters) while reactors
arrive on the nuclear clock (decades); NuScale's flagship already died on cost; SPAC pedigree + dilution
treadmill + grid bottleneck compound the gap; the real cash flows land on uranium (Cameco, U at 14-yr high)
and the existing operators, not the moonshots. Distinct from "Kilowatt Kings" (power producers) — this is the
fuel + the reactor startups. Verified below.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check `803e977` → published; title "Half-Life" held)

Verified as drafted:
- **Oklo** mkt cap ~**$13B** ($12.9B), **PRE-REVENUE**: FY2025 zero revenue, op loss ~**$139M**, net loss
  ~**$106M**, ~$82M cash burn; 14 GW pipeline (Switch/Equinix/Meta); NRC design-criteria approval; Nvidia
  collab; went public via **SPAC** (AltC, Sam Altman chair until Apr 2025).
- **NuScale** (SMR) ~**$5B** ($5.3B); only NRC-certified design; ~$1B liquidity; revenue ~early 2030s; also
  SPAC-listed.
- **NuScale Carbon Free Power Project (UAMPS)** TERMINATED **Nov 2023**; cost **$4.2B (2018) → $6.1B (2020) →
  $9.3B (2023)**, scaled to 462 MW, insufficient subscription (added at fact-check).
- **Uranium** ~**$86/lb** (long-term peaked Dec 2025 at 14-yr high $86.50; end-Q1'26 long-term $90, highest
  since 2008). **Cameco** mkt cap ~**$47B**, earnings ~**+87%** YoY.
- Data-center power 400 TWh (2024) → 1,600+ TWh (2034). Two-clocks, dilution, SPAC, and grid-interconnection
