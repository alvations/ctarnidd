## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: the bond vigilantes were dormant, not
dead — the 30Y at a 19-year high, a positive term premium, weak auctions, the Moody's downgrade, and interest
now exceeding defense signal the bond market reasserting fiscal discipline via a self-reinforcing debt/interest
doom loop. Because the Treasury yield is the risk-free rate, this reprices every other asset in the series.
Ties to "Five Thousand" (gold/de-dollarization) and "The Carry" (Japan) via the changing buyer base. Verified.

  ~**4.7%**; $25B 30Y sold at 5% first time since 2007.

- FY2026 interest ~**$1.0T** (3.3% GDP, eclipsing 1991) > **defense $947B** > Medicare; **$88B/month**; ~**19%**
  of revenue (FY2025); CRFB: $880B → **$2.5T** by 2036, ~**30%** of revenue, "fiscal crisis."
- **Moody's** downgraded US to **Aa1 from Aaa** (May 2025); S&P 2011, Fitch 2023; deficit toward ~**9%** from
  6.4%.
- Weak 2/5/7/30Y auctions (higher yields, weak demand, dealer absorption).
- National debt **~$38 trillion** (>GDP) — sharpened at fact-check from "tens of trillions" to the verified ~$38T.
