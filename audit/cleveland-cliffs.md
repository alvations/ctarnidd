# Audit — Cleveland-Cliffs Beats Estimates and Still Loses $229 Million on a Steel Price It Can't Set

## Method

The forensic thesis: Cleveland-Cliffs (NYSE: CLF) is the largest North American flat-rolled steel producer, structurally a price-taker on hot-rolled coil, scrap, and tariff policy — three prices it does not control. Its Q1 2026 "beat" was a smaller-than-feared loss, not a profit. With ~$7.7 billion of total debt (swollen by the November 2024 Stelco acquisition), a sub-two-percent quarterly EBITDA margin, and a $229 million GAAP net loss, the company is a leveraged bet on a steel-price recovery whose timing nobody — including management — can guarantee. The piece works through the price-taker problem, the GAAP-vs-adjusted bridge (the $80M weather add-back), the tariff dependence, automotive concentration, and the cyclical-priced-as-turnaround risk, then concedes the genuine bull case (scale, real sequential improvement, $3.1B liquidity, explosive upside asymmetry).

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against the SEC Form 8-K (filed April 20, 2026) and the Cleveland-Cliffs first-quarter 2026 press release:

- Q1 2026 consolidated revenues: $4.9 billion (vs. $4.6B Q1'25, $4.3B Q4'25). CONFIRMED.
- GAAP net loss: $229 million, or $0.42 per diluted share. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted net loss per share: $0.40 (vs. consensus -$0.42; beat by $0.02). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $95 million, inclusive of an $80 million one-time energy cost impact from extreme cold weather. CONFIRMED.
- Steel shipments: 4.1 million net tons (up ~338,000 tons sequentially). CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 shipment guidance maintained: ~16.5–17.0 million net tons. CONFIRMED.
- Liquidity: $3.1 billion as of March 31, 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Report date: April 20, 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Stock reaction: shares fell >6% to ~$9.13 post-announcement. Reported by secondary sources (Kavout/Meyka); labeled as market reaction, not a company figure.
- FY2025 GAAP net loss: ~$1.4 billion, or $2.91 per diluted share. CONFIRMED via multiple secondary sources citing the FY2025 release.
- Total debt: ~$7.66–7.7 billion (Q4 2025). Sourced from financecharts/macrotrends aggregations; stated as "roughly $7.7 billion" in the article to reflect a defensible range.
- Stelco acquisition: completed November 1, 2024; enterprise value ~$2.5 billion; $1.8 billion senior notes issued to partly finance. CONFIRMED via Investing.com / company release.

No invented figures. Contested/secondary figures (total debt, stock close) softened to ranges or labeled as market reaction.
