# Audit — SAP's 27% Cloud Headline Hides 6% Reported Growth and a 13-Point Currency Crutch

Article: `2026-06-20T0930-sap.md` · Subject: SAP SE (NYSE: SAP, sponsored ADR) · Sector: software

## Method

Forensic thesis: SAP's investor narrative leans on its hottest constant-currency figures — cloud revenue +27%, Cloud ERP Suite +30%, current cloud backlog +25% cc — while the euros it actually banks tell a softer story: total revenue grew only 6% as reported in Q1 2026 (reported April 23, 2026). The seven-to-eight-point wedge between reported and constant-currency growth is a currency-translation crutch (weak dollar vs euro) that flatters every cloud line. Much of the cloud growth is migration-driven (RISE with SAP re-papering an existing ECC license base into the cloud bucket — a denominator illusion), the company's own CFO guides current cloud backlog to decelerate "over the coming quarters," and demand showed cyclical sensitivity to geopolitical disruption. The honest-nuance section concedes: contracted backlog is large and real, margins (30.0% non-IFRS) are genuine with an unusually clean ~4% IFRS-to-non-IFRS gap, the ERP moat is near-unbreachable, and the ADR's ~41% six-month decline has already priced out much of the prior "priced-for-perfection" risk.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (SAP Q1 2026 Quarterly Statement, reported April 23, 2026; SAP News Center; SEC Form 6-K; sapinsider.org):
- Report date: April 23, 2026.
- Total revenue: €9.555B, up 6% reported / 12% constant currency. CONFIRMED.
- Cloud revenue: €5.962B, up 19% reported / 27% constant currency. CONFIRMED.
- Cloud ERP Suite revenue: €5.214B, up 23% reported / 30% constant currency. CONFIRMED.
- Current cloud backlog: €21.9B (€21.932B), up 20% reported / 25% constant currency. CONFIRMED.
- IFRS operating profit: €2.741B, up 17%. CONFIRMED.
- Non-IFRS operating profit: €2.867B, up 17% reported / 24% constant currency. CONFIRMED. IFRS-to-non-IFRS gap ≈ €0.126B (~4% of operating profit). CONFIRMED.
- Non-IFRS operating margin: 30.0%, up 2.9 percentage points. CONFIRMED.
- CFO Dominik Asam: "slight deceleration in current cloud backlog growth over the coming quarters"; "quarter-specific cloud tailwinds are unlikely to re-occur"; reduced Q2 cloud-revenue growth; macro/Middle East "firefighting" cited. CONFIRMED (sapinsider.org, earnings call coverage).
- CEO Christian Klein: "large-scale adoption of enterprise AI is still in its early stages." CONFIRMED.
- ADR price: near 52-week low, low-to-mid $170s; down ~41% over trailing six months; analyst targets clustered well above $220 (avg ~$228.50). CONFIRMED (Investing.com, eToro, market quotes June 2026).
- Forward P/E: low-to-mid 20s. CONFIRMED (ChartMill, market data).

Corrections made during drafting: none material — figures matched across primary release and SEC filing on first pass. Soft/range-labeled items (exact ADR price, forward P/E) are stated as ranges, not point claims, because intraday quotes move.
