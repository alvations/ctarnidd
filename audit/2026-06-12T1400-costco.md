## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Costco is the best retailer in America
(Q3'26 net sales $69.15B +11.6%, 148.5M cardholders, record-high renewals) but trades at ~50x earnings (>2x the
S&P ~24x) — a tech multiple on a high-single/low-double-digit grower. The structural reveal: merchandise gross
margin ~11% and near-zero merchandise op profit after costs → the ENTIRE profit is membership fee income
(~75-80% margin), so Costco is a subscription business wearing a retailer's apron. The whole edifice rests on the
renewal rate, which is at record highs but faces a forward MIX-SHIFT risk (digital/promo members renew lower). At
50x = ~2% earnings yield (below T-bills) and no margin of safety; long-duration / rate-sensitive; thin merchandise
margin = little cushion vs tariffs/inflation; gas/FX flatter comps. Nifty-Fifty lesson: great company ≠ great
stock. Honest-nuance section credits the genuine subscription-annuity premium, flywheel, Kirkland, international
runway, and the decades of "too expensive" being wrong.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrected → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Costco 8-K/10-Q / StockTitan / Yahoo / CNBC / longyield):
- Net sales **$69.15B** (+11.6%); comps +9.8% (+6.6% ex gas/FX); **membership fee income $1.37B**; net income
  **$2.19B** / $4.93 EPS; merchandise gross margin **~11%**; **148.5M** cardholders.
- Renewal **89.7% worldwide / 92.2% US-Canada** (record). P/E ~50 (high-40s to mid-50s) vs S&P ~24x (search 01).

### Correction (accuracy)
- Draft asserted the renewal rate showed "the first faint signs of erosion" / had "ticked the wrong way."
  Verification shows it is at/near **record highs**. Reworded the section + the kicker to the accurate framing: a
  **forward mix-shift risk** (growing digital/promo cohort renews lower) masked by the record headline — NOT
  present erosion. Avoids overstating a bearish data point.

### Editorial care
- Repeatedly concedes Costco is one of the best companies in the world; thesis is "great business, demanding
  price" (price is the question, not the business). Membership-fee-is-the-profit-engine framed as the bull case
  AND the locus of risk. Nifty-Fifty analogy explicitly notes most of those were superb companies. Valuation
  figures hedged ("roughly 50 times," "high 40s to mid-50s").

No remaining errors.
