# Audit — PDD's profit falls 15% as Temu's tariff cushion vanishes and the home-front subsidy war drains margins

Article: `2026-06-21T1600-pinduoduo.md`
Ticker: PDD (PDD Holdings Inc., Nasdaq ADR) · Sector: e-commerce

## Method

Forensic thesis: PDD is a structurally cyclical, policy-dependent, two-front combatant being valued and discussed as a secular compounder. The Q1 2026 print (revenue +11%, the slowest in company history; net income −15% even as operating profit +22%) exposes a business spending more to stand still. The two growth engines are hitting structural — not cyclical — walls: (1) Temu's US model lost its de-minimis duty-free regime (ended for China/HK May 2, 2025; globally Aug 29, 2025), revealing the international "moat" was a regulatory loophole; (2) at home, a self-funded subsidy war with Alibaba and JD.com — where rivals enjoy easier access to state subsidies via self-operated models — is structurally tilted against PDD's pure third-party marketplace. Forensic frames used: slowest-growth-ever deceleration, operating-vs-net-income divergence (treasury-income cushion / denominator illusion), moat-vs-loophole, cyclical-priced-as-secular, quality-of-earnings (non-GAAP falling faster than GAAP), VIE/China-ADR structural risk, priced-for-perfection asymmetry.
