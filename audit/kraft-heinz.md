# Audit — Kraft Heinz pays a 6.6% dividend on shrinking volumes and a $9.3 billion confession

Article: `2026-06-20T1330-kraft-heinz.md` · Ticker: KHC · Sector: consumer-staples

## Method

Forensic thesis: the 3G/Berkshire zero-based-budgeting model that built Kraft Heinz is an extraction technology, not a demand-creation one. After a decade of brand under-investment, the price lever has finally jammed against consumer elasticity. Q1 2026 (reported May 6, 2026) shows reported net sales +0.8% masking organic net sales -0.4%, with volume/mix -1.2pts against pricing of only +0.8pts. Full-year 2026 guidance concedes the trend: organic sales -1.5% to -3.5%, constant-currency adjusted operating income -14% to -18%. The 2025 GAAP record carries a $9.3B impairment (>$6B goodwill), a ~$4.7B net loss, and a soft-asset-heavy balance sheet (~$22B goodwill). The ~6.6% dividend yield is read as a market warning label, not a coupon. Frames used: price-lever exhaustion / elasticity; bought-growth illusion (reverse); adjusted-vs-GAAP quality-of-earnings; denominator problem; cyclical-priced-as-secular; under-investment lag; commodity price-taker; patron exit (Berkshire); the paused breakup; priced-for-perfection asymmetry (inverted).

- Gross profit ≈ $2.22B; gross margin 36.7%, +230bps. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted operating income -11.8% YoY; adjusted EPS $0.58 (-6.5%); GAAP diluted EPS $0.67; net income ≈ $798–799M. CONFIRMED (article uses ~$798M, defensible).
- Q1 goodwill/intangible impairment ≈ $13M. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: organic net sales -1.5% to -3.5%; CC adjusted operating income -14% to -18%; adjusted EPS $1.98–$2.10; FCF conversion ~100%. CONFIRMED (outlook maintained).
- Dividend $0.40/qtr ($1.60 annualized); yield ~6.6%. CONFIRMED.
- FY2025: net sales ≈ $24.9B (-3.5%); organic -3.4%; operating loss ≈ $8.0B; non-cash impairment $9.3B (>$6B goodwill); net loss ≈ $4.7B. CONFIRMED.
