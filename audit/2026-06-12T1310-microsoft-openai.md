# Audit — Microsoft / OpenAI (2026-06-12T1310-microsoft-openai.md)

Title: "Microsoft Booked a $5.9 Billion Gain on a Company That Loses Billions." Method + fact-check; kept out of
the article. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-12T1310-microsoft-openai/`. Batch 8 (single-line CNBC/
Bloomberg headlines), article 1 of 10 (target 2,900+ words; actual ~2,901). Source: news.

## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: MSFT's AI story contains a circular loop
— it funds OpenAI, OpenAI commits $250B to Azure, that books as Azure revenue (the +39% bull number), and
OpenAI's rising paper valuation flows back as a $5.9B non-cash equity-method GAIN (a swing from a $3.1B loss a
year earlier) on a company losing tens of billions. So part of Azure "growth" is recirculated MSFT money, part of
MSFT "profit" is mark-to-model valuation gain treated as clean earnings, and $190B capex compressed gross margin
to a multi-year-low 67.6%. The loop runs both ways — reverses in an AI-valuation downturn. Honest-nuance:
most of MSFT is OpenAI-independent; the partnership was a masterstroke; the restructuring cut real risks; the 27%
stake could be a vast IPO windfall; Copilot demand is real-but-gradual; the partnership is loosening both ways.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title held)

CONFIRMED (Microsoft 8-K/10-Q/IR / CNBC / Futurum / Alphastreet / om.co):
- Q3 FY2026 revenue **$82.9B** (+18%); **Azure +39%** cc; AI business **$37B** run rate.
- **$5.9B net gains** from OpenAI (9mo to Mar 31 2026); ~**27%** stake (equity method); $13B commit / **$11.8B
  funded**; **$3.1B** income drag a year earlier (Oct 2025); PBC-restructuring **dilution gain** mechanism.
- OpenAI **$250B** Azure commitment; API rights through 2030; IP through 2032; Azure exclusivity removed.
- Capex **$190B** (+~61%); component/memory ~$25B impact.

### Note (not a correction)
- Article's **67.6%** gross margin = total-company figure, "narrowest since 2022" (windowsnews/coindcx). The
  verify surfaced **Microsoft Cloud** gross margin **66%** (a segment metric). Both reflect the same AI-
  depreciation compression; the sourced 67.6% total figure is retained.

### Editorial care
- Circular financing framed as "not fraud and not unusual" (cf. Nvidia cross-investments); the equity-method gain
  explicitly labeled non-cash/mark-to-model, "real on paper." Bull case given a full honest-nuance section
  (fortress franchise, masterstroke partnership, risk-reducing restructuring, IPO windfall potential). Copilot
  demand stated as real-but-gradual, not dismissed. No claim MSFT is imperiled.

No corrections to load-bearing facts.
