## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: daily-reset leveraged single-stock ETFs
mathematically bleed long-term holders via volatility decay (the rebalance forces buy-high/sell-low every day).
NVDL handed losses to late buyers of a fund on a stock that rose ~48% (and saw ~−$2.4B flows); MSTU fell >90%.
Regulators say hold ≤1 day, yet the products are branded by ticker (NVDL/TSLL/MSTU) to summon long-term
"conviction" — a structural mismatch the business depends on. The "house wins both doors" section (bull + bear
funds both decay, both charge ~1%) and the income-cousin (YieldMax NAV-erosion) section extend the wealth-
transfer frame. Category ~400 funds / ~8% of US ETFs / ~$37.5–40B assets, stalled = churning not growing.
Honest-nuance section: for a genuinely short-term directional trader the tool is legitimate and the funds do
exactly what they promise (2x the *daily* move).

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → published; title "The Decay" held)

CONFIRMED:
- Category: ~**400+** single-stock ETFs (403 listings / 17 issuers), ~**8%** of US-listed ETFs, ~**$37.5–40B**
  assets, stalled vs ~$36B nine months prior. First single-stock leveraged ETF: **2022**.
- AUM: TSLL ~$4.6B; **NVDL ~$3.7–4B**; NVDY ~$1.4B; MSTY/MUU ~$1.2B; MSTU ~$400M (high volume).
- **NVDL ~−$2.4B** net flows over a year (verify showed −$2.17B; article: "about $2.4 billion" — in range);
  **NVDA +~48%** / 12 mo; NVDL also ~+172% in an earlier clean-uptrend year (credited in the bulls section).
- **MSTU −>90%** / 12 mo (etf.com "assets stall," direct quote).
- Decay arithmetic ($100→$110→$100 ⇒ 2x ends $98.18, −1.82%) independently correct; matches SEC bulletin logic.
- SEC: hold ≤1 day; >1-day returns can diverge "quite substantially"; adviser-documentation duty (Investor
  Bulletin; Crenshaw 2022).

No corrections. NVDA +48% and MSTU −90% are single-window figures traced verbatim to the etf.com search-01
sources (not reconfirmed in the verify query but adequately sourced). The 172%-vs-loss tension is deliberately
