# Audit — Workday's Subscription Growth Has Slipped to 14% While the Machines It Sells Eat Its Own Seats

Article: `2026-06-22T0900-workday.md` · Ticker: WDAY · Sector: software

## Method

Forensic thesis: Workday is a maturing back-office SaaS franchise whose subscription growth has decelerated from its long-standing twenties cadence to the mid-teens (14.3% in Q1 FY27) with full-year guidance lower still (12–13%), priced on a re-acceleration the company's own guidance denies. The forensic moves:

1. **Denominator / law of large numbers** — growth slipped to 14.3% reported, guided to 12–13%; longer-dated backlog (10.9%) growing slower than 12-month backlog (15.5%).
2. **Adjusted-vs-GAAP / quality of earnings** — non-GAAP operating margin 31.8% vs GAAP 13.3%; non-GAAP EPS $2.66 vs GAAP EPS $0.87; stock-based compensation ~16% of revenue is the main bridge.
3. **Seat-model-vs-the-machine** — Workday bills per-seat/per-employee while selling AI agents designed to do the back-office work without humans (innovator's dilemma); consumption pricing as the assumed clean transition.
4. **Demonstration on its own payroll** — ~8.5% (1,750) cut plus ~2% (375) in Feb 2026, concentrated in Global Customer Operations, AI-justified.
5. **Founder restoration as a tell** — Eschenbach out, co-founder Bhusri back Feb 9, 2026, alongside cuts and deceleration.
6. **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry** — forward P/E ~11–12 on non-GAAP earnings vs trailing P/E ~high-40s on GAAP.
7. **Moat-vs-competition** — SAP, Oracle, Microsoft, AI-native challengers contesting the back-office layer.
8. Mandatory honest-nuance section conceding entrenchment, durability, GAAP profitability, real FCF, compressed multiple, and genuine AI opportunity.

## Fact-check

- Subscription revenue **$2.354B, +14.3% YoY** — confirmed in SEC 8-K (wday-04302026x991.htm) and company press release.
- Total revenue **$2.542B, +13.5% YoY** — confirmed (8-K, CNBC, StockTitan).
- GAAP operating income **$338M, 13.3% margin** (vs $39M / 1.8% prior-year quarter) — confirmed in SEC 8-K.
- Non-GAAP operating income **$809M, 31.8% margin** (vs $677M / 30.2% prior year) — confirmed (Yahoo/Zacks).
- GAAP net income **$222M, EPS $0.87** (vs $68M / $0.25) — confirmed (Yahoo, gurufocus).
- Non-GAAP EPS **$2.66** vs consensus ~$1.00; GAAP EPS $0.87 — confirmed (gurufocus).
- 12-month subscription backlog **$8.81B, +15.5%**; total subscription backlog **$27.29B, +10.9%** — confirmed.
- FY27 subscription guidance **$9.93–9.95B (12–13%)**; Q2 subscription guide ~$2.46B (~13%) — confirmed.
- FY27 adjusted operating margin guide raised to **~30.5%** (from ~30% in February) — confirmed.
- Stock-based compensation **~16% of revenue** in fiscal 2026 (FY26 revenue $9.552B) — confirmed via macrotrends.
- CEO transition: Carl Eschenbach stepped down as CEO and from board; co-founder **Aneel Bhusri returned as CEO, effective Feb 9, 2026** — confirmed (newsroom.workday.com, CNBC).
- Layoffs: ~8.5% (~1,750) reduction + further ~2% (~375) in Feb 2026, concentrated in Global Customer Operations, AI-framed — confirmed (KORE1, Metaintro, register).
- Forward P/E ~11–12 (mid-June 2026); trailing GAAP P/E high-40s — confirmed (gurufocus, stockanalysis).
