# Home Depot grows sales 4.8% but earnings fall, comps barely move, and the stock still prices a housing thaw

## Method

Forensic thesis: Home Depot's Q1 fiscal 2026 (reported May 19, 2026) is a "cyclical priced as secular" / "bought-growth-masks-organic-stall" case. The 4.8% sales increase was driven majority by the GMS acquisition (run through SRS Distribution), not organic demand; comparable sales were a near-flat +0.6% (U.S. +0.4%, with ~55bps of FX help). Critically, both GAAP and adjusted EPS FELL year over year while sales rose — a quality-of-earnings tell, with operating margin compressing ~1 point. The company is debt-funded into the Pro market (~$44.8B long-term debt, ~$2.3B FY net interest expense) and trades at a premium forward multiple (~22-24x) that requires a housing-turnover recovery management explicitly declined to promise (CEO: demand "relatively similar to fiscal 2025"). Frames used: bought-growth, quality-of-earnings, denominator illusion, cyclical-priced-as-secular, policy/rate dependence, demonstration-vs-deployment, plus mandatory honest-nuance section conceding the moat, the Pro logic, and the plausibility of the cycle thesis.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Home Depot IR press release, PRNewswire, SEC 8-K exhibit, CNBC, StockTitan, Yahoo Finance, TIKR):
- Q1 FY2026 reported May 19, 2026.
- Total sales: $41.8 billion, +$1.9 billion, +4.8% YoY. CONFIRMED.
- Comparable sales: +0.6%; U.S. comps +0.4%; FX impact ~+55 bps. CONFIRMED.
- Net earnings: $3.3 billion; GAAP diluted EPS $3.30 vs $3.45 prior year (DOWN). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted diluted EPS: $3.43 vs $3.56 prior year (DOWN). CONFIRMED.
- GMS acquisition contributed ~$1.3 billion net sales in the quarter; GMS completed September 2025; gross margin down ~75 bps primarily due to GMS. CONFIRMED.
- GMS deal: ~$5.5B enterprise value incl. net debt; ~$4.3B equity at $110/share via SRS Distribution. CONFIRMED.
- Long-term debt excl. current installments: ~$44.8 billion at end of Q1 FY2026. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 net interest expense guided ~$2.3 billion. CONFIRMED.
- Operating margin ~12% Q1 FY2026 vs ~13% prior year. CONFIRMED (TIKR/StockTitan).
- FY2026 guidance: total sales growth ~2.5%-4.5%, comps ~flat to 2.0%, ~15 new stores, gross margin ~33.1%, operating margin ~12.4%-12.6%, adjusted operating margin ~12.8%-13.0%, adjusted EPS growth ~flat to 4.0%. CONFIRMED.
- CEO Ted Decker quote: demand "relatively similar to fiscal 2025." CONFIRMED (IR release).

SOFTENED / RANGED (defensible):
- Forward P/E stated as "north of 22 times" / "roughly 22 to 24 times" — sources show forward P/E ~22.85, trailing ~24.17 (stockanalysis.com); ranged to stay defensible across providers/dates.
- Operating-margin compression stated as "roughly a full point" / "~12% from ~13%" rather than a precise bps figure.
