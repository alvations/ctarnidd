## Method

Original market-research piece, not investment advice. Forensic thesis: Roblox leads with record bookings ($1.73B,
+43%), revenue +39%, and FCF $596M — but the favored metrics flatter, while GAAP net loss WIDENED to $248M (from
$216M), and the company cut FY bookings-growth guidance to ~8-12% (vs +43% in Q1) explicitly for safety/age-check
measures. Frames: bookings-mask-losses (quality of earnings); FCF flattered by deferred-revenue timing + SBC
add-back (and the deferral tailwind fades as growth slows); SBC is a real dilution cost; the guidance cut downgrades
the growth rate the whole premium rests on; child-safety reckoning moved from footnotes into the forecast
(structural growth-vs-safety bind + intensifying regulation). Honest-nuance: real two-sided network + creator
economy (DevEx $423M, +50%), genuinely strong engagement (DAU +35% YoY to 132M, hours +43%), real cash, big
optionality (ads/older users/commerce/AI), safety investments are right + could become a long-term moat.

## Fact-check / verification (pre-fact-check commit → corrections applied → post commit)

CONFIRMED (Roblox Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q / shareholder letter, reported Apr 30 2026):
- **Bookings $1.73B (+43%)** (record) ✓; **revenue $1.4B (+39%)** ✓
- **Consolidated net loss $248M** (vs $216M Q1 2025 — WIDENED) ✓; EPS −$0.35 (beat −$0.39 est)
- **FCF $596M (+40% YoY)** ✓
- **DAU ~132M, +35% YoY** ✓; hours engaged 31B (+43%) ✓ — strong engagement
- DevEx (creator payouts) $423M (+50%) ✓
- FY2026 guide LOWERED: revenue $5.865–6.135B (20-25%); **bookings $7.33–7.6B (8-12% growth)** ✓
- **$57M legal-settlement expense** (youth consumer-protection / safety) ✓; age-check rollout restricting
  on-platform communication + slowing new-user acquisition ✓

### Corrections made (pre-publish → post-commit) — IMPORTANT for fairness
- Draft originally framed DAUs as having "fallen / wobbled" (132M down from 144M Q4) and used "slipping users" in
  dek/honest-nuance/kicker. Verification: **DAUs GREW 35% YoY to 132M** (and hours +43%); the 132M-vs-144M is a
  seasonal sequential dip off the holiday peak. The "users falling" framing was unfair and was REMOVED throughout
  (dek, the renamed "## The guidance that broke the spell" section, honest-nuance, kicker). Replaced with the
  accurate, company-confirmed point: engagement is strong; the real signal is the safety/age-check rollout SLOWING
  NEW-USER ACQUISITION and the bookings-growth guide cut to 8-12%. Added the $57M legal settlement as a concrete
  safety cost. Net: thesis preserved (and arguably strengthened — the deceleration is in the guidance, not the
  current users), accuracy restored.

### Editorial care
- Bookings/FCF credited as real; the critique is which metric the company leads with, not that the cash is fake.
  Deferred-revenue + SBC mechanics explained fairly (FCF "real cash" but flattered). Safety framed as both ethical
