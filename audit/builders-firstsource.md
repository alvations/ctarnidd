# Audit — Builders FirstSource swings to a loss as housing freezes — yet keeps buying its own stock

Article: `2026-06-16T0830-builders-firstsource.md`
Ticker: BLDR (NYSE) · Sector: building products / distribution

## Method

Forensic thesis: Builders FirstSource is an acquisition-built, deeply cyclical commodity-products distributor whose decade-long per-share earnings growth has been substantially manufactured by an enormous buyback program (48.1% of shares retired since 2021). In Q1 2026 the housing freeze stripped away the cosmetic overlay: net sales fell 10.1%, margins and EBITDA collapsed, the company swung to a GAAP loss, and leverage jumped from 2.0x to 3.2x in a year. Yet management kept buying stock — $302.9M, ~7x free cash flow — with borrowed money and authorized $500M more. The forensic frames deployed: denominator illusion (running in reverse), buyback funded far above free cash flow, leverage doubling, bought-growth-masks-organic-stall (confirmed by 8.3% core organic decline vs 1.5% acquisition contribution), commodity price-taker dressed as value-added platform, cyclical-priced-as-secular, GAAP-vs-adjusted quality-of-earnings, and customer (homebuilder) concentration. Mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the genuine housing-shortage tailwind, scale leadership, value-added strategy, and that historical buybacks at $80.90 avg were NOT value-destructive on price.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED figures (Q1 2026, reported ~April 30, 2026; quarter ended March 31, 2026):
- Net sales: $3.29 billion, down 10.1% YoY. Breakdown: 8.3% core organic decline + 3.3% commodity deflation, partly offset by 1.5% acquisitions. CONFIRMED.
- Gross profit: ~$0.9B, down 16.7%; gross margin 28.3%, down 220 bps. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EBITDA: $213.8 million, down 42.1%; adj EBITDA margin 6.5%, down 360 bps. CONFIRMED.
- GAAP net loss: $(47.4) million; diluted EPS $(0.43) vs $0.84 prior year. CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EPS: $0.27, missed $0.39 consensus by ~31%. CONFIRMED.
- Operating cash flow: $87.5M; free cash flow: $42.7M (down 5.1% from $45.0M). CONFIRMED.
- Q1 buyback: 3.3 million shares for $302.9 million (~7x FCF). CONFIRMED.
- New authorization: $500 million (incl. ~$200M remaining from prior program), late April 2026. CONFIRMED.
- Net debt: $4.6B; LTM adj EBITDA $1.4B; net debt/EBITDA 3.2x vs 2.0x prior year. CONFIRMED.
- FY2026 guidance: net sales $14.6–$15.6B (trimmed from $14.8–$15.8B); adj EBITDA $1.1–$1.5B. CONFIRMED.
- Cumulative buyback Aug 2021–Apr 2025: 99.3 million shares = 48.1% of shares outstanding, avg $80.90, total $8.0 billion. Shares outstanding ~110.5M as of Apr 30, 2025. CONFIRMED.

Corrections made: none required; all figures verified against primary 8-K / IR and corroborating secondary sources. Labeled adjusted vs GAAP explicitly throughout.
