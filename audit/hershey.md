# Hershey's 93% profit jump is a price hike wearing a compounder's costume

## Method

Forensic thesis: Hershey (HSY) reported Q1 2026 results on April 30, 2026 that look like a turnaround from the top (GAAP net income +93.6%, GAAP gross margin 39.4% vs 33.7%, EPS $2.13 vs $1.10) but, on inspection, are a single-lever price story on a shrinking unit base. The article applies several stacked forensic frames:

- **Quality-of-earnings / GAAP-vs-adjusted:** the flattering 570bps GAAP gross-margin expansion is distorted by commodity-derivative mark-to-market; Hershey's own preferred metric — adjusted gross margin — *fell* 80bps to 40.4%.
- **Price-vs-volume decomposition:** ~10 points of net price realization minus ~2 points of volume decline drove 7.9% organic growth. Growth is price-led, not unit-led; management itself cited "price elasticity impacts."
- **Cyclical-priced-as-secular / commodity-price-taker:** chocolate = cocoa, a violently cyclical soft commodity Hershey does not price. The 2026 recovery is a leveraged bet on the cocoa curve, tariffs, and consumer tolerance.
- **Denominator illusion / bought growth:** ~2 of 10.6 reported growth points came from the LesserEvil acquisition; in Salty Snacks the deal added ~20 points of segment growth yet segment income *fell* 18.1%.
- **Segment quality:** 2 of 3 segments grew sales but shrank operating income (Salty Snacks −18.1%, International −46.8%).
- **Priced-for-perfection asymmetry:** 30–35% adjusted-EPS growth is a rebound off a ~60%-collapse 2025 base — a cyclical bounce, not compounding.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED against StockTitan (8-K summary), the SEC 8-K exhibit (data/0000047111), PRNewswire/Hershey IR, FoodNavigator, and the Q1 2026 earnings recap searches:

- Net sales $3,104.2M, +10.6% — CONFIRMED
- Organic constant-currency net sales +7.9% — CONFIRMED
- Net price realization ~10 points; volume ~(2) points — CONFIRMED
- GAAP gross margin 39.4% vs prior-year 33.7% — CONFIRMED
- Adjusted gross margin 40.4%, down 80 bps YoY — CONFIRMED
- GAAP net income $435.1M, +93.6% — CONFIRMED
- GAAP diluted EPS $2.13 (vs $1.10 prior year) — CONFIRMED
- Adjusted EPS $2.35, +12.4% — CONFIRMED
- FY2026 guidance: net sales +4–5%, organic +2.5–3.5%, reported EPS growth +79–89%, adjusted EPS growth +30–35% — CONFIRMED
- ~400 bps full-year adjusted gross margin improvement target — CONFIRMED (management guidance)
- LesserEvil contributed ~2 points to consolidated growth; ~20 points to Salty Snacks segment growth — CONFIRMED
- North America Confectionery: sales $2,489.9M (+8.3%), segment income $792.4M (+13.8%) — CONFIRMED
- North America Salty Snacks: sales $350.1M (+26.0%), segment income $34.3M (−18.1%) — CONFIRMED
- International: sales $264.2M (+16.1%), segment income $15.3M (−46.8%) — CONFIRMED
- 2025 profit "plunged ~60%" base — sourced to FoodNavigator/industry reporting; framed as approximate ("on the order of 60%"), not asserted as a precise audited figure.
- Cocoa surplus 2025/2026 outlook — CONFIRMED as management/industry commentary, framed as a forecast (not certainty) in the article.

Corrections made during drafting: none required; thesis pivoted from the assigned "cocoa crushing gross margin" premise (false for Q1 2026 on a GAAP basis, where margin expanded) to the accurate "GAAP expansion is hedge-distorted; adjusted margin fell; growth is price-led on falling volume" frame.
