# Audit — DexCom (2026-06-14T1000-dexcom.md)

Title: "DexCom's Glucose-Monitor Growth Slowed to 12% as Abbott Gains and GLP-1 Cuts Both Ways." Batch 11, article
4 of 20 (target 2,900+; actual ~2,901). Source: medtech. See `../SOURCES.md` and `../sources/2026-06-14T1000-dexcom/`.

## Method
Forensic thesis: DXCM is an excellent CGM leader (Q1 2026 rev +15% to $1.19B, +12% organic, gross margin 63.5% from
57.5%, EPS $0.52 up from $0.27, raised margin guide) but priced as a hypergrowth compounder while growth has stepped
down to ~12% (FY guide 11-13%). Frames: deceleration (mature US insulin core, US +11% vs intl +26%); Abbott duopoly
pressing the lower-value type-2/basal segments DXCM most needs (price competition); GLP-1 two-edged (near-term
tailwind via Stelo/metabolic awareness vs long-term shrinkage of the high-value insulin-progression pool);
shift-to-lower-value-patients (units up, revenue/patient down); weaker reimbursement in mass segments; premium-
multiple confidence-fragility (prior sharp selloff history). Honest-nuance: genuine leader, big margin expansion +
operating leverage, strong product cycle (G7 15-day, Stelo OTC, Smart Basal), 26% intl growth, GLP-1 likely net
tailwind near-term, huge type-2/wellness TAM.

## Fact-check (research + verification → all confirmed)
CONFIRMED (DXCM Q1 2026 8-K / 10-Q / Motley Fool transcript / Insider Monkey, reported Apr 30 2026):
- Revenue **$1.19B (+15% reported, +12% organic)** ✓; US +11%, international +26% reported ✓
- **Gross margin 63.5% (up from 57.5% = +600bps)** ✓; GAAP operating income $255.3M (21.4%, +850bps) ✓
- **EPS $0.52** (from $0.27); net income $199.5M (+89%) ✓
- FY2026 guidance: **revenue $5.16-5.25B (11-13% growth)** — confirms the low-teens deceleration; raised operating +
  EBITDA margin targets (23-23.5% op margin; ~31% adj EBITDA) ✓; gross-margin guide NOT raised (freight/geopolitical) ✓
- Product cycle: G7 15-day across US channels; Stelo (OTC); Smart Basal; type-2 non-insulin RCT pending ✓
