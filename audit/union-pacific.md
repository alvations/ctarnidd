# Audit — Union Pacific Grew Revenue 3% While Carloads Fell — and Now Bets the Story on an $85 Billion Merger

## Method

Forensic thesis: Union Pacific's record-flavored Q1 2026 print is volume-hollow. Operating revenue rose 3% to $6.2 billion and the reported operating ratio improved to 60.5%, but total carloads fell 1% year over year and the entire top-line gain came from core pricing, fuel surcharge, and business mix — not from hauling more freight. The growth-segment (intermodal) carloads fell ~9% while cyclical bulk (+~12%) and industrial (+~4%) carried the quarter, meaning the secular-growth franchise shrank while the cyclical one masked it. The piece argues this is late-cycle, pricing-led growth (denominator illusion in the operating ratio; pricing power implicitly capped by the trucking floor) being valued at a secular multiple. Layered on top is the $85 billion Norfolk Southern transcontinental merger — shareholder-approved (>99% each) but only conditionally accepted by the STB (May 28, 2026), with supplemental information due July 27, 2026, a 12-month evidentiary clock, and expected close no earlier than mid-2027. A mandatory honest-nuance section concedes the genuine operating excellence and real pricing power.

## Fact-check

CONFIRMED (Union Pacific Q1 2026, reported April 23, 2026; 8-K / IR / TradingView / Investing.com / StockTitan):
- Operating revenue $6.2 billion, up 3%. CONFIRMED.
- Reported operating ratio 60.5% (improved 20 bps); adjusted operating ratio 59.9% (improved 80 bps). CONFIRMED.
- Net income $1.7 billion (up ~5% YoY). CONFIRMED.
- Reported diluted EPS $2.87; adjusted diluted EPS $2.93 (excludes $36M / $0.06 merger-related costs). CONFIRMED.
- Adjusted EPS growth vs prior-year $2.70: sources varied between "6%" and "9%" ($2.93/$2.70 = +8.5%). SOFTENED in article to "high-single-digit" to remain defensible.
- Total carloads down 1%; bulk up ~12%, industrial up ~4%, intermodal down ~9%. CONFIRMED via first research search (StockTitan/IR summary). Verify search did not re-surface the intermodal -9% breakdown line; left as "roughly 9%" and attributed to the franchise-mix detail.
- Freight revenue +4%; freight revenue ex-fuel-surcharge +3%. CONFIRMED.
- Operational records: best-ever terminal dwell, locomotive productivity; best Q1 for freight car velocity, train length, workforce productivity, fuel consumption rate. CONFIRMED.

CONFIRMED (Norfolk Southern merger):
- ~$85 billion cash-and-stock deal (incl. debt), announced July 29, 2025; first transcontinental US freight railroad. CONFIRMED.
- Shareholders of both approved (>99% each). CONFIRMED.
- Initial application filed Dec 19, 2025; rejected as incomplete Jan 16, 2026; amended application filed April 30, 2026; STB unanimously accepted revised application May 28, 2026 but put proceeding on hold pending supplemental info due July 27, 2026; 12-month evidentiary window; expected close mid-2027. CONFIRMED.
- Projected ~$3.5 billion annual shipper savings from truck-to-rail shift. CONFIRMED (company estimate; labeled as projection).
- Deal provisions: UP may consider walking if STB requires >$750M concessions; $2.5 billion breakup fee to NSC. CONFIRMED (reported deal terms; labeled as reported).
- Administration floated a US government stake; UP CEO publicly declined. CONFIRMED (Fortune, June 2026).

Allegations vs adjudicated: merger benefits and synergies are company projections, explicitly labeled as prospective and contingent on STB. No regulatory outcome is asserted. Deal-mechanics figures labeled as reported.
