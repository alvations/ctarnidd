# Sources — BNPL phantom debt (2026-06-12T1100-bnpl.md)

Title: "Buy Now, Pay Later Is Booming. So Is the Debt Nobody Can See." Batch 7, article 6 of 10. Source:
Engadget/CNET (consumer-fintech). See `2026-06-12-search-01-bnpl.md` and `2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus
`../../audit/2026-06-12T1100-bnpl.md`.

Thesis: BNPL (~$70B/yr, +20%/yr) is mostly NOT reported to credit bureaus = "phantom debt" the financial system
can't measure. Reassuring sub-2% delinquency stats are computed on incomplete data (a LendingTree survey found
41% of users missed a payment; ~63% carry multiple BNPL loans simultaneously). ~Two-thirds (some studies 78%) of
originations go to subprime borrowers stacking BNPL on credit cards already ~60-66% utilized; financing has
drifted to groceries/DoorDash. Funding fragility (originate-to-distribute) + regulatory limbo compound it. Honest
nuance: Affirm (profitable, working WITH FICO, ~2.8% delinq) ≠ the subprime pay-in-4 end; Klarna's credit losses
rising while it scrapes a ~$1M profit, stock −62% post-IPO. Product is fine; the *invisibility* is the risk.
