# Sources — Union Pacific (UNP)

## Primary facts (Union Pacific Q1 2026, reported April 23, 2026)

- Operating revenue: $6.2 billion, up 3% YoY.
- Reported operating ratio: 60.5% (improved 20 bps). Adjusted operating ratio: 59.9% (improved 80 bps).
- Net income: $1.7 billion (up ~5% YoY).
- Reported diluted EPS: $2.87. Adjusted diluted EPS: $2.93 (excludes $36M / $0.06 merger-related costs); vs $2.70 prior-year quarter (~+6% to +9% across sources).
- Total carloads: down 1% YoY. Bulk +~12%, industrial +~4%, intermodal -~9%.
- Freight revenue: +4%; ex-fuel-surcharge: +3%.
- Revenue drivers: core pricing gains, fuel surcharge revenue, business mix; partially offset by 1% lower volume and lower other revenue.
- Operational records: best-ever terminal dwell and locomotive productivity; best Q1 for freight car velocity, train length, workforce productivity, fuel consumption rate.

## Primary facts (Norfolk Southern merger)

- ~$85 billion cash-and-stock deal (incl. debt), announced July 29, 2025. First US transcontinental freight railroad.
- Shareholders of both UP and NS approved (>99% each).
- Timeline: initial application Dec 19, 2025 → rejected incomplete Jan 16, 2026 → amended application April 30, 2026 → STB unanimously accepted revised application May 28, 2026, proceeding held pending supplemental info due July 27, 2026 → 12-month evidentiary window → expected close mid-2027.
- Projected ~$3.5 billion annual shipper savings (company estimate).
- Reported deal terms: UP may consider walking if STB requires >$750M concessions; $2.5 billion breakup fee to NSC.
- Administration floated US government stake; UP CEO publicly declined ("We do not need anybody's help to do this").
