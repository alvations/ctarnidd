Title: "Boeing Says the Worst Is Over. Its Balance Sheet Says Wait." Batch 7, article 8 of 10. Source: news. See
`2026-06-12-search-01-ba.md` and `2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus `../../audit/2026-06-12T1150-boeing.md`.

Thesis: Boeing's operational recovery is real (Q1'26 revenue $22.2B +14%, 143 deliveries, MAX ramping 42→47→52,
FCF burn narrowing, S&P off CreditWatch, record $695B backlog) — but the balance sheet says the recovery is far
from complete: ~$47-50B debt vs NEGATIVE shareholders' equity (−$3.3B, owes more than it owns), FCF still −$1.45B
(guidance $1-3B positive is back-half-loaded), Moody's Baa3 (one notch above junk) w/ negative outlook, $8B 2026
maturities, KC-46A defense losses ($0.6B) showing the fixed-price disease persists, and the 777X ~7 years late
(~$15.9B charges, ~30 jets parked at Paine Field, certification slipping past 2026). The ramp is the recovery AND
its biggest risk (quality-vs-rate, post-MAX). Honest nuance: duopoly + $695B backlog = the franchise survives;
"survives eventually" ≠ "the worst is over now."
