## Primary facts (Q3 FY2026, reported March 19, 2026; quarter ended Feb 28, 2026)

- Revenue: $24.0 billion, +8.3% YoY (prior-year Q3 FY25 ~$22.2B).
- GAAP diluted EPS: $4.41 (prior year $3.76).
- Adjusted diluted EPS: $5.25 (prior year $4.51); consensus ~$4.11.
- GAAP operating income: $1.348 billion; GAAP op margin 5.6% / adjusted op margin 6.7%.
- Federal Express segment: revenue +10%; sixth consecutive quarter of adjusted-margin expansion.
- FedEx Freight: revenue ~$1.99B, -4.7% YoY; tonnage -4.8%; shipments -5.7%; adjusted op income -$127M; FY26 unit adj op income now guided down ~$400M (vs prior -$300M).
- FY2026 full-year guidance: revenue +6.0–6.5%; adjusted EPS $19.30–$20.10; GAAP EPS $16.05–$16.85; capex ≤$4.1B (~4.6% of revenue, record low; down from $4.5B prior plan).
- DRIVE: $4.0B structural cost reductions vs FY23; FY25 $2.2B target met; FY26 transformation target ">$1 billion."
- USPS domestic air contract (~$2B/yr at peak) expired Sept 29, 2024; UPS won replacement.
- Amazon: ~6.1B packages in 2024 (vs 1.7B in 2019); now largest U.S. parcel carrier; Amazon Shipping open to third parties.
- FedEx Freight spin-off (FDXF): completed June 1, 2026; 1 FDXF per 2 FDX, record date May 15; FedEx distributed 80.1% / retained 19.9%; ~$4.1B debt-funded cash paid to parent pre-separation; FDXF priced ~$152 (~$22.6B equity, ~$26.7B EV; ~3.1x FY26 revenue, ~24x adj op income); FDXF FY26 ~$8.7B revenue, ~$1.1B adj op income; retained stake ~$4.5B.
