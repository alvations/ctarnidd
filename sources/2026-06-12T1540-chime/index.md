Title: "Chime's Free Banking Runs on a Loophole, Not a Moat." Batch 8, article 7 of 10. Source: CNET/fintech.
See `2026-06-12-search-01-chime.md` and `2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus `../../audit/2026-06-12T1540-chime.md`.
(Timing: today = 1-yr anniversary of the June 12, 2025 IPO.)

Thesis: Chime is the largest US neobank — 10.2M members, first GAAP profit Q1'26 ($647M rev +25%, $53M net
income), beloved free banking for the underbanked. But ~76% of revenue is INTERCHANGE, and it collects an ABOVE-
market rate only because it routes through tiny partner banks (Bancorp, Stride) EXEMPT from the Durbin Amendment
debit-interchange caps the big banks must obey (~50¢ per $100). The "free" product is bait; interchange is the
catch; the catch is lucrative only via a regulatory LOOPHOLE — same arbitrage-not-a-moat pattern as Hims/Kalshi.
Merchants + periodic Congress (Credit Card Competition Act, Durbin expansion) want it closed → one law could cut
~3/4 of revenue. Diversification (MyPay earned-wage-access, $400M+) migrates into ANOTHER regulatory thicket (EWA
= disguised lending). Free product is easily copied (Cash App, banks coming downmarket). Priced (since the $27/
~$11.6B IPO) for a durable platform it hasn't yet become. Honest nuance: real product, real profit, real
distribution (top US account acquirer), diversifying, Durbin survived 15 yrs of challenges.
