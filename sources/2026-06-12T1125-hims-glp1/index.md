# Sources — Hims & Hers GLP-1 loophole (2026-06-12T1125-hims-glp1.md)

Title: "Hims & Hers Built a Billion-Dollar Business on a Loophole. The FDA Just Closed It." Batch 7, article 7 of
10. Source: Verge/CNET (consumer-health/tech). See `2026-06-12-search-01-hims.md` and
`2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus `../../audit/2026-06-12T1125-hims-glp1.md`.

Thesis: Hims built a high-margin GLP-1 business on the FDA-shortage compounding provision (legal copies of
Wegovy/Zepbound). The shortage ended → mass-compounding became illegal; the "personalization loophole" (adding
B12) failed; HHS referred Hims to the DOJ (Feb 6); Novo sued for patent infringement (Feb 9). Hims pivoted to
reselling BRANDED GLP-1s — low margin — and the numbers show it: Q1'26 revenue +4% to $608.1M, gross margin
73%→65%, net income $49.5M → −$92.1M loss, $33.5M restructuring. A regulatory ARBITRAGE mistaken for a moat; the
arbitrage was the weather. Honest nuance: genuinely diversified (most 2025 revenue non-GLP-1), 2.6M subscribers,
FY guide raised $2.8-3.0B, branded partnerships + peptide optionality. Platform survives; the high-margin engine
doesn't. Legal overhang labeled as referrals/suits, not verdicts.
