# Search 01 — Research (TSCO Q1 FY2026)

Date: 2026-06-15
Goal: Find Tractor Supply's most recent quarter and verify the thesis premise (decelerating comps, deflation in big-ticket, soft discretionary consumer, unit-growth + Neighbor's Club vs flat traffic).

## Queries run

- "Tractor Supply TSCO Q1 2026 earnings results comparable sales revenue guidance"
- "Tractor Supply Q1 2026 comparable store sales deflation big ticket Neighbor's Club net sales"

## Key findings

- Q1 FY2026 reported April 21, 2026.
- Net sales $3.59B, up 3.6%, driven primarily by new store openings.
- Comparable-store sales +0.5% (vs -0.9% prior year). Average ticket +1.6%; transactions -1.0%.
- Ticket increase driven primarily by ~150bps retail price inflation plus big-ticket category mix — so the comp is a price/mix story, NOT deflation. (Premise pivot: original angle assumed deflation in big-ticket; the quarter actually shows price INFLATION in ticket and big-ticket strength. Pivoted thesis to bought-growth/negative-traffic/cyclical-as-secular.)
- 40 namesake stores opened; one Petsense closed; new-store productivity 65-70%.
- Neighbor's Club: rising penetration and engagement among most valuable members, BUT new-customer acquisition softer and "most reliant on new stores" — supports the organic-stall thesis.
- Gross margin 36.2% flat; SG&A up 6.1%, deleverage ~70bps.
- FY2026 guidance reaffirmed; comps targeted 1-3% each remaining quarter.
