## Key findings
- Q1 2026 (period ended 2026-04-04, reported ~2026-04-28): net earnings $743M; diluted EPS $3.23; net sales $9.50B (+21% YoY from $7.83B). Beat guidance of $2.70–$2.80 and consensus $2.82.
- Year-ago Q1 2025 was weak: $156M net / $0.77 EPS → headline "+264%" is comp-flattered.
- Engine: steel mills segment ASP +14% YoY; record shipments of 7M tons (all-time high); gross profit +148%, operating income +247%, op margin 12% vs 4%.
- Tariff context: Section 232 doubled 25%→50% in June 2025 (UK 25%). Import share fell 22%→~15%. Nucor expects imports to stay at/below that level.
- Demand: Nucor guides 2026 demand up only "slightly" vs 2025; nonresidential construction customers cite *improving second-half* outlooks (hope deferred).
- Capital: Q1 capex $661M (~40% WV mill); FY capex ~$2.5B; ~$950M on WV Apple Grove mill, completion late 2026. $254M returned to shareholders. Liquidity $2.48B cash + undrawn $2.25B revolver (to Mar 2030).
- Cycle context: FY2023 EPS $18.00; FY2024 $8.46; FY2025 $7.52 ($7.71 adj), EBITDA ~$4.2B — 2025 was the trough; Q4 2025 actually missed ($1.73 vs $1.86; rev $7.69B vs $7.87B).
- Bull anchor: Nov 2022 Investor Day through-cycle EBITDA target of $6.7B, plus ~$500M EBITDA uplift from completed projects; "harvest phase" framing on the call.

## Conclusion
Suggested angle calibrated: ASPs were RISING, not falling, so the thesis becomes cyclical-priced-as-secular + moat-vs-loophole (tariff dependence) + denominator illusion (comp + multiple), not "falling ASPs."
