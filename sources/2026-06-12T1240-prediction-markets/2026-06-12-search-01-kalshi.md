KALSHI:
- Valuation **~$22B**. Sports-related contracts **>90%** of platform activity/fees.
- FT estimate: Kalshi annualized **sports revenue ~$1.3B**, approaching **~25% of DraftKings' sportsbook
  revenue** (NOTE: $1.3B also appears as DKNG's OWN predictions volume → article DROPS the precise figure, keeps
  the ~quarter proportion).
- Exchange model → payouts often **beat DraftKings** (margin compression signal). Expanded into **spreads,
  totals, player props** (high-margin sportsbook categories).
- Operates under **federal CFTC** oversight → offers sports event contracts even in states where sports betting
  is illegal (bypasses state licensing/taxes).

DISRUPTION / DKNG:
- DraftKings acquired a **CFTC-regulated exchange** (routed via **CME Group**) to enter prediction markets.
  Concern: if it doesn't work, DKNG burns capital.

LEGAL:
- **MA** (Jan 2026): Kalshi sports contracts subject to MA gaming law; preliminary injunction.
- **Third Circuit (Apr 6 2026)**: affirmed prelim injunction FOR Kalshi vs **New Jersey** — CEA likely preempts
  state gambling law; CFTC exclusive jurisdiction over DCM-listed event contracts. Conflicts w/ several district
  courts → likely **Supreme Court**.
- **Minnesota**: first state to make operating/advertising prediction markets a **felony** (SF4760, signed by
  Gov Walz May 18 2026, effective **Aug 1 2026**). **Kalshi sued** Minnesota (federal); **CFTC sued** within 24h.
- CFTC chair Selig wants regulatory clarity (favorable); CFTC **ANPR (Mar 2026)** on "gaming"/"sports
  competition" event contracts.
