Thesis: Spotify's profitability inflection is REAL (Q1'26 rev €4.53B, record 33% gross margin, €715M op income,
€3.2B LTM FCF, 293M subs) — but capped by a structural fact: ~70-72% of music revenue goes to the labels/
publishers who OWN the songs. Core music gross margin is mid-single-digits; the 33% blended margin is achieved by
DILUTING music with higher-margin non-music (ads/audiobooks). The labels (UMG/Sony/Warner) hold the leverage —
1-3yr licenses, can squeeze at every renewal, own the one input Spotify can't substitute. Margin gains are
BORROWED (price hikes + mix), not won from the structural royalty war Spotify has never durably won. Escape route
(audiobook bundling) already triggered a royalty fight (~€410M MLC liability / NMPA "undercutting songwriters").
Priced like a platform, structured like a distributor; competitors (Apple/Amazon/YouTube) can run music as a
loss-leader, Spotify can't. Honest nuance: scale (761M MAU) gives real bargaining power; pricing power proven;
non-music real; €3.2B FCF real — a genuinely better business, just structurally ceilinged.
