# Search 01 — Spotify Q1 2026 + royalty economics (read 2026-06-12)

Queries: "Spotify SPOT 2026 Q1 revenue gross margin operating profit subscribers royalty record labels"; "Spotify
2026 valuation label licensing leverage royalties price increase audiobooks UMG dependence".

Q1 2026:
- Revenue **€4.53B** (+8%, +14% cc). **Gross margin 33.0%** (Q1 record, +133bp; Premium GM 34.8%). Operating
  income **€715M** (**15.8% margin**, +40% YoY; beat €660M guide). **FCF €824M** (Q1 record, +54%); LTM FCF
  ~**€3.2B**. Premium rev +10%; ad rev −5%.
- **MAU 761M** (+12%); **Premium subs 293M** (+9%). (NA premium subs declined.)

ROYALTY ECONOMICS (the ceiling):
- ~**70-72%** of music revenue → rights holders (labels + publishers); core music gross margin **mid-single
  digits**. Blended 33% achieved by mixing in higher-margin non-music (ads/audiobooks/podcasts).
- Big 3 labels (**Universal/Sony/Warner**) control most music; licenses run **1-3 years**; rates "may change based
  on bargaining power." Margin gains from **price increases** (CFO: 2026 price increases > content-cost growth) +
  **mix shift** to non-music. 30%+ gross margin "achievable only by 2027-2028."
- **MLC / audiobook bundling** dispute: ~**€410M** potential liability if MLC prevails; NMPA accuses Spotify of
  "undercutting songwriters"; Spotify calls the suit "nonsensical."
- Valuation: re-rated rich on the profitability (analyst EBITDA-multiple framing); competitors Apple/Amazon/
  YouTube Music can run music as a loss-leader.
