# Instacart (Maplebear Inc., CART) — source index

## Primary facts (Q1 2026; reported May 6, 2026)

- GTV $10.29B (+13% YoY); orders 91.2M (+10%); AOV $113 (+3%)
- Total revenue $1.02B (+14%) — first quarter ever over $1B
- Transaction revenue $733M (+13%), 7.1% of GTV (flat / "maintained")
- Advertising and other revenue $286M (+16%, fastest since Q3 2023), 2.8% of GTV
- GAAP net income $144M (+36%); Adjusted EBITDA $300M (+23%)
- Operating cash flow $268M; free cash flow $253M
- Buyback authorization raised $1B to $3.5B total
- Q2 2026 guidance: GTV $10.1–$10.25B (+11–13%); ads +11–14%; adj. EBITDA $290–$300M (+11–15%); GTV to outpace orders growth; margin-expansion pace to moderate in 2026
- Stock fell ~8% on the print

## Thesis

Advertising ($286M, ~28% of revenue from ~2.8% of GTV, near-pure margin) is the profit engine that masks a thin, flat-take-rate (7.1%), price-taker delivery business. Every guided metric decelerates while the stock is priced for secular acceleration. Competition: DoorDash, Uber, Amazon all bidding for the same baskets and the same retail-media ad dollars.
