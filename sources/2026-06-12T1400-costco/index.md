Title: "Costco Sells Groceries at Cost and Trades Like a Tech Stock." Batch 8, article 3 of 10. Source: news.
See `2026-06-12-search-01-cost.md` and `2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus `../../audit/2026-06-12T1400-costco.md`.

Thesis: Costco is the best retailer in America (Q3'26 net sales $69.15B +11.6%, 148.5M cardholders, ~90% ww /
92-93% US-Can renewal) BUT trades at ~50x earnings (>2x the S&P ~24x) — a tech multiple on a high-single/low-
double-digit-growth retailer. The structural reveal: merchandise gross margin ~11%, and after warehouse/labor/
SG&A the merchandise op profit ≈ zero — the ENTIRE profit comes from membership fee income (~75-80% margin,
$1.37B/qtr). So it's a subscription business wearing a retailer's apron, and the whole thing rests on the renewal
rate, which is at record highs but faces a forward MIX-SHIFT risk (digital/promo members renew lower). At 50x =
~2% earnings yield (below T-bills), no margin of safety; long-duration / rate-sensitive; thin merchandise margin
= little cushion vs tariffs/inflation; gas/FX flatter comps. Nifty Fifty lesson: great company ≠ great stock.
Honest nuance: membership annuity genuinely deserves a premium; flywheel/Kirkland/international real; "too
expensive" has been wrong for decades.
