# Search 01 — research

Queries:
- "Dollar General Q1 2026 earnings results same-store sales operating margin"
- "Dollar General first quarter fiscal 2025 results net sales comparable sales guidance raised"

Findings:
- Q1 FY2026 (reported 2026-06-02): net sales +3.4% to $10.79B; SSS +2.0% (traffic +1.4%, ticket +0.5%); gross margin 31.6% (+65bps); operating profit $638.5M (+10.8%); operating margin 5.9% (~+40bps from shrink mitigation + improved damages); EPS $2.00; net income $444.1M.
- Guidance RAISED: EPS to $7.20–$7.45; net sales +3.7%–4.2%; SSS +2.2%–2.7%.
- KEY PIVOT: assignment premise (margins squeezed / weak SSS) is partly outdated — DG beat and raised. The profit beat is quality-of-earnings driven: gross-margin gain from markups + reduced shrink + lower damages, not from the customer. Demand thin (traffic 1.4%, ticket 0.5%).
- FY2024 context: net sales +5.0% to $40.6B; SSS +1.4%; gross margin 29.6% (-70bps); SG&A 25.4% (+140bps). Discretionary categories (home, seasonal, apparel) declined; consumables grew.
- Year-ago quarter SG&A 25.8% vs 24.6% (+121bps).
- Real-estate program: thousands of projects, hundreds of new stores, Project Elevate/Renovate remodels.
- Tariffs: a live margin risk for an import-heavy, low-price-point retailer.
