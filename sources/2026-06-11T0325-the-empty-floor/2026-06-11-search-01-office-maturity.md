# Search 01 — office distress + maturity wall (read 2026-06-11)

Query: "commercial real estate office debt maturity wall 2026 regional banks exposure delinquency office
vacancy extend and pretend CRE losses".

- Office loan delinquency record 12.34% (Jan 2026); office vacancy 18-22% major markets (multi-decade highs).
- ~$5T total CRE debt; >$100B securitized (CMBS) office loans maturing 2026, >50% unlikely to pay off at
  maturity (Morningstar DBRS, per this source) — reversal from pre-pandemic when >75% paid off.
- Regional banks absorb losses (CRE concentration structurally higher than money-center banks); leaned into
  CRE last cycle, now in the crosshairs.
- Extend-and-pretend: lenders (regional banks, CMBS servicers, life insurers) prefer extending/modifying to
  foreclosure; ~30% of modified CMBS loans got maturity-date extensions in 2023-24.
- Root cause: 4 straight years of occupancy losses; lenders view reduced office demand as PERMANENT (hybrid/
  remote), not cyclical. Maturity default (can't refinance), not missed monthly payments.
