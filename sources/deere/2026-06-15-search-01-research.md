# Search 01 — Research (2026-06-15)

Goal: establish DE's most recent quarterly results and the figures the thesis hinges on.

## Queries
- "Deere DE Q2 2025 fiscal results net income guidance revenue earnings report"
- "Deere fiscal 2026 full year net income guidance forecast FY26 second quarter outlook agriculture"
- "Deere FY26 Q2 net income 1.773 billion sales decline large agriculture guidance 2026"

## Findings
- **FY25 Q2** (ended Apr 27, 2025): net income $1.804B, EPS $6.64 (vs $2.370B / $8.53 prior year); revenue -16% to $12.763B. FY25 guidance $4.75B–$5.50B.
- **FY26 Q2** (ended ~late Apr 2026): net income **$1.773B**, EPS **$6.55**; net sales & revenues **+5% to $13.369B**; net income -2% YoY. Beat est. (~$5.70 EPS).
- **Segments (FY26 Q2):** Production & Precision Ag sales **-14% to $4.503B**, op margin **22.0%→15.7%**; Small Ag & Turf **+16%**, 20.6% margin; Construction & Forestry **+29%**, 14.8% margin.
- **FY26 guidance:** net income **$4.5B–$5.0B**; equipment-ops cash flow $4.5B–$5.5B. Large-ag industry volumes **-15% to -20%** (US/Canada); South America tractor/combine ~-15%; production ag -5% to -10%; construction growth target raised to ~20%; small ag ~+15%.
- **Tariff refund:** $272M (IEEPA) lifted equipment margins ~2.5pts to 16.9%.
- Management: FY2026 = bottom of large-ag cycle; recovery expected 2027; lean new/used inventory cited.

## Conclusion
Thesis confirmed with a refinement: the headline "resilience" (+5% rev, -2% NI) is a blend masking a 14% core-segment decline and a six-point margin collapse, with growth from lower-margin construction and small-ag. Cyclical-priced-as-secular + bought/mix-growth-masks-core-stall.
