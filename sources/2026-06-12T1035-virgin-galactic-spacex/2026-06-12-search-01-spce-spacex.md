VIRGIN GALACTIC (SPCE):
- Halted commercial operations **2024** to develop next-gen **Delta-class** spaceplane (6 seats vs Unity's 4);
  Delta still in development.
- 2025 revenue **$1.54M** (−78% from $7.04M); cash burn **~$460M/yr** with no flying vehicle.
- Cash **<$394M** vs long-term debt **$478M**; redeeming 9.80% First Lien Notes (due 2028) by **issuing shares**
  (dilution); shares dropped 15% then ~30.9% on dilution news; **going-concern** risk. SPAC'd 2019; reverse
  split; stock down ~99%+ from highs.

SPACEX (IPO ticker SPCX):
- **IPO June 12, 2026** at **$135/share**, 556.6M shares, **~$75B raised**, **$1.75T** post-money — **largest
  IPO ever** (prev record Aramco $35.4B, 2019). Musk = first paper trillionaire.
- Valuation path: **$400B (Jul 2025) → $800B (Dec 2025) → $1.75T (Jun 2026)** — >4x in <1 yr.
- 2025 revenue **$18.7B**; $1.75T ≈ **~90x revenue**.
- **Starlink**: $11.4B 2025 revenue (+~50%); **61% of total 2025**, rising to **~69% in Q1 2026** (58% in 2024).
- **Starship**: >**$3B** invested (2014-2023, Starbase+Starship); ~**$4M/day** (2024); repeated test failures;
  not yet operational at scale.
- Government: ~$22B in govt contracts; deeply integrated into US national security (but Starlink now majority).
- **Musk**: ~**85% voting power** with ~42% equity; institutional letter: removal "would require his own vote"
  → effectively unfireable. Attention split across Tesla/xAI/X/politics.
- Competition to Starlink: Amazon Kuiper; AST SpaceMobile (direct-to-cell). Bear takes: TheStreet "5 reasons
  IPO could be a dud"; Al Jazeera "$1.8T could be highly undesirable"; Investing.com "stratospheric risk."
