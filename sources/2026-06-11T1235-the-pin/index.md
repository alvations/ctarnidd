# Sources — "The Pin" (2026-06-11T1235-the-pin.md)

Topic: the consumer-AI-hardware graveyard (Humane AI Pin, Rabbit R1) and OpenAI/Jony Ive's $6.5B encore bet.
Inspiration: The Verge / Gizmodo (gadget reviews & post-mortems). Batch 6, article 8 of 10. See
`2026-06-11-search-01-ai-hardware.md` and `2026-06-11-search-02-verify.md`, plus
`../../audit/2026-06-11T1235-the-pin.md`.

Thesis: every AI gadget dies of the same iron law — it does a subset of what the smartphone already does, on a
device you buy/charge/carry separately. Humane raised $230M, shipped <10k pins, sold to HP for $116M; Rabbit
sold 100k boxes, ~95% abandoned (modified Android underneath). With both corpses warm, OpenAI paid $6.5B for
Jony Ive's io and reportedly committed Foxconn to 40–50M units — the most credible version of a bet with a 0%
success rate, at the largest scale ever, into a phone that has *already* absorbed the assistant for free.
