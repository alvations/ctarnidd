# Search 01 — AI hardware graveyard + OpenAI io (read 2026-06-11)

Queries: "Humane AI Pin failure HP acquisition Rabbit R1 2026 AI hardware flops"; "OpenAI Jony Ive io device
2026 AI hardware consumer gadget".

HUMANE AI PIN:
- Raised ~**$230M**; sold to **HP for ~$116M** (Feb 2025); HP discontinued the Pin ~immediately.
- Shipped **fewer than 10,000** units. Reviews panned: slow, unreliable, battery/heat issues. **Returns exceeded
  sales** by 2024. $699 + $24/mo. Now listed "(Deceased)."

RABBIT R1:
- ~**100,000** units (pre-orders); reportedly **~5,000 active users** 5 months post-launch → ~**95% abandonment**;
  later struggling to make payroll.
- OS found to be a **modified Android**; "Rabbit Launcher" installable on any Android phone → device ≈ an app
  with a plastic body.

OPENAI / JONY IVE:
- OpenAI acquired **io Products** (Ive co-founded) all-stock, ~**$6.5B** (announced May 2025).
- First consumer device confirmed for **H2 2026** (Lehane, Davos); screenless/AI-native, "peaceful and calm."
- **Foxconn** reportedly tooling for **40–50M units**; form factors codenamed "Sweetpea" (earbud), "Gumdrop"
  (pen). Framed as new category, not a phone replacement.
- Context: Sora reportedly losing ~$1M/day (cited among 2025–26 AI product failures).

Incumbent context: Apple (Siri/on-device models) and Google (Gemini on Pixel/Android) have absorbed the
assistant into the phone everyone owns.
