# Search 01 — research (private credit / retail)

Query: private credit retail investors 2026 Blackstone Apollo illiquidity mismatch semi-liquid funds gating
redemptions concerns.

## Findings used
- Structural mismatch: loans 3–7 years; investors can redeem quarterly.
- Q1 2026 non-traded BDC redemption requests ~9–10% of NAV vs 5% limits.
- Blackstone BCRED (~$82B) — record 7.9% / ~$3.8B requested; met 100%.
- Blue Owl — 40.7% redemption requests on tech-focused vehicles, 21.9% on credit income funds (most visible casualty).
- BlackRock restricted withdrawals on HPS Corporate Lending fund after ~9.3% requests; string of firms gating.
- Retail entering via semi-liquid/evergreen structures; may have underestimated redemption restrictions.
- Listed BDC sector avg 16.7% software exposure (22.0% weighted) — concentration risk.
- FSB report on private-credit vulnerabilities (6 May 2026); default fears growing.

## Forensic angle selected
Liquidity illusion / maturity mismatch + the gate-is-the-product. Illiquid long-dated loans wrapped in
quarterly-redemption funds sold to retail on a yield headline + gate fine print; manager-set NAV marks; AUM-fee
distribution incentive; coupled credit+liquidity risk. NOT a collapse call — gates worked, BCRED met 100%.
