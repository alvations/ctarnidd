# Sources — "The Decay" (2026-06-11T1300-the-decay.md)

Topic: leveraged single-stock ETFs (NVDL, TSLL, MSTU) and volatility decay as a retail wealth-transfer.
Inspiration: WallStreetBets (NVDL/MSTU/TSLL are WSB staples). Batch 6, article 9 of 10. See
`2026-06-11-search-01-leveraged-etf.md` and `2026-06-11-search-02-verify.md`, plus
`../../audit/2026-06-11T1300-the-decay.md`.

Thesis: daily-reset leveraged single-stock ETFs mathematically bleed long-term holders (buy-high/sell-low
rebalancing) — NVDL handed losses to late buyers of a fund on a stock that rose ~48%; MSTU fell >90%. Regulators
say hold ≤1 day, but the products are branded by ticker to summon long-term "conviction." The house wins both
doors (bull + bear funds both decay, both pay ~1% fees); the income cousins (YieldMax) run the same erosion as
"yield." Category: ~400 funds, ~8% of US ETFs, ~$37.5–40B assets — stalled, i.e. churning not growing.
