# Search 01 — leveraged single-stock ETFs + decay (read 2026-06-11)

Queries: "leveraged single stock ETF NVDL MSTU TSLL 2026 volatility decay AUM retail"; "single stock leveraged
ETF volatility drag long term 2x daily reset SEC".

CATEGORY:
- ~**400+** single-stock ETFs (one source: 403 listings / 17 issuers); ~**8%** of all US-listed ETFs.
- Collective assets ~**$37.5B**, up only slightly from ~$36B nine months earlier (assets stalled despite
  proliferation). First single-stock leveraged ETF launched **2022**.

INDIVIDUAL FUNDS:
- **TSLL** (Direxion 2x TSLA bull) largest: ~**$4.6B**. **NVDL** (GraniteShares 2x Long NVDA): ~**$3.7B**.
  NVDY ~$1.4B; MSTY / MUU ~$1.2B each. **MSTU** ~$400M AUM but huge volume (36.4M shares/mo avg).
- **NVDL net flows ~−$2.4B** over the last year even as **NVDA up ~48%** over 12 months → late buyers lost /
  fled. (NVDL also returned ~172% in an *earlier* clean-uptrend year.)
- **MSTU fell >90%** in the past 12 months — illustration of daily-reset risk (2x MSTR, itself a BTC proxy).

MECHANICS / DECAY:
- Daily reset → fund must buy after rises, sell after falls (buy-high/sell-low) to maintain 2x. Choppy/sideways
  markets bleed even if the stock is flat. Single stocks more volatile than indexes → more decay.
- Worked example: stock $100→$110 (+10%), 2x →$120 (+20%); stock →$100 (−9.09%), 2x →$98.18 (−18.18%). Stock
  flat; 2x down 1.82%.

REGULATORS:
- SEC: returns over >1 day can diverge "quite substantially"; meant to be held ≤1 day; advisers must document
  longer holds as in client's best interest. (SEC Investor Bulletin; Crenshaw 2022 single-stock statement.)
