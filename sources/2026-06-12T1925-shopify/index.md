Title: "Shopify Is a 100x Bet on Consumer Spending That Can't Afford a Recession." Batch 9, article 5 of 10.
Source: WSB. See `2026-06-12-search-01-shop.md` and `2026-06-12-search-02-verify.md`, plus
`../../audit/2026-06-12T1925-shopify.md`.

Thesis: Shopify is the "arms dealer of e-commerce" (sells the platform/payments, takes a cut; Q1'26 rev $3.17B
+34%, GMV $100.7B +35%, merchant solutions $2.42B +39%, FCF $476M/15%, payments penetration 60%→67%) — genuinely
excellent. But at ~100x earnings (sources 98-135x) / ~9x sales (3x Amazon's), the price embeds NO room for a
consumer stumble. Shopify's revenue is a DERIVATIVE OF A DERIVATIVE: a cut of merchants' sales = a cut of
CONSUMER discretionary spending, concentrated among the most FRAGILE merchants (SMB/DTC/importers — doubly
exposed to consumer cyclicality AND tariffs). The take-rate engine (merchant solutions > subscription growth) has
a mathematical CEILING (payments can't exceed 100% of GMV). Profit flattered by ~$449M SBC + offsetting buybacks.
AI shopping agents could disintermediate the storefront (long-horizon). Amazon trades at 1/3 the multiple while
competing/encroaching. Honest nuance: secular e-commerce tide, dominant own-your-brand platform, real ecosystem
moat, enterprise/offline/B2B/intl runway, real FCF, out-executes rivals — a great company, priced for no
recessions.
