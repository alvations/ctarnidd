# Search 01 — research (DoorDash)

Queries: DoorDash Q1 2026 earnings; food delivery margins; Deliveroo/Wolt acquisition; gig worker
classification minimum pay laws.

## Findings used
- Q1 2026: revenue $4.04B (+33%), orders 933M (+27%), Marketplace GOV $31.6B (record).
- Adj EBITDA $754M (+28% from $590M). Net income $184M ($0.42), down from $193M ($0.44). FCF $420M.
- Q2 GOV guide $32.4–33.4B. Deliveroo acquisition closed; Wolt integration; heavy intl/AI/platform spend;
  duopoly competition with Uber Eats.
- Gig-labor: Dashers classified as independent contractors; NYC + Seattle delivery min-pay laws passed;
  broader reclassification push = recurring legislative/court risk on the largest cost line.
- Adjacent higher-margin verticals: grocery, retail, advertising (the ad business = key bull upside).

## Forensic angle selected
Thin-take / quality-of-earnings: moves $31.6B of food, keeps ~0.6% as net income, and that net income +
FCF FELL despite +33% revenue. Adj EBITDA flatters a structurally thin logistics business; growth partly
bought via Deliveroo/Wolt; gig-labor regulatory risk; duopoly permanent spend; autonomous-delivery wildcard.
