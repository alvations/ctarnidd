# Search 01 — Research (Hershey Q1 2026)

Queries:
- "Hershey HSY Q1 2026 earnings results cocoa prices gross margin guidance"
- "Hershey Q1 2026 net sales organic volume decline cocoa cost margin"
- "Hershey Q1 2026 adjusted EPS LesserEvil acquisition price realization volume elasticity 2026 guidance adjusted gross margin 400 basis points"

Findings:
- Q1 2026 reported April 30, 2026. Net sales $3,104.2M, +10.6%; organic +7.9%.
- Growth driven by ~10 points net price realization, partly offset by ~2-point volume decline ("price elasticity impacts in North America Confectionery and International").
- GAAP gross margin 39.4% vs 33.7% prior year — but adjusted gross margin 40.4%, DOWN 80 bps YoY.
- GAAP net income $435.1M (+93.6%); diluted EPS $2.13 (vs $1.10); adjusted EPS $2.35 (+12.4%).
- FY2026 guidance reaffirmed: net sales +4–5%; adjusted EPS growth +30–35%; ~400 bps gross margin improvement; recovery weighted to H2.
- LesserEvil acquisition contributed ~2 points consolidated; ~20 points to Salty Snacks segment growth.
- Segments: NA Confectionery sales $2,489.9M (+8.3%), income $792.4M (+13.8%); NA Salty Snacks sales $350.1M (+26.0%), income $34.3M (−18.1%); International sales $264.2M (+16.1%), income $15.3M (−46.8%).
- Cocoa: management/industry expect larger surplus 2025/2026 on supply diversification, stronger crops, demand destruction, new origins.

Pivot decision: assigned premise ("cocoa crushing gross margin") is false on a GAAP basis for Q1 2026 (margin expanded). Pivoted to accurate thesis: GAAP expansion is hedge-distorted, adjusted margin fell, growth is price-led on declining volume, two of three segments shrank income, and the recovery is a cocoa/elasticity bet priced as a secular compounder.
