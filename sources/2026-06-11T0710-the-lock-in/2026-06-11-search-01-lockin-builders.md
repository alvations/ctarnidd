# Search 01 — lock-in + builders (read 2026-06-11)

Query: "US housing market 2026 mortgage rate lock-in effect frozen resale existing home sales low; homebuilders
Lennar DHorton stock valuation margins incentives buydowns affordability".

- ~80% of outstanding US mortgages ≤6% (many ~3%) → lock-in. $350K 2021 @3% ≈ $1,475/mo P&I; comparable home
  @6.75% ≈ $2,790. Sustained sub-6% needed to unlock demand.
- "Frozen, not broken": resale inventory suppressed; market undersupplied despite weak volume; existing-home
  sales ~4.02M pace, 1.47M unsold (4.4 mo supply). Fannie Mae rates 6.3% 2026, 6.2% 2027.
- Affordability worst since the 1980s (NAR index lows).
- Builders use rate buydowns (subsidize buyer rate via forward commitments from sales proceeds) + price cuts:
  32% cut prices in May, 61% offered incentives; NAHB 64% incentives / 37% price cuts.
- Lennar: net margin ~10.5%; operating margin 5.1% (down from 9.1% YoY) on incentives/costs. LEN −47% / DHI
  −27% from 52-wk highs. DHI ROE 13.8% vs LEN 8.0%.
